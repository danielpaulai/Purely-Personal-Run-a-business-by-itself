# Rewrite Standards
# Tailor Reference — Purely Personal Bootcamp

This file contains skill-specific rewrite instructions for each of the 5 starter skills.
Read the relevant section before rewriting each skill.
Every section covers: what must change, what must stay, and what common mistakes to avoid.

---

## UNIVERSAL REWRITE RULES (apply to all 5 skills)

Before touching any individual skill, these apply everywhere:

**Replace every generic placeholder:**
- "your ICP" → [ICP ROLE] at [COMPANY STAGE] who [SITUATION]
- "your offer" → [OFFER NAME]
- "your audience" → [specific description of who reads/receives this]
- "your competitors" → [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], [COMPETITOR 3] by name
- "your niche" → [INDUSTRY — precise, not "marketing" or "business"]
- "Daniel Paul" → [PARTICIPANT NAME] everywhere it appears as the voice default

**Inject specificity at every section:**
The test: if you removed the participant's name from the skill, would it still be clearly theirs?
If yes — it's not specific enough. Add more concrete context.

**Never break the workflow logic:**
Steps exist in a specific order for a reason. Add context into steps. Never reorder them.
Never combine steps. Never remove a step because it seems redundant.

**Never invent proof points:**
If the Tailor Briefing has no proof points listed, write "[ADD PROOF POINT: result + timeframe + client type]"
as a placeholder. Never fabricate numbers or results.

## SKILL 1 — CONTENT STRATEGY REWRITE

### What changes

**STEP 1 — INTAKE:**
Remove questions 1 (who are you) and 2 (what do you sell) — already known.
Keep questions 3-7 but reframe them around the participant's specific context.
Question 4 (unique angle) becomes a prompt to surface new angles beyond the known one.
Question 6 (competitors) should name the known competitors and ask: "Any others to add?"

**STEP 2 — DEEP RESEARCH:**
Search queries must name the specific niche:
- Generic: `"[niche] LinkedIn creators"`
- Fitted: `"[INDUSTRY + ICP ROLE] LinkedIn content [year]"` e.g. `"B2B SaaS founder LinkedIn content 2025"`

Add a 5th search specific to this participant:
`"[PARTICIPANT NAME OR BRAND] competitors positioning [year]"` — to see how the competitive
landscape describes itself so the pillars explicitly contrast with that language.

**STEP 3 — POSITIONING STATEMENT:**
The fitted positioning statement must:
- Name the specific ICP role and company stage (not "professionals" or "founders")
- Reference the participant's UNIQUE ANGLE from the Tailor Briefing
- Explicitly state what the top 2 competitors are known for — then position against it

**STEP 5 — CONTENT PILLARS:**
Generic pillars are the most common failure. Each pillar must:
- Be unownable by any competitor (name a competitor and ask: could they use this pillar? If yes — rewrite)
- Connect to a specific audience pain from the ICP section of the Tailor Briefing
- Have post ideas that reference the participant's real proof points

The pillar validation tests stay unchanged. Apply them strictly.

**STEP 6 — COMPETITOR CONTENT STUDY:**
Replace generic "[competitor]" slots with the actual top 3-5 from the Tailor Briefing.
Each competitor's gap section must name specific topics they avoid or handle poorly
— not just "they miss emotional content" but "they never post about [specific situation
the ICP faces] — that's the gap."

### What stays unchanged
- The 4-search research protocol (update queries, keep the protocol)
- The pillar format (all 6 fields per pillar)
- The 5 pillar validation tests
- The 90-day structure (Month 1/2/3)
- The Quick Wins section structure
- The delivery format
- The quality gate (add participant-specific checks at the end)

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Pillar names that could belong to any LinkedIn coach ("Authenticity", "Value", "Consistency")
- Post ideas that don't reference the participant's specific proof or methodology
- Competitor study that describes them generically instead of naming their actual positioning

## SKILL 2 — LINKEDIN CAPTION WRITER REWRITE

### What changes

**VOICE LOCK (replaces Voice DNA Check):**
This is the most important section to rewrite.
Extract all 8 voice dimensions from the participant's Voice DNA and embed them directly.
Not a reference — embedded verbatim so the skill cannot drift.

Minimum required in the Voice Lock:
- Top 5 voice rules (specific, not "write conversationally")
- Banned words list (minimum 10)
- Sentence rhythm description
- CTA format example
- Hook archetype preference (which of the 5 they use most)

**STEP 2 — INTAKE:**
Pre-fill: participant name, ICP, offer.
Only ask for: the specific idea/topic/result for this post + any new proof point.

**STEP 3 — RESEARCH DECISION:**
Add the specific niche to research triggers:
"Research if the post makes a claim about what's working in [INDUSTRY] right now."
Add the participant's top competitors as signals for what NOT to echo:
"Before choosing a framework — check: does this post angle sound like [COMPETITOR 1] or
[COMPETITOR 2]? If yes — shift the angle."

**STEP 5 — HOOK SELECTION:**
Add the participant's preferred hook archetypes from their Voice DNA.
If they favour Contrarian Reframe hooks over Detection — note this as the default.

**STEP 6 — WRITE BODY:**
Add the Specificity standard: "Every post must contain at least one detail that could
only come from [PARTICIPANT] — a specific result, a specific client, a specific observation
from their [INDUSTRY] experience."

**STEP 8 — INVISIBILITY DIAGNOSTIC:**
Add one participant-specific check:
"Voice: sounds like [PARTICIPANT] — not like [COMPETITOR 1] or [COMPETITOR 2]. If yes → pass."

**STEP 9 — DE-AI-IFY:**
Add the participant's specific banned words to the standard list.

### What stays unchanged
- The 9-step workflow structure
- The framework selection table
- The Hook Quality Test
- The P.S. rules
- The delivery format
- The quality gate structure

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Keeping "write in Daniel Paul's voice" anywhere in the skill
- Leaving the intake form asking for information the Tailor Briefing already provided
- Voice Lock rules that are too generic ("be specific", "be direct") instead of precise
  ("Never start a post with 'I'. Rotate: 'Here's the thing.' / 'Most [X]...' / 'The real problem...'")

## SKILL 3 — DM SEQUENCE WRITER REWRITE

### What changes

**VOICE LOCK:**
Same as Caption Writer — embed Voice DNA directly.
Add: "DMs are shorter and more direct than posts. Voice rules apply — energy level drops
to [N-1] from [PARTICIPANT]'s standard post level. More conversational. Less punchy."

**STEP 2 — INTAKE:**
Pre-fill: participant name, offer, ICP.
The "OFFER IN ONE SENTENCE" field must be pre-filled:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s offer: [OFFER NAME] — [one-sentence transformation]."

Only ask per session: the prospect details (name, role, signals) and the temperature.

**STEP 3 — PROSPECT RESEARCH:**
Communication style table stays unchanged.
Add: "[PARTICIPANT]'s ICP defaults — [ICP ROLE] at [COMPANY STAGE] typically
skew toward [DRIVER/ANALYTICAL/etc. based on known ICP profile]. If unknown,
default to [their default style] and adjust after the opener gets a response."

**STEP 4 — PROFILE AUDIT:**
Add: "Also check: does [PARTICIPANT]'s profile currently show [top 2 proof points]?
If not, flag as a profile gap before running the sequence."

**MESSAGE 1 — CONNECTION REQUEST:**
Add the participant's typical human hooks:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s strongest connection hooks come from: [top 2-3 signal types — e.g.
'a post they wrote about [niche topic]' or 'a shared experience in [industry]']."

**MESSAGE 3 — VALUE BRIDGE:**
Add the participant's specific value delivers:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s strongest value bridges: [2-3 specific insights or frameworks they
use in conversations]. Use these as the basis for value delivery — never generic advice."

**MESSAGE 4 — SOFT ASK:**
Pre-fill with participant's standard CTA:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s preferred soft ask: [their CTA format]. Adapt the specifics per prospect —
keep the format."

**CARDINAL RULE — The test must name the participant:**
"If you could send this message as [PARTICIPANT] to someone else entirely — it fails.
Every message must pass: 'This could only come from [PARTICIPANT], to this specific prospect.'"

### What stays unchanged
- The 5-message sequence structure
- Character/word limits per message
- Communication style calibration table
- Profile audit table
- Cardinal Rule (update name, keep logic)
- Delivery format
- Quality gate

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Pre-filling the offer but leaving the value bridge generic
- Keeping Daniel Paul's hook examples as the sample language
- Forgetting to calibrate the energy level for DMs vs posts

## SKILL 4 — NEWSLETTER WRITER REWRITE

### What changes

**VOICE LOCK:**
Same embedding as Caption Writer.
Add newsletter-specific note:
"Newsletters are longer than posts. Voice rules apply — allow slightly longer paragraphs
(max 4 lines vs 3 in posts). Conversational but still specific. Never recap content
the reader already knows from [PARTICIPANT]'s posts."

**STEP 1 — INTAKE:**
Pre-fill: participant, ICP, offer.
Only ask per session: this week's topic/lesson, this week's win, the actionable takeaway, the one ask.

Add: "THIS WEEK'S WIN must be a real result — [PARTICIPANT]'s standing proof points are:
[list from Tailor Briefing]. Use one of these if no new win exists. Flag if used repeatedly."

**STEP 3 — FRAMEWORK SELECTION:**
Add: "[PARTICIPANT]'s newsletter defaults:
- If topic is a client story → BAB
- If topic is a lesson or mistake → Teachable Moment
- If topic is a contrarian opinion → Contrarian Take
Override only if the specific topic clearly calls for a different framework."

**SECTION 2 — THIS WEEK'S LESSON:**
Add specificity rule: "The lesson must contain at least one detail only [PARTICIPANT]
could have. If it could have come from any [INDUSTRY] newsletter — add a specific example
from [PARTICIPANT]'s experience."

**SECTION 3 — ONE FRAMEWORK:**
Add naming standard: "The framework name must reference [PARTICIPANT]'s methodology
or niche where possible. '[PARTICIPANT]'s [Framework Name]' is stronger than a generic name."

**SECTION 4 — THIS WEEK'S WIN:**
Hard rule: "Must use a real number. If no new win exists, use the most recent standing
proof point: [PROOF POINT 1 from Tailor Briefing]. Never use vague language here."

**STEP 5 — SUBJECT LINES:**
Add: "[PARTICIPANT]'s ICP reads email on [device/context if known]. Subject lines
that reference [niche-specific language the ICP uses] outperform generic LinkedIn tips."

### What stays unchanged
- The 7-step workflow structure
- The 5-section newsletter format
- The subject line formula table
- The Hook Quality Test
- The word count target (400-600)
- The Invisibility Diagnostic
- The quality gate structure

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Leaving Daniel Paul's standing proof points in the This Week's Win section
- Generic framework names ("The 3-Step Method") instead of participant-branded names
- Subject lines that don't reflect the participant's niche language

## SKILL 5 — SALES CALL PREP REWRITE

### What changes

**STEP 1 — INTAKE:**
Pre-fill: participant name, offer name, offer transformation, ICP profile.
The "GOAL FOR THIS CALL" section stays per-session — it changes per prospect.

Add: "[PARTICIPANT]'s standard discovery call goal: [qualify/explore/close — their default].
Adjust per prospect based on how they came in."

**STEP 2 — PROSPECT RESEARCH:**
Add: "[PARTICIPANT]'s ICP signals to look for:
- They fit if: [2-3 ICP qualification signals from Tailor Briefing]
- They don't fit if: [2-3 disqualification signals — wrong stage, wrong pain, wrong budget]"

**SECTION 1 — PRE-CALL INTELLIGENCE:**
Add the ICP awareness level standard:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s ICP typically enters at [AWARENESS LEVEL from Tailor Briefing].
Calibrate the call opening to match — don't pitch solution awareness to a problem-aware prospect."

**SECTION 4 — QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS:**
The BANT+ framework stays.
Add ICP-specific variations:
"[PARTICIPANT]'s ICP-specific pain questions:
- [Pain question 1 tailored to their ICP's specific situation]
- [Pain question 2]
- [Pain question 3]
Use these before the generic BANT+ questions."

**SECTION 5 — PAIN EXCAVATION:**
The 3-level structure stays.
Add [PARTICIPANT]'s specific excavation language:
"Level 3 (Root Pain) for [PARTICIPANT]'s ICP: The identity threat is usually [specific
fear or identity cost this ICP faces — e.g. 'being seen as a commodity' or 'losing relevance'].
Surface this specifically."

**SECTION 6 — OBJECTION SCRIPTS:**
The 3-objection structure stays.
Replace the generic objections with the 3 most likely for THIS participant's ICP and offer.
Pull from the Tailor Briefing's ICP field — the objections are implied in the ICP's pains
and previous failed attempts.

The objection scripts must use [PARTICIPANT]'s language — not Daniel Paul's defaults.
Every script: Stated / Real / Response (in participant's voice) / Move.

**SECTION 7 — TRANSITION TO CLOSE:**
Update the offer reference:
"Based on everything you've shared — I think there's a real fit for [OFFER NAME].
[OFFER NAME] is for [ICP DESCRIPTION] who [SPECIFIC SITUATION]. What you've described
matches that exactly."

**SECTION 9 — POST-CALL FOLLOW-UP:**
Update the sign-off from "Daniel" to [PARTICIPANT NAME].
Update the follow-up framing to reference [OFFER NAME] and [PARTICIPANT]'s sales process.

### What stays unchanged
- The 9-section call brief structure
- The BANT+ ordering (Pain first, Budget last)
- The 3-level pain excavation structure
- The 6 objection categories
- The Red Flags section
- The delivery format
- The quality gate structure

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Leaving "Daniel" as the sign-off in the follow-up template
- Using generic objection scripts instead of the 3 most likely for this specific ICP
- Not updating the offer name in the close transition
- Missing the ICP awareness level calibration in the opening section
