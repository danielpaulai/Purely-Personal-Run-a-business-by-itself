# ◈ THE DEAL TRACKER

> You don't need more leads. You need to know which conversations to have today, and exactly what to say in them.

This skill turns a messy pipeline into a precise action plan. No vague advice. No prioritised lists without messages. Every deal gets a diagnosis. Every Priority 1 and Priority 2 deal gets the exact DM to send next.

---

## ━━ THE OPERATING PRINCIPLE ━━

> Prioritise by momentum, not by value.

A $500 deal that's moving beats a $5,000 deal that's been stuck for three weeks. Momentum compounds. Stagnation costs you energy and calendar space you don't have.

This skill manages three things in one review:
1. **New deals**, where they are and what to do next
2. **Blocked deals**, what's actually stopping them and how to unblock
3. **Existing clients**, who needs attention before they quietly churn

---

## ━━ BEFORE YOU BEGIN ━━

Ask the user to share their pipeline. Any format works, a list of names, bullet notes, a paste of their CRM, or a brain dump.

For each person, collect what's available:

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Name / Company** | Identity |
| **Stage** | Prospect / Active / Pitched / Awaiting / Client |
| **Last contact date** | Urgency and momentum read |
| **Last conversation summary** | What was said, what was felt |
| **Objections raised** | What's in the way |
| **Buying signals shown** | How close they actually are |
| **Deal value** | Context for prioritisation |

Also ask:
- *"Any current clients to include for a retention or upsell check?"*
- *"Which deal are you most worried about right now?"*

> If information is partial, work with what you have, but note the gaps and ask for more where it would change the output.

## ━━ PART 1, PIPELINE TRIAGE ━━

For every deal in the pipeline, assign a **Priority Level** and a **Deal Momentum Score**. Run blocking analysis for anything that isn't moving.

---

### Priority Levels

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  🔴  PRIORITY 1, ACT TODAY                            │
│  Strong signals. Real momentum. A delay risks the deal. │
│  They may be evaluating other options right now.        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  🟡  PRIORITY 2, ACT THIS WEEK                        │
│  Engaged but not yet committed. Conversation needs      │
│  a deliberate next move to keep advancing.              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  🟢  PRIORITY 3, NURTURE                              │
│  Interested but timing isn't right yet. Stay visible.   │
│  Touch once, with value, then leave space.              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ⚫  DEPRIORITISE                                       │
│  No signal after multiple attempts. Wrong fit.          │
│  Or a "not now" with no re-engagement trigger.          │
│  Archive and move on.                                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Deal Momentum Score

Rate each deal 1–5. This is not about deal value, it's about the energy and direction of the conversation.

```
┌──────┬────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  5/5 │ Hot, fast replies, initiating questions,      │
│      │ asking about your offer, future-tense language │
├──────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  4/5 │ Warm, engaged, progressing, consistent        │
│      │ replies, curious energy                        │
├──────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  3/5 │ Lukewarm, replying but slow or non-committal  │
│      │ Hard to read. Could go either way.             │
├──────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  2/5 │ Cooling, long gaps, minimal engagement,       │
│      │ short replies with no follow-through           │
├──────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  1/5 │ Cold, no reply in 5+ days, no signal,        │
│      │ or the last reply felt like a door closing     │
└──────┴────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Blocking Analysis

For every deal that isn't moving, name the real blocker. Be honest, even if it's uncomfortable.

| Blocker | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **Pitched too early** | Offer went out before Stage 6 (Curiosity) in the conversation |
| **Stuck at a stage** | Conversation reached a point and stopped, often Connection or Clarity |
| **Unaddressed objection** | An objection surfaced but wasn't properly handled |
| **Timing mismatch** | They said "not now" without a clear path back |
| **ICP misfit** | This person was probably never a strong fit |
| **Conversation gap** | Too much time passed with no re-engagement |
| **Wrong energy** | Messages felt too salesy, too desperate, or too robotic |

## ━━ PART 3, CLIENT RETENTION & UPSELL ━━

**The revenue that already exists is the easiest revenue to protect and grow.**

A client who stays one more month is revenue you didn't have to prospect for. A client who adds a second service is a deal that closes without a cold outreach sequence.

Run this section for every existing client included in the review.

---

### Retention Health Check

Flag each client's status:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ⚠  AT RISK                                             │
│  No meaningful contact in 14+ days. Signs of cooling.   │
│  Declining engagement. A complaint that wasn't resolved. │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ✓  HEALTHY                                             │
│  Engaged, seeing results, communicating positively.     │
│  No red flags.                                          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ✦  CELEBRATION MOMENT                                  │
│  They've had a recent win or milestone.                 │
│  This is the most underused moment in client management. │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Generate a check-in message for At Risk clients:**
> *"Hey [Name], checking in. How's the momentum been on your end lately?"*

**Generate a recognition message for Celebration Moments:**
> *"Loved seeing [specific result], that's exactly what we built this for."*

---

### Upsell Opportunities

Look for clients who are ready to expand. The signals:
- They're getting clear results from the current engagement
- They've mentioned new goals, new challenges, or new hires in conversation
- They've been a client for 3+ months with no change in scope

**The expansion nudge, always low-pressure, always relevant:**
> *"A thought, given how [current thing] is going, have you considered applying the same approach to [adjacent area]? Might be the next natural step."*

## ━━ NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES ━━

- **No vague advice.** Every Priority 1 and Priority 2 deal gets a specific message, not a suggestion.
- **Momentum over value.** A $500 deal that's moving gets higher priority than a $5,000 deal that's been stalled for three weeks.
- **Name the blocker.** If a deal was pitched too early, say so. If the conversation died because of wrong energy, say so. Dishonest analysis produces the wrong next move.
- **One action per deal.** Don't give the user a list of five things to do for one person. One clear next step, executed well, is worth more than five half-executed options.
- **Retention is not a second-class priority.** Protecting existing revenue is the most cost-effective sales activity available. Treat it accordingly.
- **The Focus Summary is not optional.** End every review with three specific actions. Give the user their day.

## Voice lock and quality gate (added v4.1.0)
Read references/voice-dna.md before writing. Write in the user's voice. No fallback.
Before delivering anything, run this gate:
- Zero em dashes anywhere.
- No banned words or AI patterns (references/ai-pattern-blacklist.md, references/human-writing-standards.md).
- Specific to this one prospect, not a template. If you could send it to anyone, rewrite it.
- Never invent a name, number, result, or detail. If you do not have it, ask or leave a clear placeholder.
- Final test: would the user say "I wrote that"? If not, rebuild.
