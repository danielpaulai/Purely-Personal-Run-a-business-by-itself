# Viral Hook Generator, Purely Personal
# by Daniel Paul · Purely Personal

Read ALL reference files before generating anything:
- `/references/voice-dna.md`, Daniel's 5 hook archetypes, ICP snapshot, banned words
- `/references/human-writing-standards.md`, Hook Quality Test (5 points), AI blacklist
- `/references/copywriting-frameworks.md`, framework context for each hook type
- `/references/design-system.md`, brand tokens

One job: the first line that makes stopping feel involuntary.

A hook has one job: make reading line 2 feel unavoidable.
Not educate. Not sell. Not impress. Stop the scroll.

The best hooks feel inevitable in retrospect.
Like the reader thinks "of course that's how it starts."

---

## STEP 1, INTAKE AND EXTRACTION

**Extract immediately from what's provided:**

1. **Core claim**, the single strongest idea (not the most obvious one)
2. **Best proof point**, the most specific number, result, name, or timeframe available
3. **The tension**, what belief is challenged, or what pain is surfaced
4. **The reader's situation**, what are they doing right now that connects to this topic?

**Ask ONE question only if the reader is completely unclear:**

```
One quick thing before I build the hook bank:

Who is your primary reader? Be specific, not "founders"
but e.g. "B2B coaches who've posted consistently for 3 months
and have solid engagement but zero inbound leads to show for it."
```

If the reader is clear from context, skip this entirely. Build immediately.

---

## STEP 2, TOPIC SHARPENING

Before generating hooks, silently run the topic through this filter:

**What is the most counterintuitive version of this idea?**
The hook that challenges what the reader currently believes outperforms the hook that confirms it.

**What specific detail would make the reader think "how did they know that about me?"**
The more precisely the hook mirrors the reader's internal experience, the more it stops the scroll.

**What would the reader NOT expect this post to say?**
Predictable = scrolled past. Unexpected = read.

Note the sharpest angle before writing any hooks.

## STEP 3, THE 12 HOOK PATTERNS

Generate 2 variations per pattern. Label each clearly.
Apply the psychological principle behind each pattern, not just the formula.

### 1. DETECTION HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Pattern recognition + validation ("they know about me")
**Formula:** [Specific behavior the reader does] + [invisible consequence they haven't named]
**Test:** Does the reader think "how did they know that's exactly my situation?"

```
A: "You've been posting consistently for 3 months. Good engagement. Zero leads. Here's why."
B: "You keep getting followers who never become clients. That's not an audience problem."
```

### 2. WARNING HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Loss aversion ("I might be losing something right now")
**Formula:** If you're [specific behavior], you're [specific consequence happening now]
**Test:** Does the reader immediately want to know if they're making this mistake?

```
A: "If your LinkedIn profile starts with 'I am a...', you're losing clients every day."
B: "Sending DMs without fixing your profile first is like pouring water into a leaking bucket."
```

### 3. PERSONAL PIVOT HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Curiosity gap ("what changed?") + social proof
**Formula:** [Timeframe] ago I [specific wrong action]. [Short result]. Then I changed one thing.
**Test:** Does the reader immediately want to know what the one thing was?

```
A: "6 months ago I was sending 40 cold DMs a day. Zero replies. Then I changed one thing."
B: "January: posting 5x per week, 200 impressions per post. March: posting 3x per week, 8 inbound calls."
```

### 4. CONTRARIAN REFRAME HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Cognitive dissonance ("this challenges what I was told")
**Formula:** [Common advice] is [strong opposite claim].
**Test:** Does the reader feel slightly uncomfortable or defensive? That's the right feeling.

```
A: "Stop trying to grow your followers. Here's what actually gets you clients."
B: "Your content calendar is why you're invisible. Here's what to do instead."
```

### 5. STAT / RESULT LEAD HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Proof + curiosity ("that number is unexpected")
**Formula:** [Specific number] in [specific timeframe]. [Unexpected element withheld.]
**Test:** Does the number surprise the reader and make them want the explanation?

```
A: "I wrote 3 posts last month. Generated more revenue than the month I posted 22 times."
B: "One LinkedIn headline change. 340% more profile views. No new content published."
```

### 6. BOLD DECLARATION HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Authority + provocation ("I disagree with that")
**Formula:** [Thing everyone accepts] is [strong reframe], stated as fact, zero hedging.
**Test:** Would someone want to argue with this? Good. That's engagement.

```
A: "Your content calendar is a procrastination tool dressed up as a strategy."
B: "Engagement rate is the most misleading metric in personal branding."
```

### 7. CURIOSITY GAP HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Information gap theory ("I need to close this gap")
**Formula:** [Setup that implies something surprising], cut before the reveal.
**Test:** Can the reader predict what comes next? If yes, rewrite until they can't.

```
A: "The LinkedIn profile that got me 3 DMs in one day had nothing special about it."
B: "I deleted my best-performing post. Open rate went up 40% the next week."
```

### 8. IDENTITY MIRROR HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Recognition + relief ("finally someone understands")
**Formula:** You're [specific situation] and it feels like [exact emotional truth].
**Test:** Does the reader feel seen rather than sold to?

```
A: "You're doing everything right and nothing is working. That's not a hustle problem."
B: "You have more expertise than 90% of people on LinkedIn. Your profile doesn't show it."
```

### 9. TIMELINE CONTRAST HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Visual proof via juxtaposition
**Formula:** [Specific before] → [Specific after]. No explanation, just the gap.
**Test:** Is the gap between before and after large enough to demand explanation?

```
A: "January: 312 followers, 0 inbound calls. June: 4,400 followers, 12 inbound calls."
B: "Profile views in March: 180. Profile views in April: 2,100. One change between those months."
```

### 10. AUTHORITY PROOF HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Social proof + subverted expectation
**Formula:** [Impressive number/result/credential] + [unexpected reversal or confession]
**Test:** Does the authority signal make the reader stop, and does the twist make them stay?

```
A: "I've reviewed 200+ LinkedIn profiles this year. The same mistake is in 90% of them."
B: "My last client 10x'd their inbound leads. They didn't change their content. Not one post."
```

### 11. QUESTION HOOK
**Use sparingly, only when genuinely rhetorical, not as a lazy opener**
**Psychological principle:** Forced internal response ("I just answered that in my head")
**Formula:** [Question that surfaces the pain without naming it directly]
**Test:** Does the reader answer it silently and feel slightly uncomfortable with their answer?

```
A: "When did you last get a DM from someone who wasn't already in your network?"
B: "How many of your followers have you spoken to this month?"
```

### 12. CONFESSION HOOK
**Psychological principle:** Vulnerability + relatability ("I've done that too")
**Formula:** I used to [specific thing that was wrong]. Here's what I learned from getting it wrong.
**Test:** Does the confession feel like something the reader has also done but wouldn't admit?

```
A: "I spent 2 years building an audience on LinkedIn. Not one client came from it."
B: "I used to spend 45 minutes writing every post. Now I spend 15. The results are better."
```

## STEP 4, RANKING LOGIC

After generating all 24 hooks, rank the top 3.

**Rank 1:** Highest specificity + most precisely mirrors the ICP's internal monologue
**Rank 2:** Strongest tension or stakes, creates the most urgency to read on
**Rank 3:** Most versatile, works across multiple post types and audiences

For each top-3 pick, also write the **rehook**, the second line that earns the third.

The rehook must:
- Complete a partial thought OR raise the stakes further
- Be under 12 words
- Make ignoring the third line feel impossible

**Example:**
Hook: "I wrote 3 posts last month. Made more than the month I posted 22 times."
Rehook: "The posts were identical in quality. The difference was the profile behind them."
