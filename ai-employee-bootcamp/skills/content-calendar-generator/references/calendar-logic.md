## THE 7 SEQUENCING RULES

Every 30-day calendar is built using these 7 rules in strict order.
None are optional. All apply simultaneously.

**Rule 1, Pillar Rotation**
No two consecutive days from the same pillar.
The reader must experience variety. Repeating the same pillar back-to-back
signals a sales agenda, not a content strategy.

**Rule 2, The 20% Conversion Cap**
Conversion-intent posts appear no more than 6 times in 30 days.
6 out of 30 = 20%. If posting 5x per week, that is ~1 conversion post per week.
If posting 3x per week, conversion posts appear every 2 weeks at most.

**Rule 3, The Hook Rule for Day 1 and Day 30**
Day 1 is the first impression. Day 30 is the closing statement.
Neither is a sales post. Both are high-engagement hooks, curiosity, confession,
bold opinion, or strong social proof. These are the days that set the tone
and end on a memorable note.

**Rule 4, Format Variety Within Each Week**
No week should have more than 2 posts of the same format.
A week of all text posts is a wall. A week of all carousels is a production factory.
Mix: text post + carousel + poll or story + text post + text post max.

**Rule 5, The Warm-Up Principle**
Weeks 1–2 = awareness and trust-building intent dominant (60–70% of posts).
Weeks 3–4 = authority and conversion intent increase (up to 20–25% conversion).
The audience needs to know and trust before they buy. Do not rush the sequence.

**Rule 6, The Pattern Rule**
Every week should have a recognisable rhythm, not necessarily the same days,
but a consistent emotional arc: curiosity → value → authority → human.
The reader should feel like there is a person behind the content, not a machine.

**Rule 7, No Vague Topic Entries**
Every calendar entry must be specific enough to write from immediately.
✗ "Share a tip about LinkedIn"
✓ "The one LinkedIn profile mistake that kills your first impression in 3 seconds"
Every entry is a near-complete post brief.

|-------|---------|-----------|-----------------|---------|

| Trust-building | 35% | 30% | 20% | 15% |
| Conversion | 25% | 30% | 25% | 20% |

---

## THE 4 POST INTENTS, DEFINED

**Educate:**
Teaches something specific and actionable. Reader leaves knowing something they did not know.
Builds authority through generosity. Not a sales post.

**Authority:**
Demonstrates expertise through specific proof, a result, a case, a methodology, a bold opinion.
Builds credibility without teaching a full lesson.

**Entertain / Human:**
Personal, vulnerable, funny, or relatable. Builds the human connection that
makes the audience want to follow a person, not just a content stream.
No agenda other than connection.

**Convert:**
Makes a specific ask, a DM, a comment keyword, a booking link, a lead magnet.
Only works after trust is established. Never appears before Day 7.

## CALENDAR ENTRY FORMAT

Every single entry must contain all 5 of these fields:

```
Day [N] | Pillar: [Pillar Name] | Format: [Text / Carousel / Poll / Story]
Topic/Angle: [Specific post title or angle, specific enough to write from today]
Intent: [Educate / Authority / Entertain / Convert]
Goal: [One sentence, what this post achieves for the reader and the creator]
```

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS

✗ All posts being educational with no conversion, builds an audience of learners, not buyers
✗ Conversion posts in Week 1, the audience does not trust you yet
✗ Same pillar 3 days in a row, feels like a campaign, not a creator
✗ All posts the same format, content factory energy, not human energy
✗ Vague topic entries, cannot be written without additional thinking
✗ Day 1 being a sales or soft-sell post, first impression ruined
✗ Day 30 being a low-energy post, end on a high or it feels like you gave up
