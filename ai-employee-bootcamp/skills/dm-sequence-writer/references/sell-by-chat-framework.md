# Sell-by-Chat Framework, Purely Personal Reference
# by Daniel Paul · Purely Personal

---

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

Stop selling. Start serving. Selling is a side effect of being genuinely helpful.

Nobody wants to be sold to. They want to be heard, understood, and helped. Every DM is an opportunity to make someone's situation clearer, and position yourself as the person who does that best.

**The only question before every message:** Am I giving before I ask?

---

## METRICS THAT MATTER

Track these. Nothing else.

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Reply Rate | ~30% |
| Engagement Rate (replies → real conversation) | ~50% |
| Call Booking Rate | ~20% of conversations |
| Show-Up Rate | ~80% |
| Close Rate | ~50% of attended calls |
| **Overall conversion: ~1 close per 100 DMs** | |

**Daily baseline to aim for:** 25–30 outreach contacts/day → 7–9 replies → 3–5 real conversations → 1 call booked.

## CODE OF CONDUCT, NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Rule 1, Be Human, Not Robotic**
Write like you're talking to someone you respect. Short sentences. Line breaks. Real tone. No copy-paste feel.

**Rule 2, Make Them Feel Seen**
Reference something specific: their post, their role, a detail from their content. People open up when they feel noticed.

**Rule 3, Prioritise Empathy**
Listen first. Acknowledge before you respond. Trust is built in the pause before your answer.

**Rule 4, Keep Messages Short**
3–4 messages max per exchange. No walls of text. Let them breathe between replies.

**Rule 5, One Question at a Time**
Stack questions = anxiety signal. One great question = confidence signal.

**Rule 6, Match Their Pace**
Fast replies? Match them. Slow replies? Give space. Forcing pace kills the vibe.

**Rule 7, Give Before You Ask**
Always deliver value before making any request. Insight, tip, resource, observation, earn the ask.

**Rule 8, Follow Up With Purpose**
Not chasing. Showing you care. Reference the last exchange or bring something new.

**Rule 9, Personalise Every Message**
If this message could go to anyone, it goes to no one. Rewrite it.

**Rule 10, Own the Outcome**
Always close with a clear next step, even if it's just keeping the door open.

## CONVERSATION STRATEGY

### Openers That Work
The opener is not about you. It's about them.
- Make it about them
- Keep it short (shorter = more weight per word)
- Lead with observation, not pitch

### Precision Flattery
Not empty praise. Specific acknowledgment.
- Call out what they did, not just that they did it
- Reference something niche, only someone paying attention would know
- Make them feel understood, not complimented

### Pattern Interrupts
Unexpected but relevant. Break the predictable pitch format.
- Speak their niche language, something only an insider would say
- Create an emotional hook (analogy, sharp observation, GIF)
- Surprise them, but tie it back to their world

### Asking Questions
- One at a time, always
- Make them specific, not vague ("What's the hardest part about X?" not "What's your challenge?")
- Dig into the gap between where they are (A) and where they want to be (B)
- Connect your next question to what they actually said

---

## THE A→B METHOD (QUALIFYING)

**Point A:** Where are they now? What's the real problem?
**Point B:** Where do they want to be? What does success look like?

The gap between A and B = your opening. Stretch it. Make it real. The bigger they feel the gap, the more clearly they see the need for a solution.

**LVQ Rhythm (use in every exchange):**
- **Lube**, Acknowledge what they said. "That makes sense." "Got it." Remove friction.
- **Value**, Share an insight, tip, or observation that's genuinely useful.
- **Question**, One open-ended question that moves the conversation deeper.

## MICRO-COMMITMENTS

Build the "yes" habit before asking for the big yes.

1. **Make it feel easy**, "Does Tuesday at 11 or Wednesday at 2 work?" (binary, specific)
2. **Build emotional momentum**, speak as if the next step is already in motion
3. **Link to outcome**, tie every ask to a result they care about
4. **Break big decisions into small ones**, one step at a time
5. **Always tie to value**, "Once we lock this in, you'll have [specific result]"

---

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

**Never use "but."** It cancels everything before it.

Instead:
- ❌ "I understand your concern, **but** this could solve it."
- ✅ "I understand your concern, **and** I think this approach could make things easier."

**Three-step objection flow:**
1. **Acknowledge**, "That makes complete sense. Timing is always tricky."
2. **Ask for context**, "What's the underlying worry here?"
3. **Reassure with proof**, "One of my clients felt exactly the same way. Here's what changed for them."

## FOLLOW-UP RULES

80% of sales happen after the 5th touchpoint. Most people stop at 1 or 2.

**Follow-up timing:**
- Day 1, day after first message or call
- Day 3, keep momentum
- Day 7, thoughtful nudge
- After that, every 2 weeks (don't burn the lead)

**Every follow-up must do one of these:**
- Reference something from the last conversation
- Bring something new (a resource, a result, an observation)
- Connect their challenge to a recent win from another client

**Never:** "Just checking in." "Following up on my last message." These are lazy. Bring something.

## COLD TACTICS

**1. Connection requests to ICP**
Up to 200/week. Personalise every one. "Hey [Name], great to connect! [Pattern interrupt based on their content/role]?"

**2. Dormant connection revival**
"It's been a while since we connected back when [specific context]. I've been working on [brief offer description] for [specific audience]. Not sure if it's relevant to you, but if you know anyone who'd benefit, I'd love to send over the details."

**3. Strategic comments**
Comment meaningfully on posts your ICP reads. Builds visibility, earns inbound connection requests, starts conversations.

---

## WARM SIGNAL RESPONSES (PRIORITISE THESE)

Warm signals always outperform cold outreach. Act on them within hours.

**Profile views:**
"Hey [Name], noticed you checked out my profile. Are you looking into [specific outcome] or just enjoying the content?"

**Post likes:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for liking my post on [topic]. Was it [specific idea] that resonated, or something else?"

**Post comments:**
"Hey [Name], loved your comment on my post about [topic]. Are you working on [related challenge] right now?"

**New connections:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for connecting! Are you here to explore [specific challenge/solution] or just checking out content? Either way, great to connect."

---

## BOOKING CALLS, ATTENDANCE STRATEGY

**Keep it close:** Offer times within 3 business days. Enthusiasm fades fast.

**Skip the calendar link.** Propose specific options: "Tuesday at 11 or Wednesday at 2, which works?"

**24-hour reminder:**
"Hey [Name], just a quick reminder about our chat tomorrow at [time]. Can't wait to get into [their specific challenge]. Here's the link: [link]. See you then!"

**Build anticipation:**
"Before we meet, I pulled together a few notes from similar clients who tackled [their problem]. I think you'll get value even before we start. Looking forward to tomorrow."

---

## PRELOADED VALUE RULES

1. **Be specific**, reference their industry, role, content, or stated problem
2. **Make it ungated**, share it freely. Generosity builds trust.
3. **Keep it actionable**, give them something they can use immediately
4. **Tie it to the next step**, your value should naturally open the door to continued conversation

---

## CLOSING THE LOOP

Every conversation ends with a clear next step, even if it's just leaving the door open with warmth.

"I know timing might not be perfect right now, totally get it. I'll check back in a few weeks. If anything changes before then, just let me know."

Never let a conversation end in ambiguity. Ambiguity = lost deal.
