# Positioning Framework
# Matchmaker Reference, Purely Personal Bootcamp

This file teaches the Matchmaker how to evaluate whether a skill is positioning
the participant correctly in their market. Read this before auditing any skill.

---

## WHAT POSITIONING ACTUALLY MEANS

Positioning is not a tagline. It is not a mission statement.
Positioning is the context you set that makes a person's value obvious to the right buyer.

When a skill is unpositioned, it writes content that could come from anyone.
When a skill is correctly positioned, it writes content that could only come from THIS person.

The gap between those two states is what the Matchmaker exists to find.

---

## THE 5-COMPONENT POSITIONING AUDIT

For every skill, check whether it has access to all five components.
If any one is missing, the skill will produce generic, undifferentiated output.

### Component 1, Competitive Alternatives
What would the participant's ICP do if this person didn't exist?
- Hire a VA? Do it themselves? Use a cheaper tool? Hire an agency?
- The skill must know this. Without it, content and DMs cannot position against
  the real competition, which is often not a direct competitor but inaction or DIY.

**Audit question:** Does this skill know what the ICP's default alternative is?
If no, flag as Competitive Alternatives Gap.

### Component 2, Unique Attributes
What does this participant offer that the alternatives do not?
Not "I'm better at it", what is structurally different?
- Methodology, delivery model, access, speed, specificity, community, guarantee.
- These become the non-negotiable proof points in every piece of content and every DM.

**Audit question:** Does this skill know what makes this offer structurally different?
If no, flag as Unique Attributes Gap.

### Component 3, Value for the ICP
What specific outcome does the ICP get that they cannot get from alternatives?
- Not features. Not benefits. Outcomes, the actual change in their situation.
- "I save them 10 hours a week" is a feature. "They stop losing deals because their
  LinkedIn looks like a ghost town" is an outcome.

**Audit question:** Does this skill know the specific outcome this participant delivers?
If no, flag as Value Gap.

### Component 4, Target Market Characteristics
Who specifically wins most from this offer?
- What role? What stage of business? What problem severity? What previous attempts?
- The ICP is not "founders" or "coaches." The ICP is "bootstrapped SaaS founders
  who've been posting for 90+ days with under 500 followers and zero inbound leads."

**Audit question:** Does this skill know the precise ICP definition, including stage and situation?
If no, flag as Target Market Gap.

### Component 5, Market Category
What category does this person compete in, and is that category choice deliberate?
- A LinkedIn coach competes differently than a "B2B lead generation specialist."
- The category determines the comparison set in the buyer's mind.
- Owning a new or adjacent category beats competing in a crowded one.

**Audit question:** Does this skill know what category this person occupies, or is creating?
If no, flag as Market Category Gap.

## COMPETITIVE LANGUAGE STANDARDS

A skill that doesn't know the competitive landscape will:
- Use positioning language the entire market uses ("authentic," "strategic," "results-driven")
- Write content that blends with competitors instead of differentiating from them
- Produce DMs that sound like every other DM the prospect receives

Before auditing each skill, extract from the participant's foundation documents:

**Who are the top 3-5 competitors or peers in their space?**
Note what each one is known for and what they overuse in their content.
A fitted skill must actively avoid those patterns and occupy different territory.

**What language is saturated in this niche?**
If every LinkedIn coach says "I help you find your authentic voice," that phrase
is dead. A fitted skill must know the dead phrases and route around them.

**What angle is nobody taking yet?**
This is the positioning opportunity. A fitted skill should amplify this angle, 
not accidentally echo the crowd.

## MARKET AWARENESS LEVELS

| Level | ICP State | What content/copy must do |
|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Doesn't know they have the problem | Lead with a pattern interrupt |
| Problem Aware | Knows the pain, doesn't know solutions exist | Name the exact problem in their own language |
| Solution Aware | Knows solutions exist, hasn't chosen one | Show why your method is structurally different |
| Product Aware | Knows you exist, hasn't committed | Social proof, results, specificity |
| Most Aware | Knows you, likes you, needs a reason to act now | Make the offer and next step obvious |

**Audit question:** What awareness level is this participant's ICP at?
Does the skill write copy calibrated to that level?

## HOW TO IDENTIFY A POSITIONING GAP IN A SKILL

A skill has a positioning gap when its output could be published under a different
person's name without anyone noticing.

Signs of a positioning gap:
- Uses industry jargon the entire niche uses
- Doesn't name a specific ICP, uses placeholders like "your audience"
- Doesn't reference the participant's specific offer, methodology, or results
- Could apply to any competitor
- Makes claims without proof points

Signs of correct positioning:
- Names the specific type of person the content is for
- References a real result or specific methodology
- Takes a position competitors would disagree with
- Uses language the ICP uses, not a polished version of it
- Points toward a specific next step unique to this participant's sales process
