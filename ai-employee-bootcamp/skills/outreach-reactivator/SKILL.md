# ◈ THE REACTIVATOR

> Silence is not a verdict. It's usually just bad timing meeting a full inbox.

Most founders interpret no reply as rejection and move on. The Reactivator treats silence as incomplete information, and turns that information gap into an opening.

---

## ━━ THE CORE INSIGHT ━━

There are only three reasons a conversation goes quiet:

**1. Timing**, They were interested but something got in the way: a busy week, a shifting priority, a decision that couldn't be made yet. This is the most common reason. And timing changes.

**2. Uncertainty**, They weren't sure how to respond. Your last message might have asked too much, moved too fast, or landed in a moment when they couldn't engage properly.

**3. Misalignment**, The fit was never strong enough to generate momentum. This is actually the least common reason, but it's the one most people assume first.

Your job before writing anything: figure out which of these three it is.

---

## ━━ BEFORE YOU BEGIN ━━

Ask the user:

1. **When did they last reply?** (Days / weeks / months ago)
2. **What did the last exchange look like?** (Paste it if possible)
3. **Where were they in the conversation progression?** (Early / mid / near the offer)
4. **Did they ever show interest?** (Even casual interest, a question, a comment, a "sounds interesting")
5. **What was their most likely pain point based on the conversation?**

If the user doesn't know the answer to 3 or 4, ask them to paste the last few messages. Read those before proceeding.

**Before writing a single word, determine which situation this is:**

```

│  SITUATION ASSESSMENT                                       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│  TYPE A, Active Thread Gone Quiet                         │
│  They replied at some point, then stopped.                 │
│  The conversation was in motion but has stalled.           │
│  Gap: days to 2–3 weeks                                    │
│                                                             │
│  TYPE B, Cold Lead Reactivation                           │
│  A past conversation that didn't convert.                  │
```

**These require completely different approaches. Never conflate them.**

A Type A needs a quiet re-entry with fresh value.
A Type B needs to be treated as a new conversation built on an old foundation.

## ━━ TYPE A, ACTIVE THREAD GONE QUIET ━━

**The situation:** They were engaged. Something caused the silence. The conversation has stalled, not ended.

**What not to do:**
```
✗ "Hey, just checking in!", signals you have nothing to add
✗ Repeating your last message, shows you didn't notice they didn't reply
✗ Asking "Did you see my message?", guilty, pressuring, passive-aggressive
✗ Sending more than 2 re-engagement attempts, after that, it's chasing
✗ Pitching in a follow-up, you haven't re-earned the right yet
```

---

### STEP A1, SILENCE DIAGNOSIS

Before writing, identify where the conversation stalled and why:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SILENCE DIAGNOSIS                          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Last active stage:                         │
│  [Contact / Connection / Clarity /          │
│   Confirmation / Gap / Curiosity / Close]   │
│                                             │
│  Most likely cause of silence:             │
│  [Timing shift / Too much asked /           │
│   Wrong moment / Decision needed /          │
│   Genuine misalignment]                     │
│                                             │
│  Re-entry strategy:                         │
│  [What approach fits this specific gap]     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### STEP A2, RE-ENTRY MESSAGE

**Timing: 2–3 days after the last unanswered message**

The re-entry message has one job: give them a genuine reason to come back into the conversation. Not a reminder that you exist. A reason.

**The re-entry formula:**
- A fresh observation, insight, or question that connects to their world
- No reference to the silence, don't make it awkward
- End with a single, low-effort question
- Maximum 3–4 sentences

**Strong examples:**
> *"Hey [Name], came across something today that made me think of what you were saying about [their specific situation]. [One specific insight or observation]. Thought it might land well for where you're at."*

> *"Hey [Name], random one, but I saw [relevant thing in their niche] this week and it reminded me of the conversation we were having. Curious if [question tied to their goal]?"*

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  A2 · RE-ENTRY MESSAGE                      │
│  Timing: 2–3 days after last message        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Message]                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### STEP A3, CLEAN EXIT (IF NO REPLY TO RE-ENTRY)

**Timing: 3–5 days after the re-entry message**

If they don't reply to A2, this is the last message. It closes the loop with warmth and leaves the door permanently open, without any resentment or pressure.

This message is about your reputation as much as anything. People remember how they were treated when they didn't reply.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  A3 · CLEAN EXIT                            │
│  Timing: 3–5 days after A2 if no reply      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Message]                                  │
│                                             │
│  Pattern: "Not going to keep appearing      │
│  in your inbox. If [their goal/situation]   │
│  becomes relevant at any point, easy to     │
│  find me. Hope things are going well."      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## ━━ TYPE B, COLD LEAD REACTIVATION ━━

**The situation:** A conversation that happened weeks or months ago. The timing wasn't right then. But timing shifts, and you have something cold leads don't give you: a foundation.

**What makes this different from cold outreach:**
- Trust already exists at some level, you're not a stranger
- You have context: their situation, their language, possibly their pain point
- You don't have to start from zero, you can reference the past and build forward
- The familiarity lowers the guard before you've said anything

**The three-stage reactivation sequence:**

---

### STAGE 1, THE SOFT RETURN

**Timing: Day 1**

Re-enter with genuine curiosity, no agenda. The goal of Stage 1 is not a sale, not a qualified conversation, not even a full response. The goal is simply: a reply.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STAGE 1 · SOFT RETURN                      │
│  Timing: Day 1                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Message]                                  │
│                                             │
│  Pattern: "Hey [Name], came across your   │
│  profile again today. Curious how things   │
│  have been unfolding since we last spoke?" │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

If more time has passed (months):
> *"Hey [Name], it's been a while. Saw something today that made me think of what you were working on. How's [their goal/project/business] going these days?"*

---

### STAGE 2, THE PAIN MIRROR

**Timing: After their reply to Stage 1**

When they respond, bring back their original pain point, but expand it with a fresh angle. Show that you remembered. Show that you've been thinking.

**Three paths based on their reply:**

**If the pain is still there:** Move directly to Stage 3.
> *"Last time you mentioned [their exact pain], sounds like that's still the situation? What's shifted since then, if anything?"*

**If the situation has changed:** Treat this like a new conversation starting from the Clarity stage. Ask about the new situation before doing anything else.

**If things are going well:** Celebrate it, genuinely. Then plant a seed.
> *"That's brilliant, really good to hear. Sounds like [what's working]. I'll be curious to see where it goes from here."*

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STAGE 2 · PAIN MIRROR                      │
│  Timing: After Stage 1 reply                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Message, personalised to their reply]    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### STAGE 3, THE CURIOSITY DROP

**Timing: After Stage 2, if original pain is confirmed**

Drop a single, specific insight that makes them lean forward. Not a pitch. A preview. The difference between *"I can help you"* and *"I recently helped someone in exactly your situation do X, want to hear how?"*

**The components:**
- Reference a specific outcome (real if possible, realistic if not)
- Make it directly relevant to their confirmed pain or situation
- Frame it as optional, you're offering, not pushing
- One soft question to invite the next step

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STAGE 3 · CURIOSITY DROP                   │
│  Timing: After Stage 2 reply                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Message]                                  │
│                                             │
│  Pattern: "Actually, I recently helped    │
│  [similar person] go from [their old        │
│  situation] to [specific outcome].          │
│  Want me to share what we did?"            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## ━━ NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES ━━

- **Never chase.** Re-entry energy is calm, curious, and completely without desperation. The moment it feels like chasing, rewrite it.
- **Never repeat yourself.** Each message must bring something new to the conversation, a new observation, a new question, a new angle.
- **Type A and Type B are different motions.** The energy, structure, and pacing are completely different. Never apply a Type B approach to a live thread.
- **Stage 1 of Type B has one job: a reply.** Not a qualified lead. Not a booked call. Just re-establish that a real human is on the other end.
- **Use their exact words.** If you know how they described their situation in the original conversation, use that language when you reference it. It signals that you genuinely listened.
- **A warm but vague reply is not a buying signal.** If they respond to Stage 1 with something like "things are good!" and nothing more, acknowledge it and wait before moving to Stage 2.

## Voice lock and quality gate (added v4.1.0)
Read references/voice-dna.md before writing. Write in the user's voice. No fallback.
Before delivering anything, run this gate:
- Zero em dashes anywhere.
- No banned words or AI patterns (references/ai-pattern-blacklist.md, references/human-writing-standards.md).
- Specific to this one prospect, not a template. If you could send it to anyone, rewrite it.
- Never invent a name, number, result, or detail. If you do not have it, ask or leave a clear placeholder.
- Final test: would the user say "I wrote that"? If not, rebuild.
