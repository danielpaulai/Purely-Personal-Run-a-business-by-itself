# by Daniel

## THE COPYWRITER PRINCIPLE

The rules in this file teach the WHY behind great LinkedIn posts.
They are not templates. They are the intelligence.

A template-filler produces posts that sound like posts.
A world-class caption writer produces posts that sound like a specific
human having a specific thought about a specific thing they know deeply.

Every post passes this test: "Could this post have been written by anyone in this niche?"
If yes, it is not specific enough. Rewrite it.

## THE POST STRUCTURE

Every post follows this arc. The structure serves the content, not the other way around.

**Line 1, The Hook**
Already written by the Hook Generator. This is the non-negotiable opener.
Never change it unless the client requests it.

**Lines 2–4, The Setup**
Context or tension. Earns the right to be read.
Not a preamble. Not "in this post I will share." The context that makes the body land harder.
1–3 short lines. Max 2 lines per paragraph anywhere in the post.

**Lines 5–end, The Body**
The value. The story, the list, the insight, or the framework.
Structure follows the intent:
- Educate → teach one thing specifically. Steps if needed. Numbered if logical.
- Authority → show a result, a case, or a methodology with specific detail.
- Entertain/Human → a scene, a confession, a real moment. No manufactured emotion.
- Convert → the problem, the gap, the offer. Simple. Direct. One ask.

**The Transition**
One line that bridges the body to the CTA.
Signals the shift. Natural. Not "so what does this mean for you?"

**The CTA**
One ask. One. Never "comment, share, and follow me."
Matched to the intent:
- Educate → Save this + repost if useful
- Authority → Drop a comment with [specific question]
- Entertain → Tell me your experience with [specific thing]
- Convert → Comment [keyword] or DM me [keyword]

## THE HARD WRITING RULES

**Rule 1, Max 2 lines per paragraph.**
On mobile, more than 2 lines becomes a wall. Walls are scrolled past.

**Rule 2, No em dashes.**
Em dashes are the most AI-recognisable punctuation pattern on LinkedIn.
Replace every em dash with a period and a new sentence, or a comma.

**Rule 3, No haze phrases.**
These phrases add words without adding meaning. Delete on sight.
✗ "What you can do is..."
✗ "It's important to note that..."
✗ "The thing is..."
✗ "At the end of the day..."
✗ "When it comes to..."
✗ "In today's world..."

**Rule 4, No contradiction parallels.**
✗ "It's not about X. It's about Y."
✗ "Stop doing X. Start doing Y."
These constructions are so overused they have become AI tells.
Replace with direct statements or concrete examples.

**Rule 5, Grade 3 vocabulary.**
Every sentence should be readable by a 9-year-old.
Industry terms the creator uses naturally: fine.
Long words where short words exist: replace.

**Rule 6, Sentence rhythm.**
Short. Then a slightly longer one for context. Then short again.
Never three long sentences in a row. Never five short ones that feel like a list.
The rhythm is what makes the post feel human.

**Rule 7, No bullet points inside text posts.**
Unless the format explicitly calls for a list (e.g. "7 things..."), no bullets in body text.
Bullets inside text posts break the reading flow and signal AI-generated content.

**Rule 8, No fake story openers.**
✗ "Picture this..." (nobody pictures it)
✗ "Imagine you're..." (condescending)
✗ "A few years ago, I had a client..." (too vague, name the client type and the specific situation)
✓ "The founder I was coaching in December had a problem I'd never seen before."

## LENGTH CALIBRATION

**Short (150–250 characters):**
One insight. One story beat. One bold claim.
Best for: opinions, confessions, social proof, quick authority signals.
No list. No structure. One clean thought.

**Medium (300–500 characters):**
One insight fully developed. A short story with a lesson.
Best for: education, experience, methodology reveals.
2–3 paragraph rhythm. Clear structure without feeling like an essay.

**Long (500–700 characters):**
A full story or a thorough framework.
Best for: high-value educational content, case studies, conversion posts.
Must justify the length, every paragraph earns its place.
Never long just to seem thorough.

## CTA RULES

**One ask per post. Always.**

| Intent | CTA type |
|--------|---------|
| Educate | "Save this + repost if useful" |
| Authority | Question that invites comment (topic-specific, not "what do you think?") |
| Entertain | "Tell me [specific related experience]" |
| Convert | "Comment [keyword]" or "DM me [keyword]" |

**Forbidden CTAs:**
✗ "Comment, like, and follow"
✗ "Let me know your thoughts" (too vague, nobody acts)
✗ "Follow for more content like this" (cheap, signals desperation)
✗ "Tag someone who needs to hear this" (manipulative engagement bait)
