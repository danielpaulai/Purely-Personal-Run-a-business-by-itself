## WHAT A CONTENT PILLAR ACTUALLY IS

A content pillar is not a topic. It is a strategic territory.

The difference:
- "Marketing" is a topic. Anyone in any niche could claim it.
- "Why most B2B founders market to everyone and close no one" is a pillar.
  It is opinionated. It is specific. It points to a real person with a real problem.

A great pillar does three things simultaneously:
1. Signals who you are and what you stand for
2. Attracts the exact person you want to work with
3. Repels everyone else, which is a feature, not a bug

## THE 5 PILLAR ARCHETYPES

Every strong content strategy contains a mix of these types.
No single archetype should dominate, the best pillars use 3–4 of these 5.

**Archetype 1, The Expertise Pillar**
What you know that your audience does not.
Specific, technical, and positions you as the person who has done the work.
Example logic: "I have [done X] for [N years/clients] and here is what nobody tells you about [topic]."
Best for: authority building, attracting serious buyers

**Archetype 2, The Experience Pillar**
What you have lived through that your audience is about to or currently facing.
Story-led. Confession-heavy. High trust-building.
Example logic: "I went through [specific challenge] and here is exactly what happened and what I did."
Best for: relatability, DM triggers, emotional connection

**Archetype 3, The Opinion Pillar**
What you believe that most people in your niche would not say publicly.
Contrarian but grounded, not for shock value, for genuine perspective.
Example logic: "Everyone says [X]. I disagree. Here is why, and here is what I do instead."
Best for: engagement, thought leadership, standing out

**Archetype 4, The Methodology Pillar**
Your specific system, process, or framework for getting a result.
Teachable. Structured. Makes abstract advice concrete.
Example logic: "Here is the exact [N]-step process I use to [achieve outcome] without [common pain]."
Best for: pre-selling your offer, positioning, authority

**Archetype 5, The Audience Pillar**
Content that speaks directly to the ICP's world, their problems, their language, their fears.
Not about you. About them. But filtered through your lens.
Example logic: "If you are a [specific person] trying to [specific goal], here is what is actually getting in your way."
Best for: attracting leads, building community, generating replies

## PILLAR NAMING RULES

Every pillar name must pass these three tests:

**Test 1, The Specificity Test**
Would this pillar name make sense for someone in a completely different niche?
If yes → too generic. Add specificity.
✗ "Mindset", belongs to everyone
✓ "The founder's mindset shift from employee thinking to CEO thinking"

**Test 2, The Audience Test**
Does someone in the ICP read this pillar name and think "that's about me"?
If not → it is about the creator, not the audience.
✗ "My Journey", about the creator
✓ "From zero to your first 5 clients, what nobody tells you", about the audience's journey

**Test 3, The Post Test**
Can you name 5 specific post ideas from this pillar right now?
If not → the pillar is too broad or too vague.
✗ "Growth", could mean anything
✓ "LinkedIn organic growth without paid ads", 5 posts immediately visible

## SUB-TOPIC RULES

Each sub-topic must feel like a post title someone would stop scrolling for.

**Every sub-topic must:**
- Be specific enough to write a post about today
- Contain a clear point of view or angle, not just a topic
- Reference the ICP's world, their language, their problem, their goal
- Not overlap with any other sub-topic in the same pillar

**Sub-topic formats that work:**
- The bold claim: "[Common belief] is why you're stuck"
- The confession: "What I learned after [specific experience]"
- The specific how-to: "How to [achieve specific outcome] without [common pain]"
- The contrast: "[Thing everyone does] vs [better approach]"
- The specific number: "[N] reasons why [specific thing] isn't working"
- The insider perspective: "What [type of client/expert] actually does when [situation]"

**Anti-patterns for sub-topics:**
✗ "Tips for better engagement", too vague
✗ "Why mindset matters", nothing specific to act on
✗ Two sub-topics that are essentially the same angle rephrased
✗ Sub-topics that could belong to any pillar, not anchored to this specific territory

## ANTI-PATTERNS FOR THE FULL PILLAR SET

**Overlap problem:**
If two pillars could share the same sub-topics, they are too close.
Each pillar must own a distinct territory. If there is even 30% overlap, merge or rename.

**Genericity problem:**
Pillars like "Personal Development", "Business Tips", "Leadership" without specificity
are not pillars, they are categories. They belong to everyone. They attract no one specific.

**Creator-centric problem:**
Every pillar being about the creator's journey and wins, with no content about the audience's world.
The ratio should be: 60% audience-centric, 40% creator-centric across the pillar set.

**Conversion-only problem:**
A pillar that is entirely about the offer, the service, or the result the creator delivers.
Social selling, not content. Converts nobody cold. Repels warm audiences.

## POST FORMAT MATCHING BY PILLAR TYPE

| Pillar Archetype | Best formats |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Expertise | List posts, framework posts, numbered carousels, cheatsheets |
| Experience | Story posts, confession posts, nurturing narratives |
| Opinion | Contrarian text posts, comparison carousels, debate starters |
| Methodology | Step-by-step carousels, process cheatsheets, tutorial posts |
| Audience | Pain-point posts, empathy posts, "are you doing this?" posts |
