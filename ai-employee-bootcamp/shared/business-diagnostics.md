# Business Diagnostics, AI CEO Brain
# Purely Personal · by Daniel Paul
# Read when someone describes a symptom and you need to diagnose the root cause
# before prescribing a solution.

---

## THE DIAGNOSTIC PRINCIPLE

Never prescribe before you diagnose.
The symptom is rarely the problem. The problem is the problem.

"I'm not getting leads" could be:
→ A positioning problem (wrong audience)
→ A content problem (not speaking their language)
→ A distribution problem (right content, nobody sees it)
→ A conversion problem (traffic exists, not converting)
→ A pipeline problem (not doing enough outreach)

Each has a different fix. Treating the wrong one wastes time and money.

## THE GROWTH CEILING DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Business is stuck at the same revenue level for 2+ months.

**Ask these in order:**

1. "What's your primary lead source right now?"
   → If they don't have one → MARKETING problem
   → If they do → continue

2. "How many sales conversations did you have last month?"
   → Under 10 → PIPELINE problem (volume, not conversion)
   → Over 10 → continue

3. "What's your close rate?"
   → Under 30% → SALES problem (pitch, trust, or fit)
   → Over 30% → continue

4. "What's your average deal size?"
   → Low → PRICING problem
   → Fair → continue

5. "What's your client retention?"
   → Under 6 months average → DELIVERY/RETENTION problem
   → Over 6 months → continue

6. If all of the above are healthy → CAPACITY problem (can't take more clients)
   → OPERATIONS/SYSTEMS problem

## THE REVENUE PLATEAU DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Revenue was growing, now flat.

**Root causes in order of likelihood:**

1. **Founder bottleneck**, growth stopped because owner is capacity-capped
   Signal: booked out, turning away work, tired
   Fix: hire, delegate, productise

2. **Offer fatigue**, existing clients not expanding, no new demand for same offer
   Signal: same clients, no referrals, no new inbound
   Fix: new offer tier, upsell path, or audience expansion

3. **Market saturation**, competing in a commoditised space
   Signal: price pressure, "I can get this cheaper elsewhere"
   Fix: repositioning, niche down, create new category

4. **Distribution dried up**, the channel that worked stopped working
   Signal: same effort, fewer results, no content or outreach changes
   Fix: new distribution channel, increase output on working channel

## THE "I'M BUSY BUT NOT PROFITABLE" DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Full schedule, stressed, but not seeing the financial results expected.

**Ask:**

1. "What's your average hourly effective rate?"
   (Total revenue ÷ total hours worked = effective hourly rate)
   → If under target → PRICING or SCOPE CREEP problem

2. "How much of your time is billable vs admin?"
   → Under 60% billable → SYSTEMS and DELEGATION problem

3. "Do you have any clients you know are under-paying relative to value?"
   → Yes → PRICING COURAGE problem

4. "What's your biggest time sink that doesn't directly generate revenue?"
   → Identify → ELIMINATE or DELEGATE

## THE "CONTENT ISN'T WORKING" DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Posting consistently, getting engagement, zero leads.

**The 5-part check:**

1. **Profile optimised?** Does your profile convert a visitor into a follower or booking?
   → No → Fix profile before more content

2. **ICP clarity?** Could your ideal client read one post and think "this is for me"?
   → No → Niche down, get more specific

3. **CTA in posts?** Does every 2nd–3rd post have a specific call to action?
   → No → Add CTA. Direct ask. Keyword.

4. **DM follow-up?** Are you DMing people who engage with your content?
   → No → This is where the leads are. DM engagers.

5. **Lead magnet?** Is there something free they can get that warms them up?
   → No → Build the lead magnet. Add it to CTA.

## THE TEAM PERFORMANCE DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Team isn't hitting targets, quality is slipping, culture feels off.

**Ask in order:**

1. "Do they know exactly what's expected of them this week?"
   → No → CLARITY problem (not performance problem)

2. "Do they have what they need to do the job?"
   → No → RESOURCES problem (tools, training, time)

3. "Do they care about the outcome?"
   → No → CULTURE or FIT problem

4. "Are they capable of doing it?"
   → No → SKILLS problem (training or wrong hire)

5. If all yes → MOTIVATION problem (systems, recognition, incentives)

**Danny's version:**
"Before you blame the team, ask yourself: did they know exactly what was expected?
Did they have everything they needed to do it?
Nine times out of ten, the answer to one of those is no."

## THE PRICING CONFIDENCE DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Keeps discounting, nervous to state prices, getting price objections.

**The root cause check:**

1. "Do you believe your service is worth what you charge?"
   → No → This is a belief problem. What result do you create? Price to that.

2. "Have you anchored the price to the value of the outcome before stating it?"
   → No → Fix the sequence. Surface the cost of the problem first.

3. "Are you attracting the right buyer?"
   → No → Wrong ICP. People who can't afford you aren't your clients.

4. "Do you have proof of the outcome?"
   → No → Add case studies and testimonials. Proof removes price resistance.

## THE CASHFLOW CRISIS DIAGNOSTIC

**Symptom:** Revenue looks okay but always short on cash.

**The fast check:**

1. "What's your payment terms?"
   → Net 30/60 → COLLECTIONS problem. Tighten terms or charge upfront.

2. "Do you have retainer/recurring revenue?"
   → No → REVENUE MODEL problem. No predictability = cash anxiety forever.

3. "What's your biggest monthly fixed cost?"
   → Identify → Is it justified? Is it generating revenue?

4. "Do you have 1 month of expenses in reserve?"
   → No → FINANCIAL RESILIENCE problem. Build the buffer before investing in growth.

**Danny's version:**
"You can't grow from a position of financial anxiety.
Every decision you make when you're worried about cash is a bad decision.
First thing: build the buffer. One month of expenses in the bank. Non-negotiable."
