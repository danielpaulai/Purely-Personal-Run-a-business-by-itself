# Facilitator Playbook · Build a Business That Runs By Itself

> The cross-cutting "how to deliver" doc. Read this once before Day 1.
> Method: Blair Singer Train-the-Trainer + T. Harv Eker MMI / Master
> Facilitator. Adapted for a 2-day, $49 entry, AI-CEO-upsell workshop
> running over Zoom.

---

## 1 · The Container (set it on minute 1 of Day 1)

The container is the agreement between you and the room about how the
two days will work. Set it explicitly. Refer back to it whenever energy
drops or someone resists.

**The 5 agreements (slide 3 of Day 1):**

1. **Be here.** Camera on. Phone face down. One screen for Claude, one for me.
2. **Be honest.** This works only if you write your real numbers, real customers, real fears into BUSINESS-BRAIN.md. Lie to the brain, the brain lies to you.
3. **Participate aloud.** When I ask a question, you answer in chat or unmute. Lurkers don't transform.
4. **Implement live.** You build YOUR business while I teach. Not after. Not "later." Now.
5. **Trust the process.** It will feel weird. Some hours will feel slow. By Sunday at 5pm, you'll have a business that runs without you. Stay.

**Get verbal commitment:** "If that's the deal, type YES in chat." Wait
until ~80% have typed it. Anything less, you re-set the container.

## 2 · State Management (the engine of the whole workshop)

Blair Singer rule: *if your students aren't getting results, you're not
a great trainer.* You are not a teacher. You are a **state engineer.**

**The 7 state moves you'll use 50+ times across the weekend:**

| Move | When to use | What to say |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **STAND UP** | Energy dip · 7+ min on one slide · after a heavy concept | "Everyone stand up. Right now. Both feet on the floor." |
| **HANDS UP** | Trial close · checking comprehension | "Hands up if this makes sense. Hands up if you'd run this Monday morning." |
| **TURN TO YOUR PARTNER** (chat partner / breakout) | After every concept block | "In your breakout, in 60 seconds, teach this back to your partner. GO." |
| **WRITE IT DOWN** | After each engine | "Pick up your notebook. Write the ONE thing you're going to do Monday morning." |
| **CLAP IT IN** | Transition between blocks | "On 3, everyone clap and shout YES. 1, 2, 3 — YES!" |
| **DEEP BREATH** | After a long content stretch | "Hand on your chest. Breathe in for 4. Hold 4. Out for 4." |
| **RECOMMIT** | Mid-Day-1, mid-Day-2 | "Look at me. Do you still want a business that runs by itself? Type YES." |

Use one every **7-10 minutes maximum.** Set a timer if you have to.

## 3 · The Energy Curve (T. Harv's 3-3-3-3-3 rule)

Three minutes of high energy. Three of content. Three of high. Three of
content. Three of high. Repeat.

**Practical:** never lecture for more than 7 minutes without an
exercise, story, or state change. If you catch yourself on slide 12
without an interaction, you've lost them. State change immediately.

**Day-level energy curve:**

```
Day 1:  HIGH (open) → MED (foundation) → HIGH (lunch close) →
        MED (sales) → HIGH (routines reveal) → MED (preview) →
        HIGH (commit + Day 2 hook)

Day 2:  HIGH (wins share) → MED (ops/cash) → HIGH (lunch close) →
        MED (leadership) → PEAK (AI CEO reveal) → HIGH (the sell) →
        PEAK (close) → MED (Q&A, integration)
```

The PEAK on Day 2 is the AI CEO pitch. Everything before that is
calibrated to land that peak.

## 4 · The 4 Learning Styles (T. Harv: cover all 4 in every block)

Every concept block of ~75 minutes must have:

1. **Auditory** · you say it · they hear it
2. **Visual** · they see it on slide · you draw / point / show output
3. **Kinesthetic** · they DO it · type the prompt · paste it · run it
4. **Digital** · they discuss / debate / teach back / ask why

If you only auditory + visual, you've lost 50% of the room. Every block
must have a "now type this in your terminal" moment. Every block must
have a pair-share or chat discussion.

## 5 · Pre-Framing the Sell (the most important rule in the doc)

The AI CEO sell on Day 2 at 4:15pm only works if you **pre-framed** it
on Day 1. **Do not surprise them with a pitch.**

**Pre-frame moments to plant on Day 1:**

| Moment | What to say |
|--------|-------------|
| **Opening (slide 6)** | "By the end of Sunday, you'll have a business that runs without you. Some of you will want to go further. We'll talk about that on Sunday afternoon. For now, focus on the 5 engines." |
| **End of Block 4 (routines)** | "These 6 routines are your weekly autopilot. There's a 7th layer above this — an orchestration layer that runs the orchestrator — but that's Sunday afternoon. Today, master these 6." |
| **Day 1 close (slide 48)** | "Tomorrow we'll cover the last 3 engines, then I'll show you the AI CEO. The AI CEO is what you build when you've outgrown doing this yourself. Some of you will be ready for it Sunday. Some of you won't. Both are fine." |

**The phrase "AI CEO" must be spoken at least 4 times on Day 1 without
a pitch attached.** Familiarity. Curiosity. No pressure.

## 6 · The Trial Close (use 30+ times across the weekend)

Every concept block ends with a hands-up question. This builds the
"yes ladder" T. Harv talks about.

**Trial closes by block:**

- After /marketing-engine: "Hands up if you'd run this every Monday."
- After /sales-engine: "Hands up if you'd let Claude write your follow-ups for you."
- After 6-routines: "Hands up if you can see your Monday-Sunday running while you sleep."
- After /operations-engine: "Hands up if your inbox could be empty by 9am."
- After /cash-engine: "Hands up if you want to see your real revenue every Friday without opening Stripe."
- After /leadership-engine: "Hands up if you want to wake up to a 60-second brief on your whole business."

**Each "yes" is a deposit.** The pitch on Day 2 is the withdrawal.

## 8 · Handling Resistance

When someone says *"this won't work for me"* or *"my business is different"*:

1. **Don't argue.** Argue back = lose the room.
2. **Acknowledge, redirect to a peer.** "Great question. <name in room with similar biz> — how would you adapt this?"
3. **Use the brain.** "Open your BUSINESS-BRAIN.md. What does it say about your offer? What does it say about your customer? Now re-run the prompt with that context."

Most "this won't work" objections are because the BRAIN is empty.

## 9 · Selling Without Being Salesy (Day 2 · 4:15pm)

Three rules that keep the pitch human:

1. **Don't pitch from a slide deck.** Pitch eye-to-eye. Camera on, no slides for the first 10 minutes of the pitch. Story → vision → bridge → offer.
2. **Earn the right.** You earned the right to pitch by giving them 2 days of real value. Acknowledge it: "I want to ask for 60 minutes of your attention. You've earned 11 hours of mine. Fair trade?"
3. **One offer, two tiers, one deadline.** Don't confuse them. Don't add bonuses mid-pitch. The stack is set. The price is set. The deadline is by end of Sunday.

See `SALES-SEQUENCE.md` for the full pitch script.

## 11 · The 80/20 of "did I deliver"

If you catch yourself drifting into lecture mode, ask: *"Are they
typing right now?"* If no one in the room is typing into Claude Code
right now, you're not training — you're talking. Pause. Hand it back.

The single most-used phrase in the workshop should be:

> "Pause. Open Claude Code. Run this prompt right now. Tell me what
> you got."

Use it after every concept. Always.
