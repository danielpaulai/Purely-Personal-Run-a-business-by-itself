# Sales Sequence · The AI CEO Pitch · Day 2 · 16:15 - 17:15

> 60 minutes. The most important hour of the workshop.
> Method: T. Harv Eker's 7 P's (Promise · Provoke · Prove · Punch ·
> Pivot · Practice · Persuade) · adapted for a $1,999 / $2,999 offer
> over Zoom · cohort of 30.

**Economics:**
- Workshop: 30 × $49 = $1,470
- Target conversion: 10-15% = 3-5 buyers
- If 3 buyers split as (2 × $1,999) + (1 × $2,999) = $6,997
- If 5 buyers split as (3 × $1,999) + (2 × $2,999) = $11,995
- **Expected upsell revenue: $7K-$12K**
- Total weekend: ~$8.5K-$13.5K from 30 people

## VERBATIM PITCH SCRIPT

### 16:15 · 1 · PROMISE (3 minutes · slide 34 · YOU only · no slide visible)

> "I want to ask for 60 minutes of your attention.
>
> You've earned 11 hours of mine.
>
> What I'm about to share with you is what I built for myself when I
> realised the engines weren't enough. When I realised running the
> engines was still my job. When I wanted to stop being the operator
> and become the architect.
>
> What I'm going to show you, and how to get it, is the AI CEO
> program. By the end of this hour, you'll know exactly what it is,
> who it's for, who it's NOT for, and whether it's right for you. No
> pressure. Just clarity.
>
> If you're done after this hour and you decide AI CEO isn't for you ·
> beautiful. You walk out with what you came for. The 5 engines. The
> 6 routines. The 1 brain. That alone changes your business. Forever.
>
> But some of you · maybe 4 or 5 in this room · are going to look at
> what I share next and say 'that's the next layer for me.' This hour
> is for you."

**Tone:** slow, eye-contact, no rushing. Camera tight.

---

### 16:18 · 2 · PROVOKE (5 minutes · still no slide · YOU only)

> "Let me tell you what's about to happen on Monday morning if you do
> nothing.
>
> You're going to wake up. You're going to feel proud. You'll run your
> Monday Content Drop. The 3 posts will land in Notion. You'll smile.
>
> Then Tuesday: lead research will fire. Wednesday: newsletter draft.
> Thursday: meeting prep. By Friday, your week will have hummed.
>
> But here's what's going to bother you by Sunday night.
>
> You'll look at the week that just ran. You'll see the 6 routines
> all fired. You'll see the outputs. And you'll think:
>
> 'Did I run the right routines this week? Should I have pushed sales
> instead of marketing? Should I have postponed the newsletter
> because of the launch I'm doing? Should I have gone harder on
> outreach because the pipeline's thin?'
>
> The engines don't ask those questions. They just run.
>
> And right now · the only person who can answer those questions ·
> is you.
>
> That's fine for week 1. It's fine for week 4. But by week 12, you'll
> be making the same Sunday-night decisions you were making before
> this workshop. The execution got delegated. The decisions didn't.
>
> That's the gap. That's what AI CEO closes."

**Tone:** measured. This is the pain. Don't oversell. Let it sit.

---

### 16:23 · 3 · PROVE (4 minutes · still no slide · YOU only)

> "I built AI CEO for myself 6 months ago. I'll show you what it did.
>
> Last quarter I ran 3 product launches. Two of them I'd planned. The
> third · AI CEO told me to run it on a Tuesday in March because
> traffic patterns and pipeline math said the opening was there. I
> ignored it for a week. Then I ran it. It became my biggest revenue
> month of the year.
>
> I'm not telling you this to brag. I'm telling you this to make a
> point. Six months ago, I would have missed that decision. I was the
> bottleneck. AI CEO removed me from the decision-making loop on
> tactical questions. It freed me up to make the strategic ones.
>
> The engines run the business. AI CEO runs the engines. You run the
> AI CEO. That's the chain of command of a business that runs without
> you · for real."

**Tone:** real story · specifics · don't sound polished

## The math one more time

If 4 of 30 buy at average $2,300 (mix of tiers), you net $9,200 from
the upsell + $1,470 entry = **$10,670 weekend revenue**.

If 5 of 30 buy at average $2,500, you net $12,500 + $1,470 =
**$13,970 weekend revenue**.

If you sell ZERO upsell, you ran a great $1,470 weekend that built
your reputation, your alumni network, and the foundation for the next
cohort. Either way · you win.

The pitch is not the goal. **The cohort is the goal.** Pitch
accordingly.

## One last thing

The single biggest mistake first-time facilitators make in the close:
**they apologize for the price.** Don't.

If you find yourself saying "I know this is a lot" or "I get it,
$2,999 is real money" · stop. The price is the price. The value is
$12,000+. The deal is the deal. Stand in it. Anyone who feels you
flinch will not buy.

Your AI CEO is worth $1,999 and $2,999. Stand in it. Then sell.
