# AI CEO · The Upsell Pitch (Day 2 · 17:15–18:45)
## 90 minutes · 12 slides · designed for 15–25% conversion

This is the pitch you give in Day 2 between Acts 8 and 10. It's the entire
reason the workshop exists at this price point. Done well: 4–6 of 25 attendees
say YES, and you've added $20–30k in 90 minutes of speaking.

**Done badly** (preachy / pressure-y / countdown-y): you lose trust and the
whole cohort feels manipulated. So you build it the **Eker / Singer way**:
state the gap, show the path, name who it's for, name who it's NOT for, name
the price clearly, then **stop talking**. Let them choose.

---

## The Frame · before you open the deck

> "Some of you came here with a question you didn't ask. The question is:
> 'Danny, will I actually do this on Monday?'
>
> The honest answer is: **70% of you will.** You're motivated, you've built
> something, you'll publish a post or two and it'll change something.
>
> But **30% of you** want something more than a workshop. You want to vanish
> from the day-to-day. You want the autopilot to build itself further. You want
> to wake up in 90 days and your business runs without you logging in.
>
> The next 60 minutes is for those 30%. If that's not you, this is also useful
> to listen to — because it shows you what's possible if you change your mind
> later. But you can also use this hour to start drafting your Monday post.
> No judgement either way."

That permission slip is critical. Eker rule: **never make people feel guilty
for not buying.** They opt in willingly or they don't, and you stay clean.

## Q&A handling · the 6 questions you'll get

These are the questions every cohort asks. Memorise the answers.

### Q1 · "What if I don't have time?"

> "Same answer for every program: the workshop you just took saves you 8
> hours a week if you actually run the routines. AI CEO costs you 3 hours
> a week. So if you implement, you NET +5 hours/week back. The question
> isn't 'do I have time' — it's 'will I commit to the implementation.'"

### Q2 · "Can I do it later?"

> "Yes. The workshop you took today is enough for 70% of people. AI CEO
> exists for the 30% who want acceleration. If that's not you right now, no
> problem — keep running the routines. Come back to AI CEO in 6 months if
> you want it."

### Q3 · "What if my business is too small?"

> "If you have 1 paying customer, your business is the right size. If you
> have ZERO, do the workshop, run the routines, get your first customer
> from the routines, THEN apply. The right time is after first revenue."

### Q4 · "How is this different from a course?"

> "A course is one-way. You watch, you ask in a forum, you maybe do the
> work. AI CEO is engineering — I work with you, on YOUR business, to build
> agents specific to YOUR offer. There's no 'lesson 4 video.' Every call
> is your business."

### Q5 · "Can I do payment plans?"

> "Yes. 3 monthly payments at the same total. No interest. We handle the
> rest if you finish all 3 successfully."

### Q6 · "What if I apply and you reject me?"

> "I won't reject you for being early. I might suggest 'do the workshop
> twice first' if you're still pre-revenue. That's me protecting your money,
> not gatekeeping. The form has a no-pressure 'tell me your context' field —
> use it."

## After the pitch · what to do for next 60 min

After Slide U.11, **stop talking**. Open chat. Take questions. Answer
honestly. **Do not push.** Eker rule: the buyer should feel that **you
weren't selling — you were just answering questions.** That's the
psychological frame that makes the buy feel safe.

If you sense someone's a YES but holding back · DM them privately:
> "Hey [name], saw your engagement today — really strong. If you've got
> a specific question about the program before you decide, my DMs are
> open. No pressure either way."

If you sense someone's a NO · don't push:
> "Glad the workshop landed. Let me know if you want a Slack invite for
> the cohort group. AI CEO will be back in Q3."

## The application form · what it should ask

Build a Tally / Typeform with these fields:

1. Name + email
2. Current monthly revenue (range)
3. Status: ESSENTIAL or PRO
4. **What's the ONE thing you'd hand to your AI CEO if you could?**
5. **What would change in your life if you got 30 hours back per week?**
6. Calendar link (Calendly) for the application call

Questions 4 and 5 are the **diagnostic** questions. The answers tell you who's
ready and who needs the workshop again first.

## Pricing math (your case for the prices above)

Why $4,997?
- Equivalent of $1,665/month for 3 months
- The agents you build save 30 hours/week minimum
- At even $40/hour internal rate · that's $4,800/month saved · pays for itself month 1

Why $9,997?
- Custom agent built for them = $10–20k if they hired a freelancer
- Plus everything in Essential
- Math: they're getting the freelance agent essentially included

Why 8 seats?
- More than 8 = you can't 1:1 properly
- Less than 8 = doesn't justify the time investment
- 8 × $4,997 = $40k for 90 days (assume half PRO, half Essential = ~$60k)
