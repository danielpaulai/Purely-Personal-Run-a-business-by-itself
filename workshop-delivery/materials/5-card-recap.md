# 5-Card Recap · Both Days
## Print these as 5 actual physical cards · hold them up · OR show as 5 slides · read aloud · 3× each

The 5-card recap is the **single most important Eker move** in the entire
workshop. Read each card aloud. Group says it back. **Three times each.**

Yes, three times. Yes, on Zoom. Yes, with cameras on. The repetition is the
point. The first time it's awkward. The third time it's anchored. By Tuesday
the words are still in their head.

---

the brain.

What it anchors: Marketing engine works. The output is real. They're writing

of an AI-powered company.

## How to deliver the recap (script)

```
"OK, last 10 minutes of Day 1.

Stand up. Hand on your heart.

I'm going to hold up 5 cards. After each one, you say it back to me.
3 times.

You will feel weird the first time. By the third time, you won't.
Trust me.

Card 1: My voice is in the file.
[wait. group says it.]
My voice is in the file.
[wait. group says it.]
My voice is in the file.
[wait. group says it. louder this time.]

Now Card 2..."
```

Same pattern, all 5 cards, both days.

---

## Why this works (the science)

1. **Repetition consolidates memory.** Saying something 3× gets it past the
   short-term memory filter into long-term encoding.

2. **Standing + hand on heart = state change.** Their body is in a different
   position than passive watching, so the brain encodes the words differently.

3. **Public declaration creates commitment.** They said it on Zoom in front of
   24 other people. The social cost of NOT acting on it just went up.

4. **Identity language ("I AM") rewires self-concept.** "I have a business" is
   passive. "I am the CEO of my AI company" is identity. Identity drives action.

5. **The first awkwardness is the cost.** By the third repetition, the
   awkwardness has been processed. What remains is just the words. Anchored.

---

## What to do AFTER the recap

After the 5-card chant:
1. Give them 30 seconds of silence to feel it
2. Then ONE sentence from you: "Tomorrow we close the loop."
3. Then end the day. Don't add anything else. Don't dilute the moment.

End the recording. End the meeting. They sit with the cards in their head
all night. That's the point.
