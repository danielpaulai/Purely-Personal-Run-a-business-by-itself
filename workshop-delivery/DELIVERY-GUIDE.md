## Part 1 · Why it works (the methodology, in one page)

Most people teach by **dumping content**. That doesn't change behaviour. Blair
Singer and Harv Eker both built billion-dollar training businesses on the same
6 rules:

1. **State first, content second.** Manage their state every 20 minutes. If
   they're tired, no content lands. Use music, standing, partner shares, breath.
2. **Ask before you tell.** Every new concept opens with a question they can
   answer. The brain remembers what it produced, not what it received.
3. **Anchor success early.** Give the first win in the first 90 minutes. They
   should be able to point to one concrete thing they built before lunch.
4. **Demonstrate → Practice → Coach.** You show, they do, you walk and correct.
   Never lecture for >12 minutes without an exercise.
5. **Repeat the magic 7×.** Every key idea gets said 7 different ways: story,
   metaphor, demo, exercise, partner share, recap card, declaration.
6. **Public commitment.** Everything important gets written, said, and shared
   with a partner. The body remembers what the body did.

The upsell to AI CEO at the end of Day 2 is **not a pitch**. It's a frame:
"You've built the autopilot. Some of you want to take this further — here's
what that looks like." The selling has happened all weekend by giving real
value publicly.

## Part 4 · The Energy System (master facilitator level)

Energy on a Zoom workshop dies faster than in-person. You have to compensate.
Plan a state-change every 20–25 minutes:

### State-change toolbox (rotate these)

1. **Standing break** · "Everyone stand. Three breaths. Sit." · 30 sec
2. **Partner share** · "Turn to your camera. Find a partner in chat. Share for
   90 sec each." · 3 min
3. **Chat blast** · "In one word, what just clicked? Drop it in chat now." · 30 sec
4. **Chant / declaration** · "Say with me out loud: 'I am the CEO of my AI company.'"
   Done 3× through the workshop, builds the identity flip.
5. **Hot seat** · pull one volunteer to share their screen, walk through their
   work, you coach live. The other 24 people learn from watching. Do this 4×
   across the 2 days.
6. **Music** · 60 sec of upbeat music between acts. Pre-cue Spotify/Apple Music.
7. **Hand on heart** · for emotional moments (Build My Brain, AI CEO offer).
   Slows everyone down, makes it land.
8. **Public win** · "Whoever just shipped — type 'SHIPPED' in chat." Read 3 out
   loud, celebrate by name.

### The 90-second story rule

Every story you tell **must be ≤ 90 seconds**. Longer stories lose them. Have
6 prepared stories at 90 seconds each:

1. **Origin story** · why you built this (Day 1 open · 90 sec)
2. **The first AI-drafted post that hit 14k views** (Act 3 · 60 sec)
3. **Cold-DMing → BANT-scored leads in your sleep** (Act 4 · 90 sec)
4. **The Sunday night you realised your ops ran without you** (Act 6 · 90 sec)
5. **The first month Stripe revenue came in while you were on holiday** (Act 7 · 60 sec)
6. **The 'AI CEO' moment** — when the autopilot built the autopilot (Act 9 · 90 sec)

Rehearse each one out loud. Record yourself on phone. Cut anything > 90 seconds.

## Part 5 · The Permission Slip (read aloud at 11:05 Day 1)

Pull this from `materials/permission-slip.md`. Read it slowly. Pause for
acknowledgement.

> "Over the next two days, I might say things that disturb you. I might
> challenge a story you've been telling yourself. I might push you to ship
> something you've been hiding from. **Are you willing to be uncomfortable
> for two days, in exchange for a business that runs without you?**
>
> Say in chat: YES."

Wait for chat to fill with YES. Don't move on until ≥ 80% of the room has typed it.

This is the Blair Singer move: **anchor permission early, before content**. It
gives you the right to push them later without them resisting.

## Part 6 · The 5-Card Recap (Day 1 close + Day 2 close)

Two times per day, you do a **5-card recap**. Hold up an imaginary card (or
literally show 5 slides), name each one, have the group say it back. This is
the Harv Eker move: **the brain anchors what it repeats out loud**.

### Day 1 cards (recite at 20:45)
1. **"My voice is in the file"** — BUSINESS-BRAIN.md is the lever
2. **"Three posts a week, written by AI, in my voice"** — Marketing engine works
3. **"Ten leads a week, BANT scored, while I sleep"** — Sales engine works
4. **"Six routines, six weekday slots"** — the rhythm is set
5. **"I am the CEO of my AI company"** — the identity flip

After you say each one, group says it back. **Three times.** Yes, three times.
First time it's awkward. Third time it's anchored.

### Day 2 cards (recite at 20:45)
1. **"My ops run without me"** — Operations engine
2. **"My revenue is forecast"** — Cash engine
3. **"My brain runs all 5 engines"** — Leadership engine
4. **"I shipped today, in this room, on Zoom"** — Action proof
5. **"I am the CEO of my AI company"** — Identity locked in

Pre-pick **4 volunteers** on Friday May 1. Email them: "I'd like to put you on
the hot seat in one of these 4 moments. Are you in?" Get their YES in writing.

| Hot seat | When | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| **Brain Hot Seat** | Day 1 · 13:30 (mid Act 2) | Pull volunteer to share screen, walk through their /build-my-brain, you coach the answers live. The other 24 watch + learn. 15 min. |
| **Marketing Hot Seat** | Day 1 · 16:00 (mid Act 3) | Volunteer shows their 3 drafted posts. Group + you score. Volunteer ships one to LinkedIn live. 12 min. |
| **Sales Hot Seat** | Day 1 · 18:00 (mid Act 4) | Volunteer pastes a real prospect URL. Live Apify scrape + BANT score. They send the outreach in the room. 15 min. |

The Hot Seat is where the magic happens. Everyone else feels seen because they
recognise their own struggle in the volunteer. That's the moment they decide
to invest in themselves further. **Hot seats sell the upsell.**

## Part 8 · The Upsell to AI CEO (see `ai-ceo-pitch.md` for the full pitch)

The upsell at Day 2 · 17:15–18:45 is **not** a sales pitch with a countdown
timer. It's a **frame shift**:

- "You've built the autopilot. That's huge. That's enough for most of you."
- "But for some of you, the question is now: **what if the autopilot built
  the autopilot?**"
- "That's what AI CEO is. It's a 90-day program where I work with 8 people
  privately to build their autonomous AI CEO — the agent that runs the 5
  engines without you logging in."

Pricing logic (suggest, don't dictate — you choose):
- **Workshop (this weekend)** = $497 one-time (you've already sold)
- **AI CEO Program** = $4,997 (8 people · 90 days · weekly 1:1 + group + private Slack)
- **Lifetime · AI CEO Pro** = $9,997 (same as above + Danny builds 1 custom agent for them)

10× price jump from workshop to AI CEO is the standard ladder. People who buy
the $497 workshop and get value will absolutely consider $4,997 IF you've
demonstrated 10× more value during the workshop. You've spent 20 hours showing
them what's possible — the upsell is "I'll build it WITH you."

**Conversion target:** 15–25% of the room (4–6 people from a cohort of 25).
Anything above 10% is a successful upsell day.

See `ai-ceo-pitch.md` for the slide-by-slide upsell deck.

## Part 10 · If something goes wrong

### "My install isn't working"
Open the Workshop MCP. Run `quick_install_check` on their config. The output is
a punch list. Walk them through it. Have a co-pilot/assistant moderating the
chat to handle these — you stay on the main stage.

### "I don't have a business yet"
That's fine. Have them use a hypothetical ICP for the first day. By Day 2 most
people have decided what their offer would be. The workshop becomes the
business validation.

### "Zoom freezes"
You have Google Meet as backup. Switch immediately. Don't try to debug Zoom
mid-flow. Lose 5 min, switch, continue.

### "Energy is dying mid-afternoon"
Two moves:
1. **Music + stand-up break** for 60 seconds (have Spotify "Workshop Energy"
   playlist queued up)
2. **Public win shout-out** — read out 3 wins from chat, name people, celebrate

### "Someone is dominating the chat / mic"
Privately DM them: "I love your energy. Hold the next two questions for the
Q&A — I want others to chime in too." Almost always works. If not, mute.

### "Someone is publicly negative / sceptical"
Don't argue. Acknowledge: "Totally get the scepticism. Here's a hot seat for
you in 30 minutes — let's see what happens when you try it yourself." Either
they convert or they self-eject. Either way, the room watches you stay grounded.
