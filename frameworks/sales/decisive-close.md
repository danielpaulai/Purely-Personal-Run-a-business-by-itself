# The Decisive Close · How to Ask for the YES

> The single most-skipped move in modern sales. After all the
> qualification, all the value-stack, all the objection handling ·
> 90% of sellers fail to actually ASK for the deal. The ones who ask
> close 3-5x more.

---

## The 4 levels of close

```
LEVEL 1 · WIMP CLOSE     · "What do you want to do?"
LEVEL 2 · SOFT CLOSE     · "Does this feel like a fit?"
LEVEL 3 · DECISIVE CLOSE · "Are you in or out?"
LEVEL 4 · ASSUMPTIVE     · "I'll send the link · payment now or in
                            an hour?"
```

Most sellers default to Level 1 or 2. Top sellers run Level 3 and 4.
The difference: the prospect is FORCED to give a clear answer · which
shortens the cycle and removes ghost-deals.

---

## When to use which level

| Level | Use when | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (Wimp) | Never | Always · low close rate |
| 2 (Soft) | Top of pipeline · early discovery | Low · leaves deal in limbo |
| 3 (Decisive) | After qualification + value stack | Moderate · forces decision |
| 4 (Assumptive) | High-fit prospect · you've earned it | High · feels pushy if mistimed |

**Default for the AI CEO upsell on Day 2: Level 3.** The prospects
have given you 4 hours of attention. You earned the right to ask for
a clear answer.

## The Level 3 Decisive Close · the script

After the value stack and risk reversal:

```
"Everyone stand up. Right now. Both feet on the floor. Hand on heart.

If you can see how this would work for YOU · stand up.

Not for your friend. Not for the version of you in 2 years.
For YOU. Today. The business you built this weekend.

If you can see it · stand up."
```

Then **PAUSE**. Don't speak for 5-10 seconds. Let the silence work.

The standing-up move:
- Forces a physical decision (not just mental)
- Public commitment (the rest of the room sees)
- Self-selection (the standing ones are buyers)

After they stand:

```
"Beautiful. Look around the room. The people standing are who the
next 30 days are for.

If you're standing · I'm asking you to do ONE of two things in the
next 5 minutes:

ONE · click the link. Tier 1 or Tier 2. Lock yourself in. Done.

TWO · DM me right now. ONE message. 'I'm in but I have a question.'
I'll DM you back inside the next 30 minutes. Question answered.
Decision made.

If you're sitting · also beautiful. The 5 engines and 6 routines
are yours. You walk out with what you came for. We'll see each
other in the alumni Slack."
```

---

## Why this works (the psychology)

### 1 · Physical commitment

Asking them to STAND moves the decision out of their head and into
their body. Bodies don't lie · their decision is visible.

### 2 · Public commitment

When they stand, they commit publicly. Public commitments are 4-5x
more likely to follow through (Cialdini's consistency principle).

### 3 · Permission to NOT buy

The line "if you're sitting · also beautiful" releases pressure. It
reframes "no" as a valid choice · which paradoxically makes more
people say yes (because they're not being pressured).

### 4 · Two-track close

"Click the link OR DM me" gives the wavering buyer a path that's
NOT a hard yes/no. It captures buyers who need 30 more minutes of
conversation · without losing them to "I'll think about it."

## The decisive close · 1-on-1 version

For sales calls (not group settings), use the same logic without the
physical stand-up:

```
"Based on everything we talked about · the situation, the cost, the
fit · this seems like a clear yes to me. Does it feel like a clear
yes to you?"
```

Then PAUSE. Don't fill the silence.

Their answer is one of three:

### a · "Yes"
Move to logistics. "Beautiful. Let me send you the link."

### b · "No"
Ask why. "Got it · what's making it not feel like a yes?" Listen.
Address. (See objection-handling.md.)

### c · "Maybe / I need to think"
Run the TIME objection script (objection-handling.md).

## The Level 4 Assumptive Close

For high-fit prospects who've signaled clear interest:

```
"I'll send you both links. Tier 1 and Tier 2. Most founders in your
position go Tier 2 because of the 1:1 onboarding. Want me to lock
that in now or do you need 30 minutes?"
```

This skips the "yes/no" question and goes straight to "WHICH tier
and WHEN." Use only when the prospect has already given clear yes
signals · using it on a cold prospect feels pushy.

---

## What NOT to do (closing mistakes)

### 1 · Closing without the stack

If you haven't presented the bonus stack and risk reversal, the close
is premature. The buyer feels pressured to decide on incomplete info.

**Fix:** ALWAYS present stack → risk reversal → THEN close.

### 2 · Closing too early

If the prospect hasn't named the cost of inaction (NEPQ Stage 2),
they don't have the emotional reason to buy. Closing here gets a
"let me think about it" 90% of the time.

**Fix:** Run NEPQ qualification THOROUGHLY. Don't close until
they've articulated the consequences themselves.

### 3 · Apologizing for the price

The MOST common close-killer:

- "I know it's a lot..."
- "I get that this is real money..."
- "It might seem expensive but..."

Stop. The price is the price. The value is real. If you flinch · they
will. Stand in the offer.

**Fix:** when you state the price, state it FLAT. No qualifier. No
softener. "$1,999. Both links in chat. Doors close at 13:00."

### 4 · Filling the silence

After asking the closing question, SHUT UP. Most sellers fill the
silence within 5 seconds. Top sellers wait 30 seconds.

The silence is doing the work. Whoever speaks first loses.

**Fix:** physically count to 30 in your head after asking. Then
30 more if needed.

### 5 · Multiple CTAs

"You can buy now, or book a discovery call, or download the brochure
first."

Three CTAs = no CTA. Pick ONE · or at most TWO with a clear
hierarchy.

**Fix:** for the AI CEO close, the two CTAs are "click" or "DM."
Both lead to the same place (the deal). Anything else is noise.

## Closing trial-questions (use throughout the call)

Don't just close at the end. Close-test throughout. Each YES is a
deposit. By the time you ask for the close, the prospect has said
yes 6+ times.

**Trial close questions:**

- "Does that make sense?"
- "Is this the kind of outcome you're looking for?"
- "Does that match what you've been thinking?"
- "Hands up if you'd run this every Monday."
- "If we could solve [X], would you want to see how?"
- "Does this feel like the right kind of fit?"

Drop one of these every 5-7 minutes during the call. Build the yes
ladder.

---

## After the YES · what to do

The deal isn't closed when they say yes. It's closed when they
PAY.

Immediately after the verbal yes:

1. **Lock the moment.** "Beautiful. Let me send the link right now ·
   I want to make sure you can act while it's fresh."
2. **Send the link.** Pin in chat. Drop in DM.
3. **Wait for confirmation.** Don't end the call until you see the
   payment confirmation.
4. **Welcome them.** "Welcome to the cohort. First call is Tuesday
   at [time]. I'll send you the calendar invite right after this."

If they say "I'll do it tonight" · 50%+ never do. Get the click
WHILE they're on the call.

## The 1-line closer

If you only memorize ONE close line for the rest of your career,
make it this one:

> "If you can see it working for you · stand up. If you can't · it's
> not for you. Both are fine."

That single sentence · delivered with eye contact and a 5-second
pause after · closes more deals than 10 hours of pitch refinement.
