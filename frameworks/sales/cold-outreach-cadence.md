# Cold Outreach Cadence · 3-Step + 7-Touch

> Most cold outreach fails because the seller stops at message #1.
> The math: it takes 5-7 touches to convert a cold prospect.
> Here are the two cadences that work.

---

## The 3-step cadence (default · for solopreneurs)

Lighter touch. Faster cycle. Works for under-$5k offers.

```
TOUCH 1 · DAY 0   · Personalized DM        · "I noticed → I help → curious"
TOUCH 2 · DAY 3   · Value-first follow-up  · share a relevant case study or insight
TOUCH 3 · DAY 7   · Polite breakup         · "Closing the loop on this"
```

### Touch 1 · The opener (Day 0)

Compressed NEPQ in 4 sentences. ~60 words.

```
Sentence 1 · Specific observation about THEM (proof you read their work)
Sentence 2 · Reflect what's likely not working (problem you solve)
Sentence 3 · How you help (one specific way)
Sentence 4 · Soft CTA (15-min walk-through? or DM exchange?)
```

**Worked example:**

> Hey [name], saw your post about [specific topic] yesterday · the
> bit about [specific detail] resonated. Most founders at your stage
> hit a wall when [specific common pain at their stage]. I help
> founders cross that wall in 30 days without [hated method]. Open
> to a 15-min walk-through this week?

**The "I noticed" rule:** Sentence 1 MUST prove you actually read
their content. Generic openers ("I love your work!") trigger the
spam filter in their head. Specific observations don't.

---

### Touch 2 · Value-first (Day 3)

If they didn't reply to Touch 1, send something USEFUL · not another
ask.

**Options:**
- Share a case study (with someone in their niche)
- Share a tactical resource (article, video, tool)
- Share a contrarian take you wrote
- Share a specific observation you have about their market

**Example:**

> Hey [name], saw a related case I thought you'd find useful · we
> just helped [similar founder] go from [X] to [Y] in [time]. Specific
> tactic was [insight]. Happy to share the full case study if useful.

**Why this works:**
- Doesn't ask for anything
- Frames you as a giver, not a taker
- Establishes you actually have results
- Re-opens the conversation door without pressure

---

### Touch 3 · The polite breakup (Day 7)

If they still haven't replied, give them ONE more chance with
psychology working FOR you.

```
Hey [name] · checking in on [original outreach reason]. Totally fair
if the timing's not right · I'll close the loop on my end if I don't
hear back by [day]. Otherwise · happy to pick this up whenever it
makes sense for you.
```

**Why this works (loss aversion):**
- "Closing the loop" creates a deadline
- "Totally fair" lowers pressure
- The buyer often replies just to NOT lose the option

**Reply rate jump:** Touch 3 typically gets 15-25% reply rate
on its own · because it's the only one that uses loss-aversion.

## The 7-touch cadence (for B2B / higher-ticket)

For deals > $5k or longer sales cycles, extend the cadence:

```
TOUCH 1 · DAY 0   · Personalized DM (the opener)
TOUCH 2 · DAY 2   · LinkedIn comment on their recent post (engagement)
TOUCH 3 · DAY 4   · Value-first follow-up (case study)
TOUCH 4 · DAY 7   · Voice memo (if you have their phone) or video DM
TOUCH 5 · DAY 11  · Different channel (email if started on LinkedIn)
TOUCH 6 · DAY 15  · Tag them in a relevant post / introduction to a peer
TOUCH 7 · DAY 21  · Polite breakup (loss aversion close)
```

### Channel rotation

Don't use the same channel 7 times. Rotate:
- Touch 1: LinkedIn DM
- Touch 2: LinkedIn comment (visible to their network · social proof)
- Touch 3: LinkedIn DM (value drop)
- Touch 4: Voice memo on LinkedIn (cuts through · 90%+ open rate)
- Touch 5: Cold email (different channel, fresh inbox)
- Touch 6: Tag in a relevant post (public · they see you)
- Touch 7: LinkedIn DM breakup

---

## The voice memo (Touch 4 · the cheat code)

LinkedIn voice memos are the most under-leveraged outreach channel.
Open rate ~90%. Reply rate ~30%. Why?

- Effort signal · "they took time for me"
- Voice = trust (text dehumanizes)
- Most reps don't do it · novelty stands out

**Script (60 seconds max):**

> Hey [name] · this is [your name]. I sent you a DM last week about
> [topic]. Just wanted to make this more human. Quick context · I help
> [their type] do [outcome]. Saw on your profile that [specific
> observation] · made me think you might be the right kind of person
> for what I do. No rush · just wanted to put a voice to the message.
> Have a great week.

**Why it works:**
- 60 seconds = no commitment for them
- Personal touch = trust
- "No rush" = no pressure

## Personalization · the 3-line rule

Every outreach must reference 3 specific things about the prospect:

```
1 · Specific content (post, article, video)
2 · Specific company/situation detail (recent funding, new hire,
   product launch, milestone)
3 · Specific shared connection (mutual friend, niche, geography)
```

If you can't find 3 specifics in 5 minutes of research, the prospect
isn't right. Move on.

**Where to find specifics:**
- LinkedIn recent posts (last 14 days)
- Their company news (Crunchbase, AngelList, news searches)
- Mutual connections (LinkedIn shows them)
- Podcast appearances (transcripts on Listen Notes)
- Substack / newsletter archives
- GitHub if technical

---

## Subject lines (for cold email)

Top performers:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **One-word personal** | "[their name]" |
| **Reference their content** | "Your post about [topic]" |
| **Question** | "Quick question about [their company]" |
| **Outcome promise** | "30 BANT-qualified leads in 30 days" |
| **Mutual connection** | "[Mutual] suggested I reach out" |

**Subject line rules:**
- Under 6 words
- No emojis (filter trigger)
- No ALL CAPS (filter trigger)
- Mirror their last subject line if you have it

## Cadence math

Reply rate per touch (industry average for cold outreach):

```
Touch 1:  3-5%
Touch 2:  4-6%   (cumulative 7-11%)
Touch 3:  10-15% (cumulative 17-26%) ← biggest jump
Touch 4:  3-5%
Touch 5:  2-4%
Touch 6:  1-2%
Touch 7:  10-15% (the breakup pop)
```

If you stop at Touch 1, you capture 5%. If you run all 7, you capture
30%+. The deals are in touches 3 and 7.

## What NOT to do (cold outreach mistakes)

1. **Generic openers** ("I love your content!") · 95% reply-rate killer
2. **Asking for time before establishing relevance** · "Got 30 min?"
3. **Pasting templates verbatim** · the [name] tag with no name shows
4. **Sending on Mondays at 9am** · everyone does · standout fails
5. **No follow-up** · the deal is in touch 3 + 7
6. **Long messages** · keep under 100 words for Touch 1
7. **Fake urgency** · "Limited time!" on cold = trash
8. **Selling on Touch 1** · qualify first, sell later
