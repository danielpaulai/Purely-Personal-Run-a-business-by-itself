# The Grand Slam Offer · 4-Part Construction

> An offer so good people feel stupid saying no. Not a feature list.
> A construction. 4 levers stacked correctly = a price the market
> can't ignore.

---

## The math underneath every great offer

```
            Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood
   Value =  ─────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

(See `frameworks/marketing/value-equation.md` for the deep version.)

The Grand Slam Offer is what happens when you intentionally maximize
the numerators and minimize the denominators using 4 specific tools:

1. **Bonuses**           · multiply Dream Outcome × Likelihood
2. **Scarcity**          · accelerate Time Delay
3. **Urgency**           · accelerate Time Delay
4. **Guarantees**        · slash perceived Effort & Sacrifice

Stack all 4 correctly = a Grand Slam.

---

## Step 1 · The Core Offer

Before you stack, get the core right.

**Naming convention:** the offer name should be a SPECIFIC, MEASURABLE,
TIME-BOUND outcome.

- Bad: "Sales Coaching Program"
- Good: "30 BANT-Qualified Leads in 30 Days · Or You Don't Pay"

Formula:
```
[Specific outcome] in [specific time] for [specific person]
```

Examples:
- "From $0 to $10k MRR in 90 days · for solopreneurs with no list"
- "Cleared inboxes in under 9 minutes · for founders drowning in email"
- "20 BANT-qualified leads delivered weekly · for B2B founders sub-$2M ARR"

The name DOES the marketing for you. If your offer name doesn't sell
itself, the offer isn't ready.

---

## Step 2 · Stack Bonuses (multiply value)

Bonuses are HOW you get to "value $12K, price $1,999" without
discounting.

**Rules for bonuses:**
1. Each bonus solves a problem the buyer hasn't named yet but WILL
   hit during implementation
2. Each has a specific dollar value attached (anchor it)
3. Each is positioned as something you'd normally sell separately
4. Stack 3-7 (not 12 · the buyer stops believing)

**Example stack (for Purely Personal AI CEO at $1,999):**

```
Core: AI CEO orchestration agent (full setup)        ($5,000 value)
+ Bonus 1: 4-week sprint with the cohort              ($1,500)
+ Bonus 2: Advanced engines pack (10 new engines)     ($2,000)
+ Bonus 3: Tue + Thu live calls × 4 weeks             ($2,400)
+ Bonus 4: Slack community · ongoing                  ($1,164)
+ Bonus 5: 30-day money-back guarantee                (priceless)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total value:                                          $12,064
Your investment:                                      $1,999
You save:                                             $10,065
```

**Bonus principles:**
- Anchor each at retail price
- Sum them up · then reveal price
- Make at least ONE feel almost-too-good ("Bonus 4 alone is worth
  the price")
- The Slack community / ongoing access bonus is always the highest
  perceived value because "lifetime" = infinity

---

## Step 3 · Add Scarcity (limit supply)

Scarcity = number-based limit. Real scarcity, not fake.

**Real scarcity tactics:**
- "30 spots in this cohort" (delivery-bandwidth limited)
- "I'm taking 3 1:1 clients in May"
- "Only 5 founder seats at this price · then it goes to $2,999"
- "First 10 buyers get the 1:1 call"

**Fake scarcity (don't):**
- "Only 50 left!" (when there's infinite digital inventory)
- "Sale ends today!" (when the same sale runs every week)

The buyer can SMELL fake scarcity. It destroys trust faster than no
scarcity at all.

**For Purely Personal AI CEO:**
- "30 attendees in this workshop · the offer is for these 30 only"
- "Tier 2 has 5 seats · I can't 1:1 onboard more than 5 in May"

---

## Step 4 · Add Urgency (limit time)

Urgency = time-based limit. ALSO must be real.

**Real urgency tactics:**
- "Doors close at 13:00 today (when Day 2 ends)"
- "Bonus 3 disappears after the live call"
- "Price increases to $2,999 in the next cohort (3 months away)"

**Why workshops work for urgency:**
- The class is happening NOW
- The cohort starts on a specific date
- The bonuses you're offering are tied to BEING IN the live class

**Sample urgency line:**
> "Both links close at 13:00 today. The next cohort, if I run one, is
> 3 months away and the price will be $2,999 base. So this is the
> only window for $1,999. Today."

---

## Step 5 · Slash Risk with Guarantees

The guarantee is the most underrated lever in offer construction. Most
sellers under-leverage it.

**Tiers of guarantee, from weakest to strongest:**

### Tier 1 · Money-back guarantee
"If you don't love it, refund."

### Tier 2 · Conditional money-back
"If you do the work for 30 days and it doesn't move your business,
refund."

### Tier 3 · Performance guarantee
"If you don't get [specific outcome] in [specific time], you get
[specific compensation]."

### Tier 4 · Reversed-risk guarantee (the strongest)
"You don't pay until you see [outcome]." OR "Pay 50% upfront, 50% after
[milestone]."

**For Purely Personal AI CEO:**

Recommend Tier 2:
> "30-day full money back. No questions. The only condition: do the
> work · run AI CEO at least once a week · attend 3 of 4 calls. If
> you do that and it doesn't shift your business · email me. Refund.
> No defending. No exit survey. Done."

The conditional makes it FEEL like a fair deal · not a refund-grab
deal. Reduces refund rate by 60%+ vs unconditional.

## Step 6 · The Naming Math

Once you have the stack, give it a name that compresses the math:

```
[Number of buyers who succeeded] founders.
[Specific outcome] in [time].
[Risk reversal phrase].
```

Example:
> "200 founders. Business that runs without them. In 30 days. Or
> refund."

That ONE sentence compresses the entire Grand Slam Offer math.

## How to PRESENT the Grand Slam Offer

The order of presentation determines the close rate. Follow this:

1. **Reframe the prospect's problem** (from NEPQ Stage 2)
   - "Based on what you said, the cost of staying as you are is $X."
2. **Introduce the core offer**
   - "Here's what I built for that exact situation."
3. **Walk through the stack in ascending value order**
   - Start with the smallest bonus, end with the biggest
4. **Sum the value**
   - "$12K total value"
5. **Strike-through to the price**
   - "Investment is $1,999"
6. **Risk reversal**
   - "30-day money back"
7. **Scarcity + Urgency**
   - "30 seats · doors close at 13:00 today"
8. **Decisive close**
   - "If you can see how this works for YOU · stand up." (or "click")

NEVER lead with the price. Price always comes AFTER the stack. The
stack makes the price feel small.

## The 11 leverage points (offer audit checklist)

For ANY offer · score yourself /11:

```
[ ] /1 · Specific dream outcome (numbers, time-bound)
[ ] /1 · Perceived likelihood high (proof, roadmap, case studies)
[ ] /1 · Time delay compressed (results in days, not months)
[ ] /1 · Effort & sacrifice low (done-for-you elements)
[ ] /1 · Bonus stack builds to 5x+ price in value
[ ] /1 · Real scarcity (not fake)
[ ] /1 · Real urgency (not fake)
[ ] /1 · Guarantee at Tier 2+ (conditional money-back minimum)
[ ] /1 · Naming math (one sentence compresses it)
[ ] /1 · Presentation order (stack BEFORE price)
[ ] /1 · Decisive close (asks for the YES)

Total: ___ /11

Under 8: don't launch. Fix first.
```
