# Wildly Important Goals (WIGs) · Pick the Right 1-3 Things

> The single biggest mistake in execution is having too many goals.
> 5 priorities = 0 priorities. Pick 1-3 Wildly Important Goals.
> Everything else is the whirlwind. The discipline is keeping them
> separate.

---

## What makes a goal WILDLY important

3 criteria · all must be true:

```
1 · IF achieved, the business MEANINGFULLY shifts
2 · It's MEASURABLE (number + deadline · not vague aspiration)
3 · It's WITHIN current capacity (we can do it without breaking)
```

A goal that fails any of the 3 isn't wildly important · it's just
busy work dressed up.

---

## The mathematical case for fewer goals

Goal completion rate by quantity:

```
1-3 goals  · 80% completion rate
4-10 goals · 30% completion rate
11+ goals  · 5% completion rate
```

This isn't motivation. It's attention math. The brain can hold ~3
priority threads. Beyond that, attention diffuses · everything
slows.

**The discipline:** pick fewer goals than you think you can ship.
Then ship them.

---

## The WIG sentence (force the format)

Every WIG must fit this format:

```
From [current state] → To [target state] → By [deadline]
```

If you can't fit it in this format · it's not WIG-ready.

**Examples for Purely Personal Q2 2026:**

```
WIG 1: From 0 paying cohort members → To 30 paying members → By May 3 13:00

WIG 2: From 0 frameworks shipped → To 5 framework directories live → By April 30

WIG 3: From 12 alumni in Slack → To 50 alumni in Slack → By June 30
```

Each one passes all 3 criteria:
- Meaningful shift (revenue · positioning · community)
- Measurable (specific numbers + dates)
- Within capacity (can deliver with current team + tools)

## The whirlwind · everything else

Everything that's not a WIG is "the whirlwind." Important. Necessary.
Just not WILDLY important.

For Purely Personal · the whirlwind includes:
- Email triage
- Support requests
- Routine content posts
- Existing customer comms
- Vendor management
- Tech maintenance

The whirlwind has to keep running · OR THE WIGS DON'T MATTER. If
you ignore the whirlwind, customers leave · revenue drops · WIG
becomes irrelevant.

The challenge: KEEP the whirlwind handled · WHILE moving the WIGs.

**Solution:** the engines + routines handle 80% of the whirlwind.
Your time goes to the WIGs.

## How to identify the right WIGs (the focusing question)

Ask: "What's the ONE area of the business that, if dramatically
improved · would unlock everything else?"

Examples by stage:

```
PRE-PRODUCT-MARKET-FIT · WIG = "find the offer that resonates"
   - Lead measure: customer interviews per week (8-12)

POST-PMF · LOW REVENUE  · WIG = "find the channel that scales"
   - Lead measure: outreach volume + close rate

PROFITABLE · LOW MARGIN · WIG = "raise prices · improve unit econ"
   - Lead measure: avg deal size · LTV/CAC ratio

PROFITABLE · HIGH MARGIN · WIG = "build the team · install systems"
   - Lead measure: % of work delegated · SOPs documented

SCALED · STAGNANT · WIG = "find the next product or channel"
   - Lead measure: experiments shipped per month
```

Different stages · different WIGs. Don't pick a "hire" WIG when
you're pre-PMF. Don't pick a "find the channel" WIG when you're
already scaled.

## Common WIG mistakes

### 1 · Too many WIGs

"We have 7 priorities for the quarter."

That's 7 goals · 35% completion rate. Cut to 3 · 80% completion.

### 2 · WIGs that aren't measurable

"Improve our marketing."

Not measurable. Better: "Increase weekly LinkedIn engagement from
15 avg to 50 avg by quarter end."

### 3 · WIGs without lead measures

"30 cohort members by May 3."

Great WIG · no lead. What do you DO each day to move it? Lead
measure: "Send 8 personalized DMs to workshop attendees · daily
through May 3."

### 4 · WIGs that can't be controlled

"Get 1 million followers."

Can't control · only influence. Better WIG: "Ship 60 posts in 90
days." (= what you can DO. The followers come or don't · but the
input is in your control.)

### 5 · WIGs that get crushed by the whirlwind

You set the WIG · then never touch it because email demands took
over. Solution: protect WIG time on calendar (block 2 hrs/day for
WIG work · before opening email).

## The personal WIG (life · not just business)

Apply WIGs to your personal life too:

```
HEALTH WIG     · "From 200 lbs to 180 lbs by July 1"
                  Lead: 4 strength sessions/week + 1 protein meal at breakfast

RELATIONSHIPS  · "From 0 weekly date nights to 1/week by April 30"
                  Lead: book it Monday morning for that Friday

LEARNING       · "Read 4 business books by June 30"
                  Lead: 30 pages/day · 7am · before email
```

Most operators have business WIGs · skip personal WIGs · burn out.
Pick 1 personal WIG per quarter alongside the business ones.

## The WIG audit · weekly + quarterly

### Weekly audit (in the L10):
```
[ ] Did we move each WIG this week?
[ ] If a WIG didn't move · what specifically blocked it?
[ ] What's THE action this week that would move WIG #1?
```

### Quarterly audit:
```
[ ] Did we hit each WIG?
[ ] Hit · why? · what's the lesson · keep doing
[ ] Missed · why? · what's the lesson · what changes
[ ] Are these still the right WIGs for next quarter?
[ ] What's the next bottleneck stage of the business?
[ ] What new WIGs match that stage?
```

The audit is the discipline that keeps WIGs from becoming wishful
thinking. Without the audit · WIGs decay into "we said we'd do that
but didn't."

---

## The 1-question diagnostic

Once per quarter · ask:

> "Of all the things I could do this quarter · which 1-3 would
> matter MOST 12 months from now?"

That's your WIG. Everything else is the whirlwind. Run the engines
and routines to keep the whirlwind under control · and protect 80%
of your strategic capacity for the WIG.

The discipline isn't doing more. It's choosing the right thing · and
not letting anything else compete for your attention until that
thing is done.
