# The 6 Mental Faculties · Where Real Leadership Lives

> Most operators run on 1-2 mental faculties (perception + memory).
> The 6 faculties are the OS underneath all decision-making. Master
> each one and the quality of every decision rises. Built around an
> old framework · still the best map of how the mind ACTUALLY works.

---

## The 6 faculties

```
1 · PERCEPTION   ·  how you see things (point of view)
2 · WILL         ·  ability to hold focus on ONE thing
3 · IMAGINATION  ·  the future you can see
4 · INTUITION    ·  the read · the gut · pattern recognition
5 · MEMORY       ·  what you carry forward (and forget)
6 · REASON       ·  logic · sequencing · cause and effect
```

Most operators rely on REASON (analysis) and MEMORY (past
experience). They under-use the other 4 · which are the highest-
leverage faculties for leadership.

---

## 1 · PERCEPTION · how you see things

> "It's not what you see · it's HOW you see it."

Perception is the lens. Same external reality · different lenses ·
different decisions.

**Examples of perception shifts:**

| Same fact | Limiting perception | Empowering perception |
|---|---|---|
| Workshop has 22 attendees (vs target 30) | "Failure" | "Strong base · revisit positioning" |
| Cohort sales at 3 (vs target 5) | "Disappointing" | "60% of target · with room to grow" |
| Email inbox at 200 unread | "Overwhelming" | "Most of these are low-priority · I'll triage" |

Perception isn't denial. It's choosing which TRUTHS to focus on.

**Practice:** when you notice yourself reacting strongly · pause and
ask: "Is there another way to see this?" Find 2 alternative
perceptions. Pick the one that serves the next move.

---

## 2 · WILL · ability to hold focus

> "Will is the ability to hold ONE idea on the screen of your mind ·
> uninterrupted · for an extended period."

This isn't motivation. It's CONCENTRATION. The capacity to:
- Stay on the hard task when easier tasks beckon
- Hold a vision for 12 months when results take 3
- Keep one priority when 5 demands compete

**Why this matters for operators:**

The thing that distinguishes founders who ship from founders who
drift is the capacity to HOLD focus. Distraction is the rule.
Sustained focus is the exception.

**Practice:** every morning, write down ONE thing this day MUST
ship. Hold it on your mind all day. Don't let it move. End of day:
did you ship it?

After 30 days of this · your will gets stronger.

**The interrupt resistance:**
- Phone face down (out of sight, out of mind)
- One screen for the focus task · no second screen
- Communicate "deep work · 9am-12pm" to anyone who pings
- A timer (90 minutes max · then a 15-min break)

---

## 3 · IMAGINATION · the future you can see

> "Imagination is the most powerful faculty · because it's where you
> CREATE before you execute."

Most operators imagine SMALL futures. They can vividly see "next
month's revenue" but struggle to vividly see "5 years from now."

**The imagination test:**

```
1 · Close your eyes
2 · See yourself 12 months from today (April 27, 2027)
3 · What's your business doing? Where are you living? What's your
    revenue? What does your week look like? Who's around you?
```

If the picture is vague · your imagination is under-trained.

**Why this matters:**

You can only build what you can see. If you can't see a $30K MRR
business clearly · you won't build it. If you CAN see it (in detail
· in the morning routines · in the cohort dynamics · in the cash
flow) · the path appears.

**Practice:**

- Write a one-page description of your business 12 months from now
  (specific · vivid · sensory)
- Re-read it weekly
- Update it monthly

The clearer the imagination · the easier the execution.

---

## 4 · INTUITION · the read · the gut · pattern recognition

> "Intuition is the result of accumulated reps · run through pattern
> recognition · faster than reason."

Intuition isn't magic. It's compressed expertise. After 1,000
similar situations · you "just know" what to do · because you've
seen the pattern.

**Why operators distrust intuition:**

They confuse:
- Intuition (signal from accumulated experience) · TRUSTWORTHY
- Anxiety (fear in the body) · NOT trustworthy
- Wishful thinking (what you want to be true) · NOT trustworthy

The trick: discriminate between the three.

**The 3 questions to test intuition:**

```
1 · Have I seen patterns like this before? (= intuition)
2 · Is this fear about my safety / ego? (= anxiety · ignore)
3 · Is this what I HOPE will happen? (= wishful · ignore)
```

If yes to #1 and no to #2 and #3 · trust the gut. Fast decisions
made on real intuition outperform slow decisions made on incomplete
analysis.

**Practice:** before any meeting / sales call / decision · pause for
30 seconds and write down your gut prediction. After the event ·
score how accurate the gut was. Over 90 days · your intuition
calibrates.

---

## 5 · MEMORY · what you carry forward

> "Memory is what we draw from. But what we choose to remember
> changes who we are."

Two types of memory matter for operators:

### a · Episodic memory (specific events)
- The cold DM that closed
- The launch that flopped
- The moment a customer transformed

You should mine your own episodic memory for patterns. Most
operators forget their wins (and over-remember their losses) · which
poisons future confidence.

### b · Semantic memory (frameworks, structures)
- "When pipeline is dry · increase outreach by 50%"
- "When close rate is high · raise prices"
- "When inbox is full · I tighten triage"

The frameworks library you're reading IS semantic memory ·
documented · so you can RETRIEVE it instead of having to remember
it.

**Practice:**
- Keep a wins journal (1 specific win per week)
- Re-read it monthly · this is your semantic memory of capability
- When facing a hard decision · go to the frameworks first

---

## 6 · REASON · logic · sequencing · cause-and-effect

> "Reason is the cleanest faculty · but only as good as the inputs
> you give it."

Reason takes inputs (facts, data, frameworks) and produces outputs
(decisions, plans). It's the calculator of the mind.

Most operators rely on reason heavily · but feed it bad inputs:
- Outdated assumptions
- Limiting beliefs from old identity
- Incomplete data

Garbage in · garbage out.

**Why reason isn't enough:**

A perfectly reasoned decision can be the wrong decision IF:
- The inputs are limiting
- The framework is outdated
- The intuition is screaming and you ignored it

Reason is one of 6 faculties · not the master. Use it · don't be
ruled by it.

**Practice:** when reason is telling you to do X but intuition is
saying don't · pause. Ask: "What's the data the intuition is using?"
Often there's a real signal you haven't articulated yet.

## How to use all 6 in a real decision

Example · should we run another workshop in 8 weeks?

```
PERCEPTION:  How am I seeing the last workshop?
             - Limiting view: "It only had 22 attendees · failure"
             - Empowering view: "Strong test cohort · ready to scale"

WILL:        Can I commit to ONE outcome for the next 8 weeks?
             - Yes: "I'll fill 40 seats by June 15"

IMAGINATION: Can I see the next workshop vividly?
             - Yes: 40 attendees · zoom · familiar pacing · stronger
               AI CEO conversion (12-15%)

INTUITION:   What does my gut say?
             - "Yes · but tighten the qualification on the workshop
                signup. Some of the May attendees weren't fit."

MEMORY:      What did I learn from May 2-3?
             - The Day 2 pitch landed best when I held silence
               longer at slide 24
             - The Tier 2 1:1 bonus was the single biggest conversion
               lever

REASON:      Does the math work?
             - 40 attendees × $49 = $1,960
             - 12% upsell × $2,300 = $11,040
             - Total: $13,000 over 8 weeks · yes · scale
```

All 6 faculties · feeding into ONE decision. The decision is
better than any single faculty alone.

## The faculty audit · run quarterly

Score yourself 1-10 on each:

```
PERCEPTION  · how often do I default to empowering interpretations?
WILL        · how long can I hold focus on ONE priority?
IMAGINATION · how vivid is my 12-month vision?
INTUITION   · how often do I trust + act on my gut?
MEMORY      · how systematically do I draw from past wins + frameworks?
REASON      · how often do I question the inputs I'm reasoning from?
```

Most operators score 6-7-3-4-5-7. Reason and perception are
trained. Imagination, intuition, will are under-trained.

The leverage move: train the 3 weakest faculties for 90 days.

## The 1-question diagnostic

For any decision · ask:

> "Have I checked all 6 faculties · or am I deciding from just 1-2?"

A decision filtered through all 6 · with conscious weighting · is
exponentially better than one filtered through reason alone.

Most operators skip imagination ("don't have time to dream") and
intuition ("trust the data instead"). The result: decisions that
LOOK rigorous but produce mediocre outcomes.

The 6 faculties are the leadership operating system. Train all 6 ·
and the quality of every future decision compounds.
