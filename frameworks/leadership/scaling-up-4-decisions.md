# The 4 Decisions · The Operating System for Scaling Companies

> Every business that scales is making the same 4 categories of
> decisions · over and over · forever. Get all 4 right · the company
> compounds. Get any one wrong · the ceiling appears.

---

## The 4 decisions

```
1 · PEOPLE      ·  who's on the team · who's not
2 · STRATEGY    ·  what we're doing · why · for whom
3 · EXECUTION   ·  how we ship · the operating cadence
4 · CASH        ·  the lifeblood · the timing · the war chest
```

Most operators are strong on 1-2 of these and weak on 2-3.
Diagnostically · the weakest decision is the bottleneck. Fix it ·
the company unlocks.

---

## Decision 1 · PEOPLE

The most leverage-d of the 4. Get this wrong · everything else
struggles. Get this right · 3-5 people can outperform a team of 30.

### The 3 sub-decisions

```
1 · WHO IS IN  ·  who currently delivers
2 · WHO IS OUT ·  who needs to leave
3 · WHO TO ADD ·  next 3-6 hires (or AI engines)
```

### For solopreneurs (most of you)

"Team" includes:
- AI engines (do they fit your operating style?)
- Contractors (right-sized? right specialists?)
- Virtual assistants (clear SOPs · or improvising?)
- Eventually: full-time hires
- Always: yourself (in the right role?)

### The 3 questions for each team member

```
1 · GET IT?  ·  do they understand WHAT we do and WHY?
2 · WANT IT? ·  are they emotionally bought in?
3 · DO IT?   ·  do they consistently deliver outputs?
```

Yes to all 3 · keep + invest. Yes to 1-2 · coach. No to 1 or
multiple · rotate out.

For the AI engines:
- "Get it" = the engine reads BUSINESS-BRAIN.md and produces
  on-brand output
- "Want it" = the engine doesn't drift over time (frameworks updated)
- "Do it" = output is reliably saved and used

If any AI engine fails any 3 · update its prompts (= "coach") or
deprecate it (= "rotate out").

### The 30-second rule

Imagine each team member quitting tomorrow. Would you:
- Be RELIEVED · they need to go
- Be NEUTRAL · they're acceptable
- Be ALARMED · they're A-players · invest in them

If 30%+ of your team triggers RELIEVED · that's the bottleneck.
Address it.

## Decision 2 · STRATEGY

What you're doing · for whom · why. The core market positioning.

### The 4 strategy questions

```
1 · WORD       ·  what's the ONE word you own in customers' minds?
2 · WHO/WHAT   ·  who's your customer? what do you sell them?
3 · BRAND PROMISE ·  what specific outcome do you GUARANTEE?
4 · 5-15 YEAR  ·  what's the long game?
```

### a · OWN A WORD

The single biggest move in strategy: own a word in your customers'
minds. NOT a sentence · a word.

**Examples:**
- Volvo · "safety"
- Tesla · "electric"
- Stripe · "developers"
- Hormozi · "offers"

For Purely Personal · the candidate word is "AUTOPILOT" or
"WITHOUT-YOU." Pick one. Reinforce it everywhere. Don't drift.

If you don't own a word · you compete on price. Price competition
caps margins.

### b · WHO/WHAT (the offer)

For each customer segment · what do you offer?

```
ICP                          OFFER                 PRICE
Solopreneurs · pre-$1M ARR  · Workshop ($49)      → $49
Same · post-workshop         · AI CEO Tier 1       → $1,999
Same · ready for 1:1         · AI CEO Tier 2       → $2,999
Alumni · ongoing             · Slack subscription  → $97/mo
```

The offers should ladder. Each one is the natural next step from the
previous.

### c · BRAND PROMISE

The specific guarantee. Measurable. Time-bound.

For Purely Personal:
> "By Sunday at 5pm · you have a business that runs without you ·
>  or refund."

That's a brand promise. Specific. Measurable. Time-bound. Risk-
reversed.

If you don't have a brand promise · you're a vendor · not a brand.

### d · 5-15 YEAR PICTURE

Where is the business in 5-15 years? Specific.

For Purely Personal (5-year):
- 1,000 alumni founders
- $5M ARR · 70% from continuity (Slack subs) + cohort programs
- Distributed team of 8 · operating across 3 timezones
- Industry-known frameworks (this directory · published)

Without a 5-year picture · you're tactically responsive · not
strategically directed.

## Decision 3 · EXECUTION

How the work gets done · reliably · without breaking the operator.

### The 3 disciplines

```
1 · PRIORITIES   ·  3-7 quarterly Rocks
2 · DATA         ·  weekly scoreboard · measurable
3 · MEETING RHYTHM ·  daily / weekly / quarterly cadences
```

### a · 3-7 quarterly Rocks (per the 4DX framework)

See `operations/four-disciplines-execution.md` for the deep version.

Rocks for Purely Personal Q2 2026:

```
1 · 30 paying cohort members from May workshop
2 · Frameworks library shipped + integrated (this directory)
3 · 50-member Slack alumni community
4 · Phase 5 + 6 install videos
5 · One major affiliate partnership signed
```

5 rocks. Quarterly. Reviewed every Monday at the L10 meeting.

### b · WEEKLY SCOREBOARD

The 5-15 metrics you check every Friday. Visible. Updated. Linked
to Rocks.

Sample for Purely Personal:

```
Marketing:
  - LinkedIn posts shipped this week
  - Average post engagement
  - Newsletter subscribers (week change)

Sales:
  - DMs sent (lead measure)
  - Sales calls booked (lag from DMs)
  - Cohort sign-ups (lag · on-track to 30?)

Operations:
  - Engines run on schedule (out of 6 routines)
  - SOPs added/updated this week
  - Inbox processing time (hrs)

Cash:
  - Revenue this week
  - PROFIT account balance vs target
  - 13-week forecast cumulative · GREEN/YELLOW/RED

Leadership:
  - L10 meeting held? Rated 8+?
  - Identity manifesto re-read?
  - Quarterly Rocks · % complete
```

15 metrics · 5 minutes to review · keeps you grounded.

### c · MEETING RHYTHM

Daily / Weekly / Quarterly · in order.

```
DAILY    · 5 min  · stand-up (solo or with team)
            "what shipped yesterday? what ships today?
             what's blocking?"

WEEKLY   · 90 min · L10 meeting (see operations/eos-level-10-meeting.md)

MONTHLY  · 60 min · cash + unit-economics review
            (see cash/money-review-cadence.md)

QUARTERLY · 1 day · strategy + Rocks review + planning
```

The rhythm IS the operating system.

## Decision 4 · CASH

Without cash · the other 3 decisions stop mattering. Cash is the
oxygen.

### The 3 cash sub-decisions

```
1 · GENERATING CASH ·  how do we MAKE more
2 · KEEPING CASH    ·  how do we STORE more
3 · ALLOCATING CASH ·  where to deploy
```

### a · GENERATING CASH

This is what the marketing + sales engines do. Top of funnel ·
qualification · close · upsell.

Quarterly question: "What's the highest-leverage move to generate
20% MORE revenue next quarter without working 20% more hours?"

For Purely Personal:
- This quarter: workshop + AI CEO upsell
- Next quarter: another workshop + cohort + new continuity offer
- Quarter after: affiliate engine + alumni referral program

### b · KEEPING CASH (Profit First)

See `cash/profit-first.md`. The 5-account discipline. Don't keep
cash IN the operating account · transfer it OUT · so it's preserved.

Most operators "spend what's there." Profit First makes "what's
there" smaller · so spending is constrained.

### c · ALLOCATING CASH

Cash that comes IN should flow to:
- Owner pay (the founder · paid first AFTER profit)
- Reinvestment (engines · contractors · marketing)
- War chest (PROFIT account · 50% retained)
- Investments (I-quadrant moves · shift % over time)

Quarterly question: "If we have a $20K surplus in PROFIT account ·
where does it go?"

Allocation discipline distinguishes the operator-investor (Wealthy)
from the operator-employee (S quadrant · still working for paycheck).

## The 4-decision audit · run quarterly

```
DECISION 1 · PEOPLE
[ ] Right people in right roles?
[ ] Anyone needs to be coached out?
[ ] What's the next 1-2 hires (or AI engines to add)?
[ ] Score: ___ /10

DECISION 2 · STRATEGY
[ ] Do we own a clear word?
[ ] Are our offers laddered correctly?
[ ] Is the brand promise specific?
[ ] Is the 5-year picture vivid?
[ ] Score: ___ /10

DECISION 3 · EXECUTION
[ ] 3-7 Rocks defined for this quarter?
[ ] Weekly scoreboard active and reviewed?
[ ] Meeting rhythm running (daily/weekly/quarterly)?
[ ] Score: ___ /10

DECISION 4 · CASH
[ ] Profit First system installed?
[ ] 13-week forecast updated weekly?
[ ] Allocation rules clear?
[ ] Score: ___ /10

TOTAL: ___ /40
```

The lowest-scored decision is the bottleneck. Fix it next quarter.

## Apply it to /leadership-engine

When the engine generates the Sunday Brief or Quarterly Review:

1. **Audit the 4 decisions** based on observable signals
2. **Score each decision** 0-10
3. **Identify the weakest** · the next quarter's focus
4. **Generate the 1 question** to address it (e.g., "Strategy is
   weakest · what one word will you own?")
5. **Save the audit** to BUSINESS-BRAIN.md § Calendar (this quarter's
   focus)

When the engine generates the Friday Wrap:

1. Map this week's progress to current Rocks
2. Update the weekly scoreboard
3. Surface any decision that's drifting (e.g., "team-people
   challenge: contractor missed 2 deadlines")

## The 1-question diagnostic

Once a quarter, ask:

> "Of People · Strategy · Execution · Cash · which one is going to
> be the bottleneck for our next stage of growth?"

Whatever the answer · that's where you spend the next 90 days. Not
sprinkling effort across all 4. Concentrating on the bottleneck.

The compounding effect: every quarter you fix the weakest decision ·
the company moves up a level. Over 4 years · 16 levels of growth.
That's not theory · that's the math of focused improvement.

The 4 decisions · in order of leverage:
- People > Strategy > Execution > Cash

Get the right people, with the right strategy, executing the right
rhythm · cash follows. Skip any of the first 3 · cash is fragile no
matter how disciplined the management.
