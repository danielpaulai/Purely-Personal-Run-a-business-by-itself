# Vision Traction Organizer (V/TO) · The 2-Page Operating Plan

> Most strategy lives in slide decks no one reads. The V/TO is 2
> pages. Held in the operator's mind. Reviewed weekly. Updated
> quarterly. THE artifact that aligns the team and traction.

---

## The 2-page structure

```
PAGE 1 · VISION   ·  the long-game (5-15 years)
                     - Core values
                     - Core focus
                     - 10-year target
                     - Marketing strategy
                     - 3-year picture

PAGE 2 · TRACTION ·  the next 12 months
                     - 1-year plan
                     - Quarterly Rocks
                     - Issues list
```

PAGE 1 changes slowly (quarterly review). PAGE 2 changes weekly (L10
meetings). Together · they're the entire operating plan.

---

## PAGE 1 · VISION

### a · CORE VALUES (3-7 max)

The non-negotiables of how you operate. Not aspirations · who you
ALREADY are.

Test: "If we hired someone and they violated this value · would we
fire them?" If yes · it's a real value. If no · it's a poster.

For Purely Personal:

```
1 · BUILD-FIRST · we build the system before we sell the system
2 · IMPLEMENT-LIVE · we don't lecture · we install while you watch
3 · SPECIFICITY · numbers · names · dates · not vibes
4 · NO BANNED PHRASES · the language reflects the brand
5 · COMMITMENT TO SHIP · weekly · always · without exception
```

5 values. Real. Operational. Filterable for hiring + firing + brand
decisions.

### b · CORE FOCUS

ONE sentence. What you do · for whom · why.

```
"We help solopreneurs install AI-powered businesses that run
without them · in 4 hours of live workshop · with a 30-day
money-back guarantee."
```

Core Focus filters every opportunity:
- Does this product fit the focus? Yes → build · No → skip
- Does this customer fit the focus? Yes → serve · No → refer

### c · 10-YEAR TARGET (BHAG)

Big hairy audacious goal. Specific enough to be measurable.

For Purely Personal · 10-year target:

```
"By 2036:
 - 10,000 alumni founders · running engine-driven businesses
 - $50M ARR · 60%+ from continuity revenue
 - The standard for 'AI-installed businesses' · industry-known
   frameworks · published"
```

The BHAG is aspirational but plotted on a real path. Re-read
quarterly · update annually if needed.

### d · MARKETING STRATEGY

3 components · each in 1 sentence:

```
TARGET MARKET     · Solopreneurs · pre-$1M ARR · who feel they
                    ARE the bottleneck of their business

3 UNIQUES         · 1 · Live install (not theory)
                    2 · AI-engine architecture (not human VAs)
                    3 · Cohort + ongoing community (not 1:1
                       coaching)

PROVEN PROCESS    · 5 engines + 6 weekly routines + AI CEO

GUARANTEE         · "30-day money back · do the work · refund
                    if business doesn't shift"
```

These are the 4 sub-questions of marketing strategy. Answer each.
Now your positioning is locked.

### e · 3-YEAR PICTURE

Detailed picture of the business in 3 years (the "future state"
that makes the BHAG plausible).

```
3-YEAR PICTURE · April 2029

REVENUE:           $5M ARR
                   $3M from continuity (Slack subs · alumni)
                   $1.5M from cohorts (4/year · 100 each)
                   $500K from workshops (12/year · 30-50 each)

TEAM:              You + 3 full-time + 4 contractors
                   Plus 12 AI engines · running operations

CUSTOMERS:         500 active cohort members
                   1,500 alumni (lifetime)
                   60% retention from cohort to alumni community

PRODUCT:           Workshop · 4 cohort tiers · Slack continuity
                   12 advanced engines beyond the original 5

SYSTEMS:           All operations run by engines · you focus on
                   strategy + content + new launches
```

Re-read every Monday morning · 30 seconds · keep yourself in the
3-year frame.

## PAGE 2 · TRACTION

### a · 1-YEAR PLAN

Same structure as 3-year picture · but for 12 months out.

```
1-YEAR PLAN · April 2027

REVENUE GOAL:      $1.5M ARR
                   - Continuity ($300K)
                   - Cohorts × 4 ($1M)
                   - Workshops × 8 ($200K)

KEY OBJECTIVES (3-7):
1 · 4 cohorts run · 30-50 students each
2 · 8 workshops run · pipeline for cohorts
3 · Slack alumni community · 500 active
4 · Frameworks library expanded · 50+ frameworks
5 · 1 industry partnership signed (affiliate or co-marketing)
```

### b · QUARTERLY ROCKS (3-7)

The next 90 days. The Wildly Important Goals (see
`leadership/wildly-important-goals.md`).

For Purely Personal Q2 2026:

```
1 · 30 paying cohort members (May 3 deadline)
2 · Frameworks library shipped (April 30 deadline)
3 · 50 Slack alumni members (June 30 deadline)
4 · Phase 5 + 6 install videos (June 1)
5 · Affiliate partnership signed (May 30)

Owner: All on Danny (solopreneur · until Q3 hire)
```

Each Rock has a clear owner + deadline. Tracked weekly in the L10.

### c · ISSUES LIST

Running list of issues (problems · obstacles · decisions needed)
that surface during the week. Not all issues get worked on
immediately · they accumulate · then get prioritized in the L10.

```
ISSUES LIST · WEEK OF [date]

1 · Cohort sign-ups stuck at 18/30                  [TOP PRIORITY]
2 · Voice memo outreach test results                [waiting on data]
3 · Newsletter subject lines under-performing       [parked]
4 · Stripe webhook to Notion intermittent            [delegated]
5 · Need to redesign onboarding email flow          [parked]
...
12 · Pricing audit overdue (last done Q4)            [parked · Q3]
```

Each issue gets tagged. Top priority moves to L10 IDS (see
`operations/eos-level-10-meeting.md`).

## How to USE the V/TO

### Daily

Don't look at it. The daily is for execution · not strategy.

### Weekly (Monday morning · 5 minutes)

```
1 · Re-read PAGE 1 (vision) · 60 seconds (just the values + core focus)
2 · Re-read 3-year picture · 30 seconds
3 · Re-read 1-year plan · 30 seconds
4 · Review Rocks status · 60 seconds
5 · Look at top issue on the list · 30 seconds (in case L10 needed)
```

5 minutes. Aligns the week BEFORE the work starts.

### Monthly (last Friday · 15 minutes)

Update the 1-year plan based on actuals.
Review Rocks · which are at risk?
Add new issues · prune resolved ones.

### Quarterly (last Friday of quarter · 90 minutes)

Full review:
- Did we hit Rocks? (yes/no per Rock)
- Update 1-year plan
- Set new quarterly Rocks
- Review marketing strategy · still right?
- Review 3-year picture · still aspirational + plausible?
- Update if needed

### Annually (early January · half-day)

Full vision refresh:
- Core values still right?
- Core focus still right?
- 10-year BHAG still right?
- Re-write 3-year picture from scratch
- Set 1-year plan from new 3-year picture

## V/TO as the alignment tool

When a team member (or you yourself) asks "should we do X?":

```
1 · Does X fit our CORE FOCUS?           Yes/No
2 · Does X fit our 1-YEAR PLAN?           Yes/No
3 · Does X advance a current ROCK?        Yes/No
4 · Or is X just NOISE?
```

If yes to 2-3 of those · do it.
If yes to only 1 · question if it's worth it.
If no to all · don't.

The V/TO becomes the filter. Saves you from "shiny object syndrome."

## The 1-question diagnostic

Once a quarter · ask:

> "If I disappeared for 6 months · could the team execute on this
> V/TO without me?"

If no · the V/TO is in your head, not on paper. Write it down.
Communicate it. Make it the team's source of truth.

If yes · you've installed leadership at the document level. The
business runs without you · because the strategy doesn't depend on
you being in every meeting.

The V/TO is the leadership artifact. 2 pages. Re-read weekly.
Updated quarterly. Communicates the business better than any 50-page
strategy doc.
