# The Eisenhower Matrix · 4-Quadrant Prioritization

> Not a productivity hack. A philosophy. Built around two questions
> that decide where every minute of your day goes.

---

## The 2 questions

```
1 · Is this URGENT?
2 · Is this IMPORTANT?
```

That's it. Two questions. Each task you encounter, ask both. The
answer determines which of 4 quadrants it lives in · which determines
what you do with it.

---

## The 4 quadrants

```
                ┌──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
                │  IMPORTANT &     │  IMPORTANT &     │
                │  URGENT          │  NOT URGENT      │
   IMPORTANT    │                  │                  │
                │  Q1 · DO         │  Q2 · SCHEDULE   │
                │  fires · crises  │  strategy · prep │
                │                  │                  │
                ├──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
                │  NOT IMPORTANT & │  NOT IMPORTANT & │
                │  URGENT          │  NOT URGENT      │
   NOT          │                  │                  │
   IMPORTANT    │  Q3 · DELEGATE   │  Q4 · DELETE     │
                │  interruptions   │  noise · waste   │
                │                  │                  │
                └──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
                  URGENT             NOT URGENT
```

---

## Quadrant 1 · IMPORTANT + URGENT · DO

Crises. Real fires. Hard deadlines.

**Examples:**
- Customer payment dispute · today
- Sales call with $20K prospect at 3pm
- Workshop opens in 4 hours · final tech check
- Critical system down · revenue-impacting

**Rule:** these get done. Today. By you (no one else can do them).

**Trap:** most knowledge workers have NOTHING in Q1 most weeks.
What feels like Q1 is actually Q3 dressed up. Real Q1 is rare.

---

## Quadrant 2 · IMPORTANT + NOT URGENT · SCHEDULE

The single most-important quadrant. Where the future of your
business is built.

**Examples:**
- Building the AI CEO program (Q2 work · ships in Q3 quarter)
- Writing a high-quality piece of content (compounds for years)
- Networking · forming new partnerships
- Health · fitness · sleep
- Strategic planning · quarterly reviews
- Filling out BUSINESS-BRAIN.md properly
- Building frameworks (this directory!)

**Rule:** these get SCHEDULED. Calendar block them. Make them
non-negotiable.

**Why this is the master quadrant:**
- The work in Q2 prevents Q1 fires
- The work in Q2 builds the long-term moat
- The work in Q2 is the only place compounding happens

**80/20 rule:** spend 80% of your time in Q2. The other 20% in Q1.
Almost nothing in Q3/Q4.

---

## Quadrant 3 · NOT IMPORTANT + URGENT · DELEGATE

Other people's emergencies. Interruptions. Things that FEEL urgent
but don't move your business.

**Examples:**
- Most of your unread emails (someone else's deadline)
- Slack pings (their conversation, not your priority)
- "Quick question" requests
- Random meeting invites
- Inbound requests from non-clients
- Someone else's bug report
- Most phone notifications

**Rule:** delegate, defer, or batch. NEVER do these one-off as they
arrive.

**Tactics:**
- **Delegate:** assistant or AI handles them (this is what
  /operations-engine does for inbox)
- **Batch:** check email twice a day, not 30 times
- **Defer:** "I'll get to that next week" (most don't ask again)

**Trap:** Q3 FEELS productive because you're "responsive." But Q3
work doesn't compound. You're just running on someone else's
treadmill.

---

## Quadrant 4 · NOT IMPORTANT + NOT URGENT · DELETE

Pure noise. Waste. Should not exist in your life.

**Examples:**
- Mid-day Twitter scrolling
- Most "industry news" reading
- Reply-all email threads about nothing
- Watching the stock market every hour
- Comparison-scrolling competitors
- Most YouTube algorithmic feeds
- Most podcast bingeing

**Rule:** delete from your life. Block. Unsubscribe. Uninstall.

**Trap:** Q4 disguises itself as Q2 ("I'm researching!" "I'm
learning!"). Test: does this make me MORE effective tomorrow? If no
clear yes · it's Q4.

## Daily triage · the Eisenhower 5-minute morning

Every morning, before opening email/Slack:

```
1 · List today's intended work (5-10 items)
2 · Score each: Urgent? Important?
3 · Plot on the matrix
4 · Q2 first (the master block · 2-4 hours)
5 · Q1 next (only the real fires)
6 · Q3 only IF Q1+Q2 are clear (and only batched)
7 · Q4 = delete/block
```

5 minutes. Done. Now you know what gets your attention.

---

## The BIG TRAP · urgent feels important

Most people confuse URGENT with IMPORTANT. They're not the same.

| Urgent | Important |
|---|---|
| Has a near-term deadline | Has lasting impact |
| Demands immediate response | Compounds over time |
| Created by someone else | Aligned with YOUR goals |
| Solves the present | Builds the future |

**Test:** if I didn't do this thing for 7 days, would my business be
worse off? If yes · IMPORTANT. If no · NOT IMPORTANT (regardless of
how urgent it feels).

## Why most operators get stuck in Q1+Q3

The default state of an unstructured operator:

```
                URGENT             NOT URGENT
              ┌──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
   IMPORTANT  │  Q1 · 30%        │  Q2 · 5%         │
              │  (firefighting)  │  (the gap!)      │
              ├──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
   NOT IMP.   │  Q3 · 50%        │  Q4 · 15%        │
              │  (others' fires) │  (scrolling)     │
              └──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

Most operators spend 80% of their time in Q1+Q3 (urgent stuff). Only
5% in Q2. They wonder why nothing compounds.

**The shift:** invert it. 80% Q2. 15% Q1. 5% Q3. 0% Q4.

Result: business grows. Stress drops. The quarter ends and you
shipped something real.

## The weekly Q2 audit

Every Friday, ask:

```
[ ] How many hours did I spend in Q2 this week?     ___ hrs
[ ] What Q2 work shipped?                            ___
[ ] What Q1 fire would have been preventable by Q2?  ___
[ ] What Q3 work can I delegate next week?           ___
[ ] What Q4 leak do I need to plug?                  ___
```

If the answer to "what Q2 work shipped" is "nothing" · the week
was wasted regardless of how busy you felt.

---

## The 1-line check

When in doubt about whether to do something, ask:

> "Will doing this make me MORE effective in 30 days?"

- Yes, clearly → Q2 → schedule it
- Yes, but it's on fire → Q1 → do it now
- No, but someone else needs it → Q3 → delegate it
- No → Q4 → kill it

That single question, applied 50 times a day, is the difference
between an operator who builds and an operator who reacts.
