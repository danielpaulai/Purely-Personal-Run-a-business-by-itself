# The Level 10 Meeting · 90-Minute Weekly Operating Cadence

> The single most-used weekly meeting framework in operating-team
> environments. Same agenda. Same time. Every week. Forever. The
> difference between teams that ship and teams that drift.

---

## When to use

- You have a team of 2-10 operators
- You have at least 1-3 quarterly priorities (Rocks)
- You're tracking weekly metrics (Scorecard)
- You can commit to 90 minutes / week without fail

For solo operators · run a compressed 30-minute version with yourself
(see "Solo L10" at the bottom).

---

## The agenda · 90 minutes · in order

```
1 · SEGUE              ·  5 min  ·  good news / personal + business
2 · SCORECARD          ·  5 min  ·  weekly numbers · on track or off?
3 · ROCK REVIEW        ·  5 min  ·  quarterly Rocks · on track or off?
4 · CUSTOMER + EMPLOYEE HEADLINES · 5 min · top callouts
5 · TODOS REVIEW       ·  5 min  ·  last week's commitments · done/not done
6 · IDS · ISSUES LIST  · 60 min  ·  identify · discuss · solve issues
7 · CONCLUDE           ·  5 min  ·  recap commitments · rate the meeting
─────────────
TOTAL: 90 min
```

The proportions matter. 60 of the 90 minutes go to ISSUES (#6). Most
teams spend 60 min on STATUS UPDATES (which are just #1-5) and 30
min on issues. That's backwards. Reverse it · running the issues
list IS the meeting.

---

## Segment 1 · SEGUE (5 min)

**Question to each person:** "What's good · personal and business · in
the last week?"

30-60 seconds per person. Goes around the room.

**Why this matters:**
- Transitions everyone OUT of their previous task into the meeting
- Sets a positive tone before getting into hard issues
- Builds team trust through small personal disclosures
- Forces gratitude practice (find SOMETHING good every week)

**Trap:** letting Segue run long. Keep to 5 min total. Don't let it
become a chat.

---

## Segment 2 · SCORECARD (5 min)

Review the team's weekly metrics dashboard. 5-15 metrics max.

**Each metric is one of:**
- ON TRACK · meeting goal
- OFF TRACK · below goal · this becomes an Issue (segment 6)

**The leader reads the scorecard.** Each metric · one word: "On" or
"Off." If off · note it. Don't discuss yet · that's Issues.

**Sample team scorecard:**

```
METRIC                    GOAL         ACTUAL    STATUS
Weekly LinkedIn posts     5            6         ON
Cold DMs sent             40           32        OFF  ← issue
Sales calls booked        10           12        ON
Sales calls held          10           8         OFF  ← issue
Cohort sign-ups (MTD)     30           18        OFF  ← issue
Cash position             $50K floor   $62K      ON
NPS (rolling)             ≥ 50         53        ON
```

3 OFF metrics = 3 candidate issues for segment 6.

**Why this works:**
- Numbers prevent BS
- Pattern recognition (OFF for 3 weeks straight = systemic issue)
- Forces metric thinking

---

## Segment 3 · ROCK REVIEW (5 min)

Review the team's quarterly Rocks (3-7 priorities for the 90-day
quarter).

**Each Rock is:**
- ON TRACK · likely to ship by end of quarter
- OFF TRACK · likely to miss · becomes an Issue

**Sample quarterly Rocks (for Purely Personal, May-July):**

```
ROCK                                          OWNER    STATUS
1 · Ship 4 install-guide videos               Danny    ON
2 · 30 cohort members from May 2-3 workshop   Danny    OFF
3 · Build out frameworks library              Danny    ON
4 · First Slack community: 50 alumni members  Danny    OFF
```

OFF Rocks → Issues list.

---

## Segment 4 · HEADLINES (5 min)

Each person shares ONE customer headline + ONE employee headline.

**Format:** "I had a customer say [...]" or "I noticed [employee] is
[...]"

This surfaces qualitative signals that don't show up in the
Scorecard.

For solopreneurs · skip employee headlines · just customer signals.

---

## Segment 5 · TODOS REVIEW (5 min)

Review last week's commitments (Todos from segment 7 last week).

**Each Todo:** done or not done. No excuses. No context.

If a Todo isn't done:
- It rolls forward to this week (still owned by the same person)
- OR it becomes an Issue (segment 6) if there's a real block

**The 7-day rule:** Todos take ≤ 7 days. If you can't ship it in 7
days, break it into smaller Todos.

---

## Segment 6 · IDS · ISSUES LIST (60 min · the heart of the meeting)

This is where the actual work happens. The other 30 minutes were
setup. This is the 60.

### IDS · Identify · Discuss · Solve

```
1 · IDENTIFY  ·  what's the REAL issue (not the symptom)?
2 · DISCUSS   ·  what do we know · what do we believe · what are options?
3 · SOLVE     ·  what's the decision · who owns the action · by when?
```

### How to run IDS

1. **Build the list** · combine OFF metrics, OFF Rocks, missed Todos,
   plus anything else surfaced during the week. Aim for 5-15 issues.
2. **Prioritize** · the leader picks the top 3 issues. Other 12+ can
   wait.
3. **Tackle them one at a time** · don't multitask issues.
4. **5-15 minutes per issue.**

### The IDS protocol per issue

```
IDENTIFY   ·  90 sec  ·  state the REAL issue (not the symptom)

DISCUSS    ·  3-5 min ·  team contributes
              · what data do we have?
              · what do we believe?
              · what are 2-3 options?

SOLVE      ·  60 sec  ·  decision · owner · deadline
              · "We'll do X · I'll own it · done by Friday."
              → becomes a Todo for next week
```

### The "real issue vs symptom" trap

Most teams discuss SYMPTOMS, not issues.

| Symptom | Real issue |
|---|---|
| "Cold DMs are off this week" | "Our ICP definition is fuzzy · so messaging misses" |
| "Sales calls held is off" | "Calendar sends auto-reschedules · no confirmation flow" |
| "Cohort sign-ups stuck at 18" | "We don't have a follow-up sequence after standing up · post-workshop" |

The leader's job in IDENTIFY: keep asking "but WHY is that
happening?" until you hit the real issue. THEN solve.

---

## Segment 7 · CONCLUDE (5 min)

3 quick things:

### a · Recap todos
Each person reads back their commitment from this meeting.

### b · Cascading messages
What needs to be communicated to the rest of the org / customers /
partners about decisions made today?

### c · Rate the meeting
Each person gives the meeting a 1-10 rating. If anyone says < 8,
WHY?

**Why ratings matter:** if you don't measure meeting quality, it
degrades. The 1-10 rating self-corrects the meeting drift.

## The Issues List (running document)

Keep a single Issues List (Notion page, doc, anything) that runs
across weeks. Add to it AS you encounter issues during the week.
Don't try to remember them in the meeting.

```
ISSUES LIST · WEEK OF 2026-04-27
────────────────────────────────────
1 · Cold DM volume off (32 vs goal 40)              [active]
2 · Sales call show-rate off (8/12 = 67%)            [active]
3 · Cohort sign-ups stuck at 18/30                   [TOP PRIORITY]
4 · Need to redesign the post-workshop email flow    [parked]
5 · Voice memo outreach test results                 [waiting on data]
6 · Tier 2 conversion rate not tracking              [parked]
...
12 · Stripe webhook to Notion is failing             [DELEGATED to assistant]
```

The list is rolling. Most issues stay until solved. Some get parked
(low-pri). Some get delegated outside the L10.

## Solo L10 (compressed for solopreneurs · 30 min)

Same agenda · compressed:

```
1 · WIN     · 1 min · what won this week (1 sentence)
2 · METRICS · 3 min · scorecard pull · ON or OFF per metric
3 · ROCKS   · 2 min · quarterly Rocks status
4 · TODOS   · 2 min · last week's commitments · done?
5 · ISSUES  · 18 min · top 1-3 issues · IDS each
6 · TODOS   · 2 min · this week's commitments
7 · RATING  · 2 min · 1-10 + why
─────────────
TOTAL: 30 min
```

Run with yourself every Monday at 09:00. Calendar block. Notion
template. Don't skip · even when you "don't have time."

## Apply it to /operations-engine

When the engine generates the Friday Wrap (and weekly L10 prep):

1. **Auto-pull metrics** for the Scorecard from connected tools
   (Stripe, Notion, Gmail counts)
2. **Render Rocks status** by checking commitment progress
3. **Generate the Issues List** from this week (off-metrics +
   missed-todos + flagged stuck items)
4. **Suggest top 3 issues** to IDS this week
5. **Save the L10 agenda** to Notion as next Monday's prep doc

When the engine generates Sunday Pipeline Report:

1. Surface 1-2 issues that should hit the Monday L10
2. Format them as "real issue vs symptom" (engine attempts the
   diagnosis)
3. Suggest IDS prep questions for the leader

## Why most teams fail at L10

Top 5 failure modes:

1. **Skipping weeks** · "We're too busy this week" · L10 dies in 6
   weeks
2. **Letting status updates eat the meeting** · 60 min of segments
   1-5 instead of 30 min
3. **Discussing without solving** · IDS becomes ID without the S
4. **No real Issues List** · trying to remember issues in the
   meeting
5. **Vague Todos** · "Look into the email thing" → never done

Avoid these and L10 runs forever.

## The 13-week rhythm

The L10 ties to a 13-week (quarterly) rhythm:

```
Week 1     · Quarterly kickoff · set 1-3 Rocks for the quarter
Weeks 1-12 · Run L10 every week
Week 13    · Quarterly review · close out Rocks · plan next quarter
```

13 weeks = 90 days. Long enough to ship something meaningful. Short
enough to course-correct. The L10 keeps the ship moving · the
quarterly kickoff sets the heading.
