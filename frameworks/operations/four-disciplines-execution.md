# 4 Disciplines of Execution (4DX) · The Operator's Playbook

> The single most-used execution framework in operating-team
> environments. Used across 4,500+ organizations to ship hard things
> consistently. 4 disciplines, in order. Skip one and execution dies.

---

## The 4 disciplines

```
1 · FOCUS on the WILDLY IMPORTANT GOALS (WIGs)
2 · ACT on the LEAD MEASURES
3 · KEEP a COMPELLING SCOREBOARD
4 · CREATE a CADENCE of ACCOUNTABILITY
```

The disciplines are sequential. You can't run #2 without #1. You
can't run #4 without #3. Most operators try to skip to #4 (weekly
meetings) without doing #1-3. That's why their meetings feel
useless.

---

## Discipline 1 · FOCUS on the WILDLY IMPORTANT (WIGs)

**The principle:** the more goals you pursue, the fewer you achieve.

Most teams have 5+ priorities. 5 priorities = 0 priorities.

**The math:**
- 1-3 goals · 80% completion rate
- 4-10 goals · 30% completion rate
- 11-20 goals · 5% completion rate

**Pick 1-3 WIGs.** No more.

### What makes a goal "Wildly Important"?

Three criteria · all must be true:

1. **Significant impact** · if achieved, the business shifts
   meaningfully
2. **Specific & measurable** · numbers + deadline (not "increase
   sales")
3. **Within current capacity** · we can do it without breaking the
   team

### Format · the WIG sentence

```
From [current state] → To [target state] → By [deadline]
```

**Examples:**
- "From $14k MRR → to $30k MRR → by September 30"
- "From inbox at 200 unread → to inbox at zero by 9am daily → by July 1"
- "From 0 paying cohort members → to 30 paying cohort members → by May 3 workshop close"

## Discipline 2 · ACT on the LEAD MEASURES

**Lag measures** = the WIG itself (revenue, conversions, growth)
**Lead measures** = the inputs that PREDICT the lag

You can't ACT on a lag measure. Revenue is a result. You can only act
on what produces revenue.

**Lead measures must be:**
- **Predictive** · doing them moves the lag
- **Influence-able** · the team controls them

### Examples

| WIG (lag) | Bad lead measure | Good lead measure |
|---|---|---|
| $30k MRR by Sept 30 | "Sell more" | "20 sales calls / week" |
| Inbox to zero by 9am | "Be more organized" | "Triage at 8:30 every weekday" |
| 30 cohort members | "Close more" | "10 1-on-1 outreach DMs / day" |

The lead measure is what you DO. The lag measure is what you GET.

### The 80/20 rule for lead measures

For every WIG, identify the ONE lead measure that produces 80% of
the result. Drop the others. Track only that one.

For Purely Personal · "30 cohort members from May 2-3 workshop":
- Lead measure: "5 personalized 1:1 DMs to attendees per day · between
  workshop end and Tuesday cohort kickoff"

That's it. ONE lead measure. ONE behavior. The rest follows.

## Discipline 3 · KEEP a COMPELLING SCOREBOARD

**The principle:** people play differently when keeping score.

**Bad scoreboards:**
- A spreadsheet only the manager sees
- Numbers buried in dashboards
- "Trust me, we're doing great"

**Good scoreboards:**
- Visible at a glance
- Shows lead measure + lag measure side by side
- Updated daily or weekly · NOT monthly
- Anyone can tell at a glance: are we winning or losing?

### The 3 rules of compelling scoreboards

1. **Simple** · understood in 5 seconds by someone who hasn't seen it
2. **Visible** · physical or digital · always in view
3. **Shows winning vs losing** · not just current state · target line clear

### Worked example for AI CEO cohort goal:

```
WIG: 30 cohort members by May 3 13:00

LEAD MEASURE: 1:1 DMs sent today
[●●●●●○] 5/8 sent today
[ ▓▓▓▓▓░░ ] 25/40 sent this week (target 8/day × 5 days)

LAG MEASURE: Cohort sign-ups
[ ▓▓▓░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░ ] 3/30 (10%)

LAST UPDATED: Today 14:32
NEXT REVIEW: Tomorrow 09:00
```

That single graphic, on a wall or pinned in Slack, drives more
behavior change than any dashboard.

## Discipline 4 · CREATE a CADENCE of ACCOUNTABILITY

**The principle:** WIGs that aren't reviewed weekly don't ship.

**The structure:** weekly 20-30 min meeting. Same time. Same place.
Same agenda. Every week without fail.

### The 4DX weekly meeting agenda (the L4 meeting)

```
1 · ACCOUNT  ·  5 min  ·  what did each person commit last week ·
                          did they do it?
2 · REVIEW   ·  5 min  ·  scoreboard · are we winning or losing?
3 · PLAN     · 10 min  ·  what does each person commit to this week?
4 · CLEAR    ·  5 min  ·  what's blocking? what help is needed?
─────────────────────
TOTAL: 25 min
```

### Account · the most important segment

Each person reports on what they committed LAST week. Two answers
allowed:

- **"Yes · I did it."**
- **"No · I didn't do it."**

NO excuses. NO context. NO "well, here's what happened..."

The brutal honesty is the entire point. 4DX dies when meetings turn
into excuse-sharing.

### Plan · the commitment

Each person commits to ONE action this week that will move the lead
measure. ONE. Not five. Not "I'll work on a few things."

The commitment must be:
- Specific
- Within their control
- Visible to the team
- Reportable next week with a clear yes/no

**Example commitment:** "I'll send 8 personalized DMs to workshop
attendees by Friday at 17:00."

### Clear · what's blocking?

The accountability + plan loops are the work. Clear is the relief
valve. If someone CAN'T deliver on their commitment because of a
block, name it · let the team unblock.

## Solo-operator 4DX (for solopreneurs)

If you're solo, you still need the structure. Run a weekly meeting
WITH YOURSELF.

```
EVERY MONDAY · 09:00 · 25 min · "the L4"

1 · ACCOUNT  · review last week's commitment · honest yes/no
2 · REVIEW   · update scoreboard · pull real numbers
3 · PLAN     · commit to ONE thing for this week
4 · CLEAR    · what's blocking? · what help do you need?
            · book the call / send the DM / order the thing
```

Calendar invite to yourself. Don't skip. Even if it feels silly.

## How 4DX scales as you grow

**Solo (just you):** weekly L4 with yourself · 25 min · Monday morning

**You + VA / contractor:** weekly L4 (45 min) · scoreboard in
Notion · both report

**Team of 3-10:** team L4 (30 min) · individual lead measures · one
shared scoreboard

**Team of 10+:** rolling L4s by department · departmental
scoreboards rolling up to a company scoreboard

The discipline scales perfectly. Most operators break it by adding
more goals as they grow · 4DX would say add more L4s, not more goals
per L4.

---

## Why 4DX dies in most companies

Top 5 failure modes:

1. **Too many WIGs** · "we have 7 priorities" · = 0 priorities
2. **Lag measures only** · tracking revenue, not the inputs · = no
   action handle
3. **Hidden scoreboard** · only manager sees it · = team doesn't play
   to win
4. **Skipped L4s** · "we're too busy this week" · = WIGs die
5. **Excuse-laden Account segment** · soft accountability · = no
   pressure to ship

Avoid these 5 and 4DX runs forever.
