# 90-Day Cash Flow Forecast · Rolling Weekly

> Most businesses run their finances on lagging indicators (last
> month's P&L). The 90-day rolling forecast is the LEADING indicator
> · what's about to happen. Update weekly. Adjust monthly. Survive.

---

## Why 90 days (not 30 · not 12)

**30 days is too short:**
- Doesn't capture quarterly cycles (taxes · cohort launches)
- Can't see crisis approaching far enough to react

**12 months is too long:**
- Too many assumptions stack up · variance compounds
- Updates feel pointless because everything changes

**90 days is the sweet spot:**
- Captures one full quarter
- Short enough to forecast accurately
- Long enough to see major events approaching (tax day, cohort
  start, end-of-quarter cash crunch)

---

## The structure · 13 weeks · cash IN and OUT

```
        WEEK 1  WEEK 2  WEEK 3  WEEK 4  ...  WEEK 13
        ─────   ─────   ─────   ─────        ──────
CASH IN:
  Workshop sales
  AI CEO sales
  Continuity subs
  Other revenue
  ────────────
  Total IN

CASH OUT:
  Salary (owner pay)
  Software / SaaS
  Contractors
  Marketing
  Tax allocation
  Other
  ────────────
  Total OUT

NET CASH FLOW:
  IN - OUT       (per week)

CUMULATIVE BALANCE:
  Start balance + cumulative net  (per week)
```

The cumulative balance line is the critical row. It tells you when
you'll run out of cash · or when you'll have surplus to invest.

---

## What goes in CASH IN

### a · Recurring revenue
Subscriptions, retainers, continuity. Predictable. Low forecasting
error.

For Purely Personal:
- Slack community at $97/month × X members
- Annual subscriptions paid quarterly

### b · Forecasted new sales
Based on pipeline + close rate.

```
Forecasted new revenue = (qualified pipeline value) × (close rate)
```

**Example:**
- Pipeline this week: 8 prospects at AI CEO ($2,300 avg)
- Close rate: 25%
- Forecasted: 8 × $2,300 × 0.25 = $4,600 expected this week

Be conservative. Use BANT-qualified pipeline only · not "interested"
contacts.

### c · One-time projects
Workshops, launches, consulting projects with set close dates.

For Purely Personal:
- Next workshop: $49 × 30 attendees = $1,470 on Saturday May 2

### d · Other income
Affiliate revenue, royalties, partnerships.

---

## What goes in CASH OUT

### a · Fixed costs (predictable · weekly or monthly)
- Owner salary (per Profit First system)
- Software subscriptions
- Long-term contractor retainers
- Rent / office (if applicable)
- Insurance

### b · Variable costs (depends on volume)
- Stripe fees (% of revenue)
- Per-customer delivery costs
- Marketing spend (variable based on campaign)
- Per-call contractor costs

### c · One-time costs
- Annual subscriptions (e.g., Notion · Loom)
- Quarterly tax payments (estimated)
- Big purchases (equipment, software upgrades)
- Legal · accounting · annual fees

### d · Profit First transfers
- Profit account allocation
- Owner pay above salary (quarterly distribution)

## The critical line · CUMULATIVE BALANCE

Every week, calculate:

```
Cumulative balance (Week N) = Starting cash
                            + Sum of net cash flow weeks 1-N
```

Plot this line. The shape tells you everything:

**Healthy shape:**
```
Cumulative
 balance
   │
   │       ╱╲
   │     ╱╲  ╲╱
   │   ╱╲   ╲   ╲╱╲
   │ ╱╲ ╲    ╲    ╲╲
   ├──────────────────────
                       Time
```

Up and to the right with breathing room above zero. Some bumps
(tax payments, slow weeks) but trending up.

**Warning shape:**
```
Cumulative
 balance
   │
   │       ╱╲╲
   │     ╱╲  ╲╱╲╲
   │   ╱╲   ╲    ╲╱╲╲
   │ ╱╲ ╲    ╲      ╲╱
   ├──────────────────────
                       Time
                        ↑
                        runs out around week 10
```

The line approaches or crosses zero. This is the warning · 4-6 weeks
before the crisis.

## The actionable thresholds

Set 3 thresholds on the cumulative balance line:

```
GREEN  · > 3 months operating expenses    · healthy
YELLOW · 1-3 months operating expenses    · attention
RED    · < 1 month operating expenses     · crisis
```

When balance enters YELLOW · cut variable costs + accelerate
collections.

When balance enters RED · all hands · raise capital · cut
contractors · stop new spend · close deals fast.

When balance is GREEN · invest the surplus (Profit First
distribution + war chest growth + new bets).

## How to update the forecast (weekly · 30 minutes)

Every Friday afternoon:

```
1 · UPDATE actuals for the past week
   - What actually came IN?
   - What actually went OUT?
   - Reconcile vs forecast (variance)

2 · UPDATE pipeline forecast for next 13 weeks
   - Add new prospects
   - Remove dead ones
   - Update close-date assumptions

3 · UPDATE expense forecast
   - Add new known costs (annual renewals coming)
   - Adjust variable costs based on planned activity

4 · RECALCULATE cumulative balance
   - Plot the line
   - Check thresholds

5 · NOTE actions
   - YELLOW alerts · what to cut
   - GREEN surplus · what to invest in
```

30 minutes. Every Friday. Done.

## Forecast accuracy · expect 20-30% variance

Your forecast WILL be wrong. The point isn't precision · it's
visibility.

**Targets:**
- Week 1-2: ±10% (you can be quite precise short-term)
- Week 3-4: ±20%
- Week 5-8: ±30%
- Week 9-13: ±50% (long horizon · pipeline assumptions stack)

If you're MORE than 30% off in week 1-2 · your forecasting needs
work. Specifically: your pipeline conversion assumptions are
fantasy.

---

## What to do when forecast goes wrong

### Variance is GOOD (better than forecast)
- Don't celebrate · audit. Why was the forecast low?
- If pipeline closed faster · increase close-rate assumption
- If a one-time event happened · don't repeat it in the model
- Take the surplus to PROFIT account

### Variance is BAD (worse than forecast)
- Run the post-mortem THIS WEEK · don't wait
- Close 1 specific deal that slipped (call them today)
- Cut 1 variable cost that's recoverable (pause an ad campaign)
- Push 1 expense to next month (annual renewal · negotiate)

The goal isn't to never have variance. The goal is to RESPOND fast
when you do.

## The 1-question diagnostic

Every Friday, ask:

> "If I do nothing different · how much cash will I have in 90 days?"

The answer should be in the forecast. If you don't know · you're
flying blind. The 90-day rolling forecast is the instrument.
