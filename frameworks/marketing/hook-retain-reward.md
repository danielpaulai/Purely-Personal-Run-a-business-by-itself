# Hook · Retain · Reward · The Algorithmic Content Principle

> Every social platform's algorithm rewards the same 3 things in the
> same order. Master this and the algorithm becomes a megaphone. Miss
> any one and the post dies in 6 minutes.

---

## The 3 phases of every post

```
HOOK   ·  earn the open       ·  the first 3 seconds / 210 chars
RETAIN ·  earn the read       ·  the body · keep them scrolling
REWARD ·  earn the share/save ·  the close · pay them off
```

Each platform weights these slightly differently, but all three matter
on every platform.

---

## 1 · HOOK · earn the open (first 3 seconds)

The platform decides whether to show your post to MORE people based
on what happens in the first 3 seconds. If they scroll past in <2
seconds, your reach is capped.

**What kills hooks:**
- Generic openings · "Today I want to talk about..."
- Slow builds · "There are 5 things you should know..."
- Throat-clearing · "Just had a great conversation with..."
- Vague specificity · "I helped a client recently..."

**What works:**
- Specific number in line 1 · "$47k in 9 days"
- Pattern interrupt · "Stop building 'high-ticket offers'"
- Implied stake · "If you're scrolling LinkedIn at 11pm, you have a marketing problem"
- Question your audience already asked themselves · "What if your business could run for 7 days without you?"

See `linkedin-hooks.md` for 7 working hook archetypes.

**Algorithm signal:** dwell time on the hook. If they pause for 3+
seconds reading line 1-2, the algorithm shows it to more people.

---

## 2 · RETAIN · earn the read (the body)

The body's job is to keep them moving. NOT to make them think. NOT to
make them admire. Just to scroll DOWN, not AWAY.

**Retention principles:**

### a · Short paragraphs (1-3 lines max)
Walls of text kill retention. Even brilliant ones.

### b · Open loops every 3-4 paragraphs
Plant a question at paragraph 4 that doesn't get answered until
paragraph 7. The reader's brain auto-pulls them down to close the
loop.

Example:
```
... [paragraph 3]

But there was one thing I missed completely.        ← OPEN LOOP

[paragraph 5-6 deliver setup]

The thing I missed: [resolution]                     ← CLOSE LOOP
```

### c · Specificity stack
Every paragraph should add a number, a name, a screenshot, a date.
Generic body = abandoned read. Specific body = compulsive read.

- Bad: "It really helped me grow my business"
- Good: "On March 14 I sent the first one. By March 27 we'd booked 8 calls. 3 closed by April 2."

### d · The "scrolling rhythm"
Mix sentence lengths. Short. Then medium-length sentence. Then a
really long sentence that builds in clauses and lets the reader's eye
flow downward through phrases that pile up the picture. Then short.

### e · The visual break
On LinkedIn: use line breaks, dashes, or numbered lists every 4-5
lines. Visual rest = retention extension.

**Algorithm signal:** scroll depth + scroll velocity. If readers reach
the bottom (and especially if they slow down at the bottom), the
algorithm boosts.

---

## 3 · REWARD · earn the share/save (the close)

This is the most-skipped phase. Most creators end with a CTA. Wrong.
End with a REWARD first · CTA second.

**What's a reward?**
The dopamine hit. The aha. The "I'm going to screenshot this" moment.

**4 reward patterns:**

### a · The summary insight
Compress everything you said into ONE line. The reader can quote it.
- "The system you build is the life you live."
- "Most marketing problems are positioning problems in disguise."

### b · The unexpected reframe
End with something that recasts the whole post.
- "But here's the thing: this isn't a productivity problem. It's a self-respect problem."

### c · The screenshot-able list
End with a numbered list of takeaways from the post.
- "Recap:
  1. Hook earns the open
  2. Retain earns the read
  3. Reward earns the share
  Save this."

### d · The provocative question
- "What's the ONE recurring decision you're going to remove this week?"

**Algorithm signal:** saves > shares > comments > likes. A save tells
the algorithm "this person wants to see this AGAIN" — strongest signal
of all. A reward-driven close earns saves.

---

## The CTA · what comes AFTER the reward

CTA is separate from the reward. Reward earns the share. CTA earns
the lead.

**CTA placement:**
- LinkedIn: P.S. line at the bottom · separated from the reward
- X thread: last tweet · always
- Newsletter: end of body · before the sign-off
- Long-form (sales page, blog): top + middle + bottom

**CTA rules:**
- ONE per post (multiple = none chosen = scroll past)
- Action verb + low effort
- Match CTA energy to post energy

```
HIGH-VIBE post → "Comment 'autopilot' for the playbook"
HEAVY post     → "DM me if this is you"
TACTICAL post  → "Save this thread for Monday morning"
PERSONAL post  → no CTA (or just "thanks for reading")
```

## The 6-minute rule

Every social platform's algorithm makes its decision about your post
in the first 6 minutes after you publish.

**The first 6 minutes determine 90% of the post's reach.**

What earns reach in those 6 minutes:
- 5+ comments (algorithm bait #1)
- Long dwell time on the hook
- Saves + shares (less common but high-weight)

**What kills reach:**
- Posting and ghosting (no engagement = dead)
- Posting in a low-engagement window (early morning · weekend nights)
- Generic content that triggers the AI-detection heuristic

**The 6-minute protocol:**
1. Pre-stage 3-5 friends/colleagues to comment within 5 min of post
2. Reply to every comment within 5 min (signals "alive")
3. Cross-post to your other channels to drive traffic back
4. Don't edit (re-edits flag the post)

---

## The retention curve

For longer posts (carousels, threads, newsletters), audiences typically
drop off in this pattern:

```
100% read line 1
 80% read line 3-5
 50% reach paragraph 4
 25% reach paragraph 7
 10% reach the end
  3% take action on the CTA
```

Your job is to flatten that curve. The first 5 lines determine what
% reach paragraph 4. Spend 50% of your edit time there.

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When the engine generates a post, it must:

1. Score the hook against the 3-second test (specific? interrupt?
   stake?)
2. Audit retention (open loop? short paragraphs? specificity?)
3. Generate a reward line (NOT a CTA) before the CTA
4. Add the CTA as a separate line (P.S. or last paragraph)
5. Output a "6-minute checklist" for the user to execute when posting

Default output structure:

```
HOOK     · 1-2 lines
[blank]
BODY     · 4-6 short paragraphs · 1 open loop · specifics
[blank]
REWARD   · 1 line · summary insight or reframe
[blank]
P.S./CTA · 1 line · single action
```
