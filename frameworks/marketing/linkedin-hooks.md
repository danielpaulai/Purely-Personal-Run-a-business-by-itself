# LinkedIn Hook Library · 7 Archetypes That Work

> Real hook patterns that produce real engagement. Each archetype
> includes the structure, 3 working templates, and 5 example openings
> you can adapt to any business.

**The rule of 210 chars:** LinkedIn truncates posts at "...see more"
around 210 characters. Your hook must complete the sale BEFORE the
cutoff. If your hook ends mid-thought, you've lost.

---

## Archetype 1 · PERSONAL PROOF

You did something specific. Numbers. Time-boxed.

**Structure:** [I/We] + [specific action] + [specific result] + [specific time]

**Templates:**
- "I [verb] [number] in [time]. Here's exactly how."
- "We went from [X] to [Y] in [time]. Here's what shifted."
- "I [achievement] without [common method]. Here's the playbook."

**Examples (adapt the structure):**
1. "I 10x'd my pipeline in 7 days. Here's exactly how."
2. "I closed $30k in 14 days with one cold DM template."
3. "We grew from 0 to $20k MRR in 90 days. No paid ads."
4. "I built a business that runs without me in 4 hours."
5. "I stopped doing sales calls in March. Revenue went up 40%."

When to use: you have receipts. Real numbers. Don't fake this · the
internet will check.

---

## Archetype 2 · QUIET TRUTH

State the unspoken truth your audience already feels.

**Structure:** Most [X] don't have [common diagnosis]. They have [real diagnosis].

**Templates:**
- "Most [X] don't have a [Y] problem. They have a [Z] problem."
- "[X] isn't the bottleneck. [Y] is."
- "The reason [X] doesn't work isn't [Y]. It's [Z]."

**Examples:**
1. "Most founders don't have a marketing problem. They have a writing problem."
2. "Your inbox isn't full because you get too much email. It's full because you don't decide."
3. "The reason most cold DMs don't work isn't volume. It's qualification."
4. "Most coaches don't fail because of pricing. They fail because of positioning."
5. "If your content isn't converting, it's not a copy problem. It's a clarity problem."

When to use: pattern-interrupt for an audience that's seen too many
generic takes. Reframes a tired problem.

---

## Archetype 3 · STAT CONTRAST

Two numbers. One unexpected. The contrast IS the hook.

**Structure:** [Big number]. [Small number]. [The pivot that explains it].

**Templates:**
- "[$X] in [Y]. [$Z] in [contrasting metric]. Here's what changed."
- "[N] hours of [activity]. [M] hours of [activity]. Same result."
- "[Big number]. [Small number]. One thing connects them."

**Examples:**
1. "$2M in revenue. $0 in marketing spend. One thing changed."
2. "47 sales calls last quarter. 3 last week. Same revenue."
3. "$10k/mo in ads. $40 in coffee. The coffee outperformed."
4. "8 hours of content per week. 80 hours of replies. Where's the leverage?"
5. "100 cold emails. 1 reply. 1 close. Math still works."

When to use: when you have data that defies expectation. Don't
fabricate the contrast — it must be real.

## Archetype 4 · CONTRARIAN

Stake a position against the herd. Make a defendable claim that 80%
of your audience disagrees with.

**Structure:** Stop [common practice]. Start [your reframe].

**Templates:**
- "Stop [doing X]. Start [doing Y]."
- "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why."
- "The reason [thing everyone does] doesn't work."

**Examples:**
1. "Stop building 'high-ticket coaching offers.' Build cash-flowing assets instead."
2. "Cold email is dead. Cold DMs work. Here's the difference."
3. "Your morning routine isn't broken. Your evening routine is."
4. "Niching down is bad advice. Niche UP."
5. "Selling on LinkedIn is dead. Building on LinkedIn is alive."

When to use: when you have an opinion you're willing to lose
followers over. Weak contrarian = bait. Strong contrarian = magnet.

---

## Archetype 5 · HOW-TO

Pure utility hook. Promise a specific outcome with a specific method.

**Structure:** How to [outcome] in [time/condition] without [common method].

**Templates:**
- "How to [outcome] in [X] minutes."
- "How to [outcome] without [hated method]."
- "[N]-step process to [outcome]."

**Examples:**
1. "How to write a LinkedIn post that gets 100k views in 7 minutes."
2. "How to qualify a prospect in 4 questions before any sales call."
3. "5-step process to triple your reply rate on cold DMs."
4. "How to onboard a client without a single Zoom call."
5. "How to clear your inbox to zero in 9 minutes flat."

When to use: top-of-funnel content. Saves and shares. Highest reach
archetype.

---

## Archetype 6 · MISTAKE/REGRET

Show vulnerability. Name the cost. Then deliver the lesson.

**Structure:** I [mistake] for [time]. Here's what I'd do differently.

**Templates:**
- "I wasted [time] on [mistake]. Here's what I'd do today."
- "I lost [amount] before I figured out [lesson]."
- "[X] mistake I made [Y] times before I learned [Z]."

**Examples:**
1. "I wasted 18 months building the wrong product. Here's what I'd do differently."
2. "I lost $40k on Facebook ads before I understood my ICP."
3. "I sent 1,200 cold emails before I figured out my hook was wrong."
4. "3 mistakes I made running my first cohort. All preventable."
5. "I undercharged by 80% for 2 years. Here's how I fixed pricing."

When to use: builds trust like nothing else. Especially powerful when
you're known as the expert · vulnerability earns you the right to
lead.

---

## Archetype 7 · QUESTION

A real question your audience is asking themselves at 11pm.

**Structure:** What if [counterintuitive premise]?

**Templates:**
- "What if [common assumption] is backwards?"
- "Why do [audience] keep [doing X] when [Y]?"
- "What does [outcome] actually require?"

**Examples:**
1. "What if your business could run for 7 days without you · and grow?"
2. "Why do solopreneurs keep building tools they could buy in an afternoon?"
3. "What does it actually take to charge $5k for a workshop?"
4. "What if your content is fine and your CTA is what's broken?"
5. "Why do some founders 10x while others plateau · same niche, same offer?"

When to use: opens curiosity loops. Especially good as the FIRST line
of a longer thought piece. Not great for hard CTAs.

## Hook stacking · combine 2 archetypes

Strongest hooks combine TWO archetypes:

- **Personal proof + Stat contrast** · "$0 in marketing. $30k MRR. Here's how."
- **Quiet truth + Contrarian** · "Most founders don't have a marketing problem. Stop fixing it."
- **Mistake + How-to** · "I wasted 18 months. Here's the 4-step fix that saved me."
- **Question + Personal proof** · "What if you could run a business in 4 hours? I do."

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When generating 3 LinkedIn variants, the engine MUST use 3 DIFFERENT
archetypes. Same archetype × 3 = three variations of the same post.
Different archetype × 3 = three angles · giving the user real choice.

Default rotation:
- Variant A · Personal Proof + Stat Contrast
- Variant B · Quiet Truth or Contrarian
- Variant C · How-to or Question

## Voice rules (enforced by validate_voice)

- 0 em-dashes · use period or middle-dot ·
- 0 banned phrases · "unlock" "delve" "supercharge" "seamless" "elevate" "leverage" "synergy" "ecosystem" "harness" "embark" "navigate" "robust" "comprehensive" "tapestry" "intricate" "vibrant"
- Hook completes within first 210 characters
- 150-350 words per post
- ONE CTA per post
- Numbers are specific · "$30k" not "lots of revenue"

## The grade-your-hook checklist

Before publishing any hook, score it 0-3 on each:

```
[ ] /3 · Specific (numbers, names, dates)
[ ] /3 · Pattern interrupt (breaks expectation)
[ ] /3 · Stake clear (what's at risk if I scroll past?)
[ ] /3 · Under 210 chars

Total: ___ /12

If under 9, rewrite.
```
