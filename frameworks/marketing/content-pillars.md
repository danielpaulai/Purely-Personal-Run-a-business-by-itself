# Content Pillars · The 3-Pillar Authority System

> Most creators post randomly. The ones who BUILD authority pick 3
> pillars and post against them every week, every quarter, every year.
> Three pillars × consistency = compounding authority.

---

## The 3-pillar structure

Every business should have **exactly 3 content pillars.** Not 5.
Not 7. Three. Why three?

- 3 is what an audience can remember (4 starts to blur)
- 3 lets you balance Hard / Soft / Personal (see below)
- 3 = enough variety to not get boring · little enough to compound

```
PILLAR 1 · HARD VALUE       ·  what you teach
PILLAR 2 · SOFT POSITIONING ·  who you are
PILLAR 3 · PERSONAL STORY   ·  why anyone should care
```

---

## Pillar 1 · Hard Value (40% of posts)

What you teach. Tactics. Frameworks. How-tos. Lists. Tools.

This pillar earns saves and shares. Algorithm-friendly. Builds
search-able authority.

**Sub-categories:**
- "How to ___" tutorials
- "X mistakes" lists
- Tool reviews + recommendations
- Framework explanations
- Case studies with numbers

**Example titles (for Purely Personal):**
- "How to set up a Monday Content Drop in 4 minutes"
- "5 LinkedIn hook patterns that beat my last 50 posts"
- "The 4-question script I use to qualify any lead"
- "Why I switched from ChatGPT to Claude Code"

**Voice for Hard Value:**
- Numbered lists work
- Step-by-step structure
- Specific numbers
- Slightly less personality · more utility

---

## Pillar 2 · Soft Positioning (35% of posts)

Your opinions. Hot takes. Reframes. Things you believe that 80% of
your industry doesn't.

This pillar builds your moat. People don't follow you for tactics
(they can get those anywhere). They follow you for your TASTE.

**Sub-categories:**
- Contrarian takes
- Industry observations
- Reframes of common beliefs
- "Stop doing X · start doing Y"
- Predictions / trend takes

**Example titles:**
- "Stop building 'high-ticket coaching offers'"
- "The reason cold email is dead and cold DMs aren't"
- "AI isn't replacing creators · it's replacing creators who don't think"
- "Niching down is bad advice. Niche UP."

**Voice for Soft Positioning:**
- First-person opinion
- Stake a position
- Defend it (or invite challenge)
- Be willing to lose followers · the ones who stay are the right ones

---

## Pillar 3 · Personal Story (25% of posts)

Your origin. Failures. Journey. Day-in-the-life. Family. Hobbies.
The "why anyone cares about your tactics" content.

This pillar builds the parasocial relationship that converts content
into clients. Without it, you're a tactics-output machine. With it,
you're a person.

**Sub-categories:**
- Founder origin moments
- Failures + lessons
- Behind-the-scenes
- Travel / family / non-work life
- "What I'm thinking about right now"
- Reflection posts (annual review · quarter recap)

**Example titles:**
- "I wasted 18 months on the wrong product. Here's what I'd do differently."
- "Sunday morning. Coffee. Watching the kids. My business runs without me."
- "I almost quit in March. Here's what changed."
- "Behind the workshop · 14 hours of prep for one 60-minute pitch."

**Voice for Personal Story:**
- Vulnerable
- Specific moments (Sunday morning. Coffee. The exact thought.)
- No moral · let the reader extract their own
- Lower CTA energy (or no CTA at all)

## The weekly rhythm

If you post 3-5x per week, balance like this:

| Day | Pillar | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Hard Value (tutorial) | Audience is in "let's get to work" mode |
| **Tuesday** | Soft Positioning | Mid-week reframe lands well |
| **Wednesday** | Newsletter (your hand-picked format) | Long-form weekly anchor |
| **Thursday** | Hard Value (case study) | Decision-maker ahead of weekend |
| **Friday** | Personal Story | Friday energy is reflective |
| **Saturday** | Off (or carousel) | Lower engagement day |
| **Sunday** | Soft Positioning OR Personal | Sunday is for thinking |

This rhythm produces ~5 LinkedIn posts + 1 newsletter = 6 weekly
content drops. Matches the 6-routine system in the install guide.

## Choosing your 3 pillars

Each business has different pillars. Here's the matrix to find yours:

```
HARD VALUE pillar = What you'd teach if someone paid you $5,000.
                    What's the framework you'd defend in a debate?

SOFT POSITIONING = What's the contrarian belief you'd put on a t-shirt?
                   What does 80% of your industry get wrong?

PERSONAL STORY  = What's the moment that explains why you do this?
                   What's the failure that earned the right to lead?
```

For Purely Personal, the 3 pillars might be:

1. **HARD VALUE** · "Building businesses that run without you using AI"
2. **SOFT POSITIONING** · "AI is replacing operators, not strategists"
3. **PERSONAL STORY** · "From 80-hour weeks to 4-day work weeks · the journey"

Once you pick · STICK to them for at least 90 days. Authority
compounds. Pillar-hopping resets the clock.

---

## The pillar audit · run quarterly

Every 90 days, audit your last 30 posts:

```
[ ] How many posts in Pillar 1 (target: 40%)?
[ ] How many in Pillar 2 (target: 35%)?
[ ] How many in Pillar 3 (target: 25%)?
[ ] Which pillar got the highest engagement?
[ ] Which pillar drove the most DMs / leads?
[ ] Which pillar is missing?
```

Most creators are 80%+ Hard Value · 0% Personal Story. They wonder
why their audience doesn't convert. The math is the answer.

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When the engine generates 3 LinkedIn variants, it should consult the
USER's pillars (saved in BUSINESS-BRAIN.md) and:

1. Auto-detect which pillar the topic falls into
2. Generate the post in the voice of that pillar
3. If the user has been over-indexing on one pillar this week, suggest
   a different pillar's topic for variant 2 or 3
4. Track pillar distribution in the brain (running tally)

The engine should ALSO ask once during BUSINESS-BRAIN.md setup:
"What are your 3 content pillars? Hard value: ___ · Soft positioning:
___ · Personal story: ___"

Save to brain. Reference forever.
