# Post 004 · LinkedIn is the worst place for ideas

**Hook archetype:** Contrarian Reframe · "X is the worst place for Y. Because Z."
**Topic:** Content idea sourcing · Reddit > LinkedIn for problem-mining
**Format:** Process walkthrough · how I do it
**Length:** ~290 words

## RAW POST · paste exactly as written

LinkedIn is the worst place for fresh content ideas.
Because it only shows what already worked yesterday.

Yeah. So let me share how I actually find content ideas.

And it is not by scrolling LinkedIn.
That's the worst place to look.

Because LinkedIn only shows what worked yesterday.
By the time you see it, it's already done and reposted by someone else.

The thing is, I don't want ideas.
I want the problems of my clients.

That's why I spend time on Reddit.

People don't posture there.
They complain and ask for help when something is stuck.

And that's where the real content lives.

I'll open Grok and ask it to find subreddits where my audience hangs out.

Then I jump into those threads and search for phrases like
"I'm struggling with" or "I can't stand."

What shows up isn't theory.
It's frustration, raw and specific.

Now here's the important part.

I don't touch AI yet.

I first read.
I copied the post.
Then I read the comments.

Because the comments tell you what they tried and what failed.

And what they're tired of hearing.

Only after that, I bring AI in.

I paste everything into Claude and ask it to shape ideas around those problems and organize what's already there without invention from scratch

Then I validate with Grok again.
To see what angles are missing, not what's popular.

That's it.

You have to mine problems, not the random posts.

PS: Where are you looking for ideas instead of listening to problems?
