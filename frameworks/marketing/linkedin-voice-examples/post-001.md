## RAW POST · paste exactly as written

I stopped using a design tool for every small edit.
Because you can fix them directly inside ChatGPT.

Yeah. This is something interesting I've been experimenting with.

ChatGPT can now connect straight to Adobe Photoshop, inside the same chat.

You upload an image, type what you want changed, and it edits it there.

No switching tabs. No back-and-forth with a designer for small fixes.
No opening three tools just to change one thing.

Here's what I want you to do.

Open ChatGPT, go to Settings, then Apps & Connectors, and connect Photoshop.
Once it shows as connected, you're ready.

After that, you upload your image and type @AdobePhotoshop in the chat.

That's how you tell ChatGPT to use it.
From there, it's just about being clear with what you ask.

If your prompt is vague, the output will be vague.
If you say exactly what you want, you follow it properly.

What I like is that you're not stuck redoing everything.
When the visual is close but not quite there, you get sliders to adjust things like detail, intensity, and style. You tweak it a bit, see the preview, and move on.

This matters if you post carousels on LinkedIn.
The ideas are usually not the problem.

The friction is waiting for edits, fixing small design things, and losing momentum.
This workflow cuts that down. You can get clean, consistent visuals without slowing yourself down.

If carousels are part of your content, this is worth trying once and seeing how it feels.

P.S. Have you tried this Photoshop + ChatGPT workflow yet?
