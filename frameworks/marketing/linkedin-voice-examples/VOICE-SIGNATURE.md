## THE 10 NON-NEGOTIABLE VOICE RULES

These are not preferences. They are pattern-matched from the corpus.
A draft that violates ANY of these is wrong.

### 1 · The 2-line declarative hook

Every post opens with a 2-line hook. Line 1 is a declaration. Line 2
starts with "Because" or is the consequence of line 1.

Examples from corpus:
- "I stopped using a design tool for every small edit. / Because you can fix them directly inside ChatGPT."
- "If you're using AI to post more / Then, you're slowly destroying your brand on LinkedIn."
- "I can spot AI content instantly. / Because it repeats words people never actually say."
- "LinkedIn is the worst place for fresh content ideas. / Because it only shows what already worked yesterday."
- "I audited 500+ founder brands on LinkedIn. / And 97% are losing calls because of 3 messaging mistakes."

**Generation rule:** every variant the engine produces must follow this pattern.
Line 1 = declarative statement (8-14 words ideal).
Line 2 = "Because [reason]" OR "Then, [consequence]" OR "And [stat/payoff]".

### 2 · The "Yeah." conversational opener

Line 3 (after the 2-line hook) is often "Yeah." followed by a
conversational connector.

Examples:
- "Yeah. This is something interesting I've been experimenting with."
- "Yeah. So I've been feeling this a lot lately."
- "Yeah. So let me share how I actually find content ideas."

**Generation rule:** ~70% of educational posts open the third line with
"Yeah." or "So." Use this OR the equivalent ("So, I spent 120 hours...").

### 3 · The "The thing is," transition

Used to pivot from setup to insight. Appears in 4 of 5 posts. Always at
roughly the 30-40% mark of the post.

Examples:
- "The thing is, when AI starts from templates, it copies the same patterns."
- "The thing is, the problem isn't AI. It's how people are using it."
- "The thing is, I don't want ideas. I want the problems of my clients."

**Generation rule:** every educational post needs ONE pivot transition.
Use "The thing is," OR "Here's what actually works." OR "Now here's the
important part."

### 4 · The directive pivot · "Here's what I want you to do."

After diagnosing the problem, the post pivots to instruction with one
of these phrases:

- "Here's what I want you to do." (Post 1)
- "Here's what actually works." (Post 3)
- "Now here's the important part." (Post 4)

**Generation rule:** every how-to post must use ONE of these phrases
at the 50-60% mark · marks the transition from "what's wrong" to "what to do."

### 5 · One-sentence paragraphs dominate

Average paragraph length across the corpus: **1.4 sentences.** Many
paragraphs are exactly 1 line. Some are 1 word ("Yeah." "Too easy." "That's it.").

**Generation rule:** never write a paragraph longer than 3 lines.
Average paragraph should be 1-2 lines. At least 4 paragraphs per post
should be exactly 1 line.

### 6 · Triple-fragment landings

Daniel uses 3-stop sentence patterns to hit a point:

- "No switching tabs. No back-and-forth with a designer for small fixes. No opening three tools just to change one thing."
- "Same words, rhythm, and same fake confidence."
- "I first read. I copied the post. Then I read the comments."
- "Like this post / Comment 'DM' / Connect with me"

**Generation rule:** every post should have ONE triple-fragment block ·
either parallel-structure ("No X. No Y. No Z.") or anaphoric ("I X. I Y. Then I Z.").

### 7 · Specific numbers · always

Daniel never says "many" or "a lot." He says:
- "120 hours"
- "97%"
- "500+ founders"
- "5-10 calls monthly"
- "below 40%"
- "in less than 60 seconds"
- "for the next 24 hours"
- "Ten times. Today."

**Generation rule:** every post must contain at least 2 specific numbers.
Round numbers are fine. Specific is mandatory. Never use "many", "lots",
"a few", "several."

### 8 · Tool names by exact name

Daniel never says "an AI chatbot." He says:
- "ChatGPT"
- "Adobe Photoshop"
- "Apps & Connectors"
- "@AdobePhotoshop"
- "Claude"
- "Grok"
- "Reddit"

**Generation rule:** when mentioning tools, always use the exact brand
name. Capitalize correctly. Never use generic terms like "AI tool" when
a specific name applies.

### 9 · The short landing sentence

Single-line sentences for emphasis at key moments:

- "Too easy."
- "But the thinking?"
- "That's already done."
- "That's the difference."
- "That's where it breaks."
- "That's it."

**Generation rule:** every post should have AT LEAST ONE 3-word-or-less
landing sentence. These are NOT filler · they're rhythm controls.

### 10 · PS as direct question

Every post ends with "PS:" (or "P.S.") followed by ONE direct question
to the reader.

Examples:
- "P.S. Have you tried this Photoshop + ChatGPT workflow yet?"
- "PS: If this feels familiar, it's probably because you've been letting AI lead instead of following:)"
- "PS: What part of your writing are you outsourcing to AI right now?"
- "PS: Where are you looking for ideas instead of listening to problems?"

**Generation rule:** every post ends with "PS:" + 1 question. Question
must be specific (asks about THEIR experience, not abstract). One smiley
allowed (`:)` only) but optional.

## BANNED PHRASES (extracted from corpus by absence)

These words/phrases NEVER appear in Daniel's posts. The engine must
treat them as forbidden:

- `unlock` · `delve` · `supercharge` · `seamless` · `elevate` · `leverage` (as verb)
- `it's not just X, it's Y`
- `game-changer` · `revolutionary` · `cutting-edge`
- `harness` · `embark` · `navigate` · `robust` · `comprehensive`
- `tapestry` · `intricate` · `vibrant`
- Em-dashes (—) · use period or comma instead
- "In today's fast-paced world" / "In the age of AI"
- "It's important to note" / "It's worth mentioning"
- Any 4-syllable Latinate word where a Germanic word would do

## STRUCTURAL TEMPLATE FOR EVERY POST

```
[Line 1 · declarative hook · 8-14 words]
[Line 2 · because/then/and · the consequence]

[Line 3 · "Yeah." or "So," + conversational lead-in]

[Setup · 2-3 paragraphs · 1-2 lines each · sets up the problem]

[Pivot · "The thing is," OR "Here's what actually works." OR "Now here's the important part."]

[Insight · 2-3 paragraphs · the answer · with specific tools/numbers]

[Triple-fragment block · 3 parallel sentences for emphasis]

[1-3 short landing sentences · "That's it." or "That's the difference."]

[PS: + ONE direct question to reader]
```
