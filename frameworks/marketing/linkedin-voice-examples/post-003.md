## RAW POST · paste exactly as written

I can spot AI content instantly.
Because it repeats words people never actually say.

Yeah. So I've been feeling this a lot lately.

AI made content easy. Too easy.
And because of that, everything started sounding the same.

Same words, rhythm, and same fake confidence.
You scroll LinkedIn, and it's like… okay, I've read this already. Ten times. Today.

The thing is, the problem isn't AI.
It's how people are using it.

And most people here on LinkedIn open a tool and ask it to write from scratch.

That's where it breaks.

Because AI doesn't know about you and how you explain things.
It doesn't know what you repeat when you're thinking out loud.

So it fills the gap with generic language.
And that's where this whole AI fatigue comes from.

Here's what actually works.

You talk first.
You dump your raw and unfiltered thoughts.

Then you use AI to refine them.

I use AI like my second brain.
It helps me refine my drafts, tighten my tone of voice, and fix flow.

But the thinking?
That's already done.

If you let AI think, your content sounds like everyone else.

But if you let AI help you edit, it still sounds like you.

That's the difference.

So yeah, use AI. Please do.
Just don't ask it to be you.

PS: What part of your writing are you outsourcing to AI right now?
