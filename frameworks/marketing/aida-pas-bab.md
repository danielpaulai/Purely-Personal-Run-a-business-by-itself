# 3 Classic Copy Frameworks · AIDA · PAS · BAB

> Three structures that have sold trillions of dollars of products
> over 100 years. Use them when you don't know what structure to
> use. They're the canon for a reason.

---

## AIDA · Attention · Interest · Desire · Action

The oldest. From the 1898 advertising era. Still works because human
psychology hasn't changed.

```
A · ATTENTION   · grab them. Stop the scroll.
I · INTEREST    · make them care. Tell them why.
D · DESIRE      · make them want it. Paint the after.
A · ACTION      · tell them what to do next.
```

### Worked example · LinkedIn post

```
A · "Your inbox is the reason your business isn't growing."
                      (attention · contrarian + stake)

I · "Founders spend 4.7 hrs/day on email. That's 23 hrs/week.
     1,196 hrs/year. 30 working weeks lost. To inbox."
                      (interest · math forces them to face it)

D · "Imagine: 10 minutes of email in the morning · then closed.
     Claude triages 80%. Drafts 15%. You handle 5%. Day reclaims
     itself."
                      (desire · paint the after with sensory detail)

A · "Comment 'inbox' for the 3-prompt sequence I use every morning."
                      (action · one move, low effort)
```

### When to use AIDA
- Top of funnel content (LinkedIn posts, X threads, ads)
- Email subject lines + body
- Sales pages with linear logic flow
- Cold outreach (compress to 4 sentences)

## PAS · Problem · Agitate · Solution

The pain-amplifier. Most useful when your audience is in active pain
but hasn't named it. You name it. Make it sting. Then end the sting.

```
P · PROBLEM    · name what's wrong (specific, observable)
A · AGITATE    · twist the knife (why it matters · cost · feeling)
S · SOLUTION   · the relief (your offer · the path out)
```

### Worked example · email body

```
P · "Most coaches charge $5k. Make $40k. Burn out by month 9."

A · "Because every dollar requires a 60-min call. Every call
     drains energy. By Friday you're exhausted. Saturday you're
     fried. Sunday you panic about Monday. Repeat."

S · "I rebuilt my offer as a self-serve product. Same revenue.
     Zero calls. 4-day work week. Here's the playbook ↓"
```

### Agitation rules
Agitate the **internal problem** (the feeling), not the external
problem (the symptom). External agitation feels like nagging. Internal
agitation feels like recognition.

- External: "Your inbox is full" (symptom)
- Internal: "You feel guilty every time you open your laptop" (feeling)

The internal hits 10x harder.

### When to use PAS
- Pain-driven niches (B2B SaaS · coaching · health · finance)
- Sales emails
- Webinar hooks
- Mid-funnel content where the audience is aware of the problem

### Don't use PAS for:
- Joy-driven niches (entertainment · luxury · lifestyle) · feels heavy
- Audiences early in awareness · they don't yet feel the pain

## BAB · Before · After · Bridge

The transformation framework. Most useful when your audience CAN see
the better future but doesn't believe THEY can get there.

```
B · BEFORE   · the current state (specific, real, observable)
A · AFTER    · the desired state (sensory, painted, vivid)
B · BRIDGE   · the path between (your offer)
```

### Worked example · LinkedIn post

```
BEFORE:
"Last quarter I worked 80-hour weeks. Sundays I'd lie awake
planning Mondays. My business owned me."

AFTER:
"Today I close my laptop Friday at 5. The next week is already
written. Marketing for Monday. Leads for Tuesday. Newsletter
Wednesday. Pipeline review Sunday. I open Monday and approve."

BRIDGE:
"What changed: I built 6 weekly routines, all powered by Claude.
The same 6 routines I'm teaching at the workshop May 2-3.
Comment 'routines' for the link."
```

### When to use BAB
- Transformation-led businesses (coaching · fitness · finance · therapy)
- Case studies + customer stories
- Webinar opening
- Long-form sales pages
- "Why this works for you" justification sections

### The Bridge rule
The bridge MUST be specific. "Mindset shift" or "discipline" doesn't
work. Specific tools, specific decisions, specific 2pm-on-a-Wednesday
moments. The bridge is the proof that the transformation is
reproducible.

## How to choose · which framework when

| Your audience is... | Use this |
|---|---|
| Cold · scrolling · doesn't know they have a problem | **AIDA** |
| Aware · in pain · ready to be sold the relief | **PAS** |
| Aspirational · sees the dream but doesn't believe in the path | **BAB** |
| Skeptical · seen it all · needs proof | StoryBrand 7-part (see separate file) |

---

## Stack frameworks for long-form

For sales pages or 800-word newsletters, stack 2 frameworks back to
back:

- AIDA (above the fold) → BAB (mid-page case study) → PAS (objection handling) → AIDA again (final CTA)
- Hook + Story + Offer (overall) WHERE Story = BAB

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When the user picks a topic, the engine should auto-detect which
framework fits:

```
IF topic is pain-driven (inbox · burnout · leads stuck) → PAS
IF topic is transformation (rebuild · relaunch · turn around) → BAB
IF topic is awareness-stage (intro · explainer · "what is") → AIDA
```

For 3 LinkedIn variants:
- Variant A · AIDA
- Variant B · PAS
- Variant C · BAB

User picks the best one for the moment.

## The 4-line cold email (compressed PAS)

For cold DMs, compress PAS to 4 sentences:

```
1 · "I noticed [specific observation about them]."          (problem signal)
2 · "Most [their type] struggle with [specific pain]."       (agitate)
3 · "I help [their type] [specific outcome] without [hated way]."  (solution)
4 · "Open to [low-effort next step] this week?"             (action)
```

That's it. Under 60 words. Reply rate 20%+ when targeted.
