# Newsletter Structures · 4 Formats That Work

> Four newsletter structures with track records measured in millions of
> opens. Pick one. Stick to it for 12 weeks. Switch only when you have
> data on what works.

---

## 1 · The 3-2-1 Format

Most-used newsletter structure on the internet. Why it works: 3-2-1
is a cognitive shape. It's pre-chewed. The reader knows EXACTLY what
they're getting.

```
3 · IDEAS from me           ·  3 short thoughts (2-3 sentences each)
2 · QUOTES from others      ·  2 quotes that connect to the ideas
1 · QUESTION for the reader ·  1 question to provoke reflection
```

### Worked example

> **3 ideas from me · 2 quotes from others · 1 question for you.**
>
> **3 ideas:**
> 1. Most founders don't have a marketing problem. They have a writing problem.
> 2. The reason routines work isn't discipline · it's removing decisions.
> 3. Your AI is generic because you're generic to it. Fix the brain, fix the output.
>
> **2 quotes:**
> *"The system you build is the life you live."*
> *"You don't rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."*
>
> **1 question:**
> What's the ONE recurring decision you're going to remove this week?

### When to use 3-2-1
- Twice-weekly cadence (Tue + Fri works well)
- Audience that wants takeaways, not stories
- 400-700 words total
- Best for B2B, productivity, mindset

## 2 · The Curve

Single-narrative newsletter. One idea. Build it through three beats.

```
HOOK         · the open · curiosity loop or pattern interrupt
DEEP DIVE    · the body · the actual insight, with depth
TAKEAWAY     · the close · the one thing they should do
```

The "curve" is shape: low (hook) → high (insight) → low (call back to
the hook).

### Worked example

> **Subject: I 10x'd a founder's pipeline by removing 9 things**
>
> Last quarter I worked with a founder who was sending 50 cold DMs
> a day. Closing 1 a week.
>
> We made one change. He started sending 5 cold DMs a day. Closing
> 3 a week.
>
> 10x revenue. 90% less effort. (...curve down here · this is the hook)
>
> Here's what we changed: ... [800 words of mechanism]
>
> The lesson isn't "send fewer DMs." The lesson is: most pipeline
> problems are qualification problems. We removed 9 unqualified
> leads to find the 1 right one. The math will surprise you.
>
> **The takeaway:** look at your last 50 DMs. Cross out the 45 that
> were never going to close. The 5 left? That's your real pipeline.

### When to use The Curve
- Story-driven newsletters
- Once-weekly cadence (Sunday morning works best)
- 800-1500 words
- Best for thought-leadership positioning

## 3 · The Open Loop

Built around a single unanswered question that pulls the reader to the
next issue. Used by master copywriters for 80 years.

```
LAST WEEK CALLBACK     ·  resolve last issue's open loop
THIS WEEK'S BIG IDEA   ·  the meat
NEXT WEEK'S TEASER     ·  open the next loop
```

### Worked example

> Last week I told you I'd reveal what changed when I stopped doing
> sales calls. Here's the answer: I stopped trying to close on the
> call. I started qualifying ON the call · then closing async by
> email. Close rate went from 18% to 41%.
>
> The mechanism: [600 words]
>
> Next week · I'll show you the exact 4-question script I use to
> qualify in 7 minutes. (Hint: only 1 of the 4 questions is about
> their problem.) See you Sunday.

### When to use The Open Loop
- Established list with high open rates (you've earned the right to tease)
- Once-weekly cadence
- 600-1000 words
- Builds long-term retention · readers know to expect the resolution

## 4 · The 5-Bullet Friday

Curation format. Lower effort. Higher consistency. Built around 5
shareable items.

```
BULLET 1 · Something I learned this week
BULLET 2 · Something I'm building / shipping
BULLET 3 · Something worth reading / watching / listening
BULLET 4 · Something I changed my mind about
BULLET 5 · Something to ask yourself
```

### Worked example

> **5-Bullet Friday · Workshop edition**
>
> 1. **Learned:** Most "marketing problems" are positioning problems
>    in disguise. If your audience can't repeat your offer back to
>    you in their words, you don't have a marketing problem.
>
> 2. **Building:** A workshop installer that uses Claude Code to do
>    the entire setup automatically. Watch a 90-second demo: [link]
>
> 3. **Reading:** "$100M Offers" - if you've never read it, do.
>    The Value Equation chapter alone is worth the book.
>
> 4. **Changed my mind:** Used to think "niche down." Now I think
>    "niche up · pick the most expensive customer in the niche
>    you're already in."
>
> 5. **Ask yourself:** What's the ONE recurring decision you make
>    every week that you could systemize this Friday?

### When to use 5-Bullet
- Audience that wants curation, not deep takes
- Weekly cadence (Friday is canonical · also "Tuesday Tactics", "Saturday Stack")
- 300-500 words · skim-friendly
- Best for personal-brand newsletters

## Newsletter rules (for ALL formats)

1. **One idea per issue.** Newsletters that try to say 3 things say 0
   things. Pick one. Cut the rest.
2. **The subject line is the issue.** 50% of opens come from subject.
   Spend 30% of your time on it.
3. **First line is the second hook.** Email previews show ~80 chars.
   Use them.
4. **No corporate voice.** Sound like you. Imperfect grammar fine.
   Stilted sentences not fine.
5. **One CTA per issue.** Comment trigger. DM bait. Reply request.
   Link click. Pick ONE per issue.
6. **Send on the same day, same time, every week.** Trains the reader.
7. **150-character preheader.** This is what shows up under the
   subject in inbox previews. Don't waste it.

## Subject line frameworks

Top performers across millions of sends:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Curiosity gap** | "The 1 thing I changed that 10x'd my pipeline" |
| **Question** | "Are you charging too little for your time?" |
| **Number** | "5 mistakes that kill a workshop launch" |
| **Personal** | "I lost $40k last quarter. Here's what I learned." |
| **Promise** | "How to clear your inbox in 9 minutes" |
| **Provocation** | "Stop building 'high-ticket offers'" |
| **Insider** | "What I told my coaching client yesterday" |

**Subject rules:**
- Under 50 characters when possible
- No clickbait. Deliver what you promise.
- Test 3 subjects per issue if you have list size to A/B

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When the engine generates a newsletter (Wednesday routine), it must:

1. Pick the format from the user's preference (default 3-2-1)
2. Generate 3 subject line variants (different patterns from above)
3. Write the full newsletter following the picked structure
4. Generate one preheader (under 150 chars)
5. Generate ONE CTA · at the end · low effort
6. Save to Drive as `/newsletters/YYYY-Www.md`
7. Save to Gmail Drafts ready to send

The engine should ASK once: "Format preference: 3-2-1 / curve / open
loop / 5-bullet?" Then save preference to BUSINESS-BRAIN.md so it
defaults next time.
