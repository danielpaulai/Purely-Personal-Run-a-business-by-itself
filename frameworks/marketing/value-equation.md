# The Value Equation

> The single math underneath every offer that converts. Use it to grade
> any post, ad, headline, or pitch — and to design new offers that
> close themselves.

---

## The equation

```
              Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
   Value =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

Four levers. Two on top, two on the bottom. Move any one of them and
the perceived value shifts.

---

## The 4 levers

### 1 · Dream Outcome (numerator · maximize)

The end state your customer secretly wants. Not the feature. Not the
process. The **after**.

- Bad: "We help you build a business."
- Good: "By Sunday at 5pm you have a business that runs without you."

Make the dream **specific, measurable, and visualizable.** Numbers,
deadlines, sensory detail.

### 2 · Perceived Likelihood (numerator · maximize)

Their belief that THEY personally will get it. Drop this lever if you
oversell — confidence collapses.

Levers that raise likelihood:
- **Social proof** · 500 founders shipped this last quarter
- **Specifics** · the exact process · with names, screenshots, numbers
- **Reverse-engineered logic** · "here's why it works for everyone"
- **Risk reversal** · 30-day money back · we eat the risk
- **Step-by-step roadmap** · they see the path, not the destination

### 3 · Time Delay (denominator · minimize)

How long until the dream lands. Compress this aggressively.

- Bad: "Build your business over the next year"
- Good: "By Sunday at 5pm" / "First win in 7 days" / "Setup in one weekend"

Even better: **deliver value DURING the sale.** A free audit. A
preview. A "you'll know inside 60 seconds." That's why this workshop
opens with a live install.

### 4 · Effort & Sacrifice (denominator · minimize)

What they have to GIVE UP. Not just the price. The cognitive load. The
learning curve. The lifestyle change. The relationships they have to
manage. The ego cost of asking for help.

- Bad: "Take this 12-week course"
- Good: "Watch one video · paste one prompt · we'll do the rest"

Done-for-you > Done-with-you > Done-by-you. Always.

## Apply it · grading template

For any draft (post, email, sales call):

```
Dream Outcome:        ___ / 10  (specific? measurable? visualized?)
Likelihood:           ___ / 10  (proof? roadmap? risk reversal?)
Time delay:           ___ / 10  (how fast can they see value?)
Effort & sacrifice:   ___ / 10  (how little do they have to do?)

Value score:          (DO × Like) / (TD × ES)
```

If any single lever is below 6, the offer is broken. Fix that lever
before fixing copy, design, or price.

## Apply it to /marketing-engine

When drafting LinkedIn posts:
- **Hook** carries Dream Outcome + Time Delay (specific, fast)
- **Body** carries Likelihood (proof, story, mechanism)
- **CTA** carries low Effort (one click, one reply, one DM)

Example post structure:

```
HOOK    · "I 10x'd a client's pipeline in 7 days."         (DO + TD)
BODY    · "Here's the exact 3-step process · with numbers."(Like)
CTA     · "Reply 'pipeline' and I'll send the playbook."   (Low ES)
```

---

## Apply it to offers

Stack improvements on each lever instead of cutting price:

| Lever | Bad upgrade | Good upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| Dream | Vague: "more revenue" | Specific: "30 BANT-qualified leads in 30 days" |
| Likelihood | Single testimonial | Case studies × 5 + roadmap + 30-day refund |
| Time delay | "8-week course" | "Day 1 you ship · 7 days first result" |
| Effort | "Self-paced lessons" | "Done with you · live setup call · I install it" |

A higher-priced offer with better levers ALWAYS outperforms a cheaper
offer with worse levers. Never compete on price.
