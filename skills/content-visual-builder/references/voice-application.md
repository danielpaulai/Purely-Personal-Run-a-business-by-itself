# Voice Application — Content Visual Builder

How to apply `BUSINESS-BRAIN.md` voice rules to each of the 4 platforms. Each platform has different constraints, but the voice must stay consistent.

---

## The Universal Voice Rules

Apply these to every platform, always. Pulled from the Brain's Voice section.

### 1. Short sentences
Average ≤18 words per sentence. Break compound sentences with periods, not commas.

**Before (AI):** "I built my entire content team using Claude, which has been incredibly transformative for my workflow and has saved me countless hours each week that I used to spend on content creation."

**After (voice):** "I built my entire content team using Claude. And it costs me zero dollars a month."

### 2. One idea per paragraph
Each paragraph is one sentence or one tight cluster. Not a wall of prose.

### 3. Specifics beat generalities
"12 hours a month" beats "many hours". "$75K" beats "a significant amount".

### 4. No hedging
Cut "I think", "maybe", "perhaps" unless it's an opinion hook (`"I think no one is saying this..."`).

### 5. Start with "And" or "But" when it lands
These aren't grammar errors in voice-driven writing. They're pacing tools.

### 6. P.S. ends every LinkedIn post with a question
Non-negotiable for LinkedIn. Optional elsewhere.

### 7. Never include banned phrases
List from `BUSINESS-BRAIN.md` voice section. Default: em-dash, unlock, delve, supercharge, game-changer, seamless, elevate, leverage.

## Platform-Specific Application

### LinkedIn

**Length:** 150–350 words. Use line breaks liberally. LinkedIn rewards readability.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — 1-2 lines, strong opener]

[Context — 2-4 lines, problem or scene]

[Pivot line — e.g. "But let me tell you something first."]

[Body — list or narrative, 3-7 short paragraphs]

[CTA — comment bait or DM trigger]

[P.S. — one question]
```

**Hook patterns for LinkedIn:**
- Personal proof: `"I built [X] using [Y]. And it cost me zero dollars."`
- Quiet truth: `"I think no one is saying this out loud."`
- Stat contrast: `"That is [N hours] [every X]. [Consequence]."`

**P.S. formulas:**
- `P.S. What is one thing about you that no AI could ever replicate?`
- `P.S. Are you still [doing X] without a system behind it?`
- `P.S. What's the first engine you'll [run unattended] this week?`

**Emoji rule:** sparingly, in lists (1/, 2/, 3/), never in hooks.

---

### X (Twitter)

**Length:** 280 chars per tweet. Thread of 5–8 tweets for anything meaty.

**Structure (thread):**
```
Tweet 1: Hook + promise. "Here's how I did it 👇"
Tweet 2: Context/problem.
Tweet 3-6: Numbered points or steps.
Tweet 7: Outcome.
Tweet 8: CTA + link or quote-tweet of tweet 1.
```

**Voice compression:**
- Drop articles ("the", "a") where possible without losing meaning
- Use line breaks within a tweet for scannability
- Numbers in digits, not words ("5" not "five")
- One emoji per tweet max, end-of-line

**Hook patterns for X:**
- `Most [X] get [Y] wrong.\nHere's what actually works:`
- `I used to [bad pattern].\nNow I [new pattern].\nResult: [specific].`
- `Hot take: [contrarian claim]`

---

### Instagram

**Length:** Caption 100–500 words. Aesthetic drives discovery.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook line — often a question or quote]
⁣
[Body — short paragraphs with blank lines between]
⁣
[CTA — save this, share, comment]
⁣
[Hashtags — 5-15, mixed size]
```

Note the `⁣` character (invisible separator) — Instagram collapses standard blank lines. Use `⁣` to preserve spacing.

**Voice adjustments:**
- Slightly more informal
- Emoji OK in line (1 per paragraph max)
- Always save-worthy or shareable concept
- First 125 chars are the "preview" — hook hard

**Hook patterns for Instagram:**
- `This changed everything for me:`
- `Save this for [specific use case]:`
- `[Contrarian statement]. Here's why:`

---

### Newsletter

**Length:** 400–1,200 words. Editorial pace. Reader paid attention — give them something.

**Structure:**
```
[Subject line — curiosity gap, <50 chars]
[Preview text — extends the hook, 90 chars]

Hey [first name],

[Opening anecdote or tension — 2-3 paragraphs]

[The actual lesson or framework — 3-6 paragraphs with subheadings]

[Action step — what they do with this]

[P.S. — personal note or question]
— Daniel
```

**Voice adjustments:**
- More narrative, less list-driven
- Subheadings OK (LinkedIn/X don't get them)
- First-person reflection is welcome
- Signs off as Daniel personally, not a brand

**Hook patterns for newsletter:**
- `Last Tuesday at 11pm, I was about to [do something]. Then [moment happened].`
- `I've been thinking about [topic] for 3 weeks. Here's what I finally figured out.`
- `[Reader name asked me] [question]. My answer surprised me.`

## Rewriting AI Draft → Voice Draft

If you draft copy that doesn't feel right, run this checklist:

1. **Is the opening in the Brain's hook pattern list?** If not, rewrite.
2. **Are any banned phrases present?** Remove them.
3. **Are sentences ≤18 words average?** Break compound sentences.
4. **Is there at least one specific number/name?** Add one from the Brain.
5. **Does the voice match an example opening from the Brain?** If it feels different, rewrite.

## Cross-Platform Consistency

When rendering 4-platform grid mode, the *core message* stays identical across all four. The *form* changes:

| Element | LinkedIn | X | Instagram | Newsletter |
|---------|----------|---|-----------|------------|
| Hook | Full sentence | Punchy | Question or quote | Scene |
| Body | Paragraph list | Thread | Short paragraphs | Narrative |
| CTA | Comment trigger | Quote-tweet trigger | Save/share | Reply trigger |
| Length | 300 words | 1,500 chars (thread) | 200 words | 800 words |
| Ending | P.S. question | Quote tweet of T1 | Hashtags | "— Daniel" |

Never change the core claim across platforms. Change the delivery.

## Voice Failure Modes (catch before shipping)

**1. AI residue** — the draft still has "unlock", "supercharge", em-dashes, or "it's not just X, it's Y". Reject, rewrite.

**2. Brand voice instead of personal voice** — sounds like a company is speaking, not a person. Reject. Voice-driven content is always first-person singular.

**3. Over-polished** — every sentence perfectly balanced, no rhythm variation. Reject. Danny's voice has intentional roughness (sentence fragments, "Yeah," openings).

**4. Missing specifics** — "I saved a lot of time" instead of "I went from 12 hours to 2 hours". Reject, add the number.

**5. Weak P.S.** — ends on a statement or platitude instead of a question. Reject, rewrite as a question.

**6. Wrong hook for platform** — long narrative hook on X, single-line hook on newsletter. Reject, rewrite for platform.

## Canonical Voice Examples (reference set)

These are the gold-standard voice benchmarks. When drafting, compare against these. If your draft doesn't feel like it could sit next to these, rewrite.

### LinkedIn example

> And it costs me zero dollars a month.
>
> But let me tell you something first.

> And when you go quiet, your pipeline dries up.

>

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Never write a 4-platform render without first drafting the LinkedIn version — it's the longest and forces voice discipline.
- ❌ Never re-use the exact same opening across platforms. Adjust the hook to the platform.
- ❌ Never end a LinkedIn post without a P.S. question.
- ❌ Never use hashtags on LinkedIn, X, or Newsletter. Instagram only.
- ❌ Never include banned phrases even if they'd fit grammatically. They're poison.
