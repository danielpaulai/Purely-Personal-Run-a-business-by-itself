01 · Voice

How Daniel Paul Writes

Direct, conversational, confident. Short sentences. One idea per line. No hedging, no hype, no corporate polish.

Banned phrases
em-dash — unlock delve supercharge game-changer seamless elevate leverage
Voice rules
  • Write like you talk. Conversational beats polished.
  • One idea per sentence. No compound complexity.
  • Start sentences with "And" or "But" when it lands right.
  • Use specifics. "12 hours a month" beats "many hours".
  • P.S. ends every post with a question.
// SIGNATURE
avg sentence: 11 words
short/long ratio: 4:1
hook rate: 100% first line
Hook patterns
  1. Personal proof: "I built [X] using [Y]. And it cost me zero dollars."
  2. Quiet truth: "I think no one is saying this out loud."
  3. Pivot: "But let me tell you something first."
  4. Stat contrast: "That is [N hours] [every X]. [Consequence]."
  5. Contrarian: "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong because [reason]."
Example openings
"I built my entire content team using Claude. And it costs me zero dollars a month."
"I think no one is saying this out loud. In this AI hype, information has become cheap."
"So that is why I built these 5 skills that handle my whole content marketing."
"I went from 12 hours a week on content to two hours max."
